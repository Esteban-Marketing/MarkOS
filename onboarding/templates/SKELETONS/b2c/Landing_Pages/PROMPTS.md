# B2C — Landing Pages Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2C landing pages convert through visual-forward layouts, social proof at the point of decision, and frictionless paths to purchase. High-performing B2C pages lead with the lifestyle outcome, build trust with peer proof, and offer a single clear next step. Prompts here cover product pages, brand collection pages, and promotional landing pages.

## Prompts

### 1. Hero Product Landing Page — DTC Conversion

Write a full product landing page for a [D2C consumer product, e.g., "premium hair care serum"] targeting [buyer persona, e.g., "women 25–45 who color their hair and have damage concerns"].

Page structure:
- **Above fold**:
  - Product image (art direction: lifestyle context, not white background)
  - Headline (10–14 words): Benefit-led, outcome-first — "The serum that repairs damage without weighing your hair down."
  - Subheadline (1 sentence): Mechanism or differentiator
  - Review star rating + count ("4.8 | 2,400+ reviews")
  - Price + CTA button: "Add to bag" or "Try it now"
  - Trust line: "Free shipping over $35 · 30-day returns · No questions asked"

- **Below fold**:
  - **Benefit stack (3 points)**: Icon + benefit label + 1-sentence explanation. Benefits, not features.
  - **How to use (3 steps)**: Visual walkthrough — what the routine looks like.
  - **Ingredients / Key proof**: 3–4 ingredient callouts with consumer-friendly explanations (not INCI names — what the ingredient does in plain language)
  - **Review section**: 3 featured reviews — each with star rating, reviewer name/type (e.g., "Verified buyer, color-treated hair"), and specific outcome quote
  - **FAQ (4 questions)**: Answer the top 4 purchase hesitations for this product type
  - **Secondary CTA**: Repeat CTA + trust signals

Tone: Consumer-warm, benefit-fluent, brand-voice consistent.

### 2. Collection Page — New Arrivals or Seasonal Promo

Write a promotional landing page for a B2C brand's seasonal collection or new arrival launch.

Context: [Brand name], [product category, e.g., "summer skincare"], [target audience].

Page structure:
- **Hero section**:
  - Full-width lifestyle image (art direction: seasonal context, no product on white)
  - Headline: Evocative, season or occasion-relevant — not "New Arrivals" as the headline
  - Subheadline: The angle or story of this collection (why now, why this, for whom)
  - Optional countdown (only if there is a real deadline — launch date, limited run, etc.)

- **Collection grid**:
  - 6–12 product cards with: product image, name, short benefit label (not just category), price, and "Best for" tag (e.g., "Best for dry skin," "Fan favorite")

- **Editorial story section** (100–150 words): Why this collection was created — the specific context, season, or intention behind the product selection. Not a generic "Now featuring…" block.

- **Social proof**: 3 UGC-style photos with short quotes from buyers using products from the collection.

- **CTA section**: Shop the full collection + email signup ("Be first to know about new releases").

Tone: Visually editorial, brand-voice consistent. This page competes with editorial content, not catalog listings.

### 3. Brand Welcome / First Purchase Landing Page

Write a landing page for new visitors who clicked a brand awareness ad and are encountering the brand for the first time. Goal: convert an unfamiliar visitor into a first-time buyer.

Context: [Brand name], [product category], [primary brand differentiator], [first-time offer if any].

Page structure:
- **Hook (above fold)**: A problem the target audience knows they have — stated in their language, not the brand's marketing language. No brand name in the first line.
- **Brand introduction (100 words)**: Why this brand was built — specific, personal, not a generic mission statement. Photo of founder or team optional but effective.
- **3 reasons people choose [Brand]**: Consumer-benefit framed, not feature-list. Each paired with a short proof element (review excerpt, stat, or visual).
- **Social proof wall**: 4–6 review excerpts, each with a specific outcome quote, not a generic "love it!"
- **First purchase offer** (if applicable): Disclosed clearly, no dark patterns, easy to redeem.
- **Primary CTA**: One action — "Start with [popular product]" or "Explore the collection."

Tone: Earned trust, low pressure, brand-warm. A visitor should feel like they've found something worth staying for.

### 4. Thank You / Post-Purchase Confirmation Page

Write a post-purchase confirmation page that builds brand loyalty at the moment of maximum buyer openness.

Requirements (this is a webpage, not an email):
- **Opening**: Confirm the order (order number, "on its way" messaging) — utilitarian but warm
- **Brand moment (100 words)**: One sentence or short copy block that reinforces the buyer's decision — why the product they just bought matters, what it will do for them. Not a feature list. A brief "you're going to love this because…" statement.
- **Community invitation**: Option to join brand community, follow on social, or submit a review after they receive the product — framed as a genuine invitation, not a task list
- **Cross-sell (optional)**: ONE product recommendation — the most natural complement to what was ordered. Not a grid of "you might also like." One product, brief reason why it pairs well.
- **Referral or share nudge**: Frictionless social share or referral offer — "Tell a friend, get [reward]"

Tone: Celebratory, brand-warm, peer-invited. The buyer just made a decision — make them feel great about it.
