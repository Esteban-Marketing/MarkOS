# B2C — Paid Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2C paid media is dominated by Meta (Instagram/Facebook), TikTok, YouTube, and Google Shopping. Creative quality and scroll-stopping authenticity drive performance — UGC-style content outperforms polished brand creative in most consumer categories. Prompts here focus on creative briefs for performance-oriented consumer ads across these channels.

## Prompts

### 1. Meta Video Ad Brief — UGC-Style (Awareness to Consideration)

Write a creative brief for a 15–30 second UGC-style Meta video ad for a [D2C consumer product, e.g., "a reusable water bottle brand"] targeting [audience, e.g., "women 25–40 interested in sustainability and wellness"].

Brief structure:
- **Hook (first 3 seconds)**: What the viewer sees and hears in the first 3 seconds — must stop the scroll. Use a real-life scenario, problem reveal, or genuine "before" moment. No logo. No product on a white background.
- **Core of the video (8–15 seconds)**: Product in use in a natural context — show the benefit, not the feature. What does the person do differently with this product in their daily life?
- **Social proof moment (3–5 seconds)**: UGC-feel testimonial line OR a visually displayed review OR a comparison ("used to carry plastic — now I don't").
- **Close + CTA (3–5 seconds)**: Product name + simple CTA ("Shop now" or "Find yours at [URL]").
- **Casting direction**: Describe the creator persona (authentic, relatable to target audience — not influencer-polished).
- **Tone**: Genuine, lifestyle-authentic, not an ad.

### 2. TikTok Ad Brief — Problem-Solution Hook

Write a creative brief for a 20–45 second TikTok ad for a [consumer product/service] targeting [age/interest segment].

Brief structure:
- **Hook (first 2 seconds)**: The most direct, relatable articulation of the problem the product solves. Start mid-thought — no intro, no branding.
- **Agitation (5–10 seconds)**: Show or describe the problem getting worse or more frustrating before the solution arrives.
- **Solution reveal (5–10 seconds)**: Product reveal in a natural way — unboxing, first use, or "one week later" montage.
- **Proof element**: One specific data point, review bar rating, or before/after comparison.
- **Outro + CTA (3 seconds)**: Brand name + clear CTA. "Link in bio" or "Shop now."
- **Tone**: Native to TikTok — spoken to camera, energetic, honest, peer-to-peer.

### 3. Google Shopping Campaign — Product Feed Optimization Brief

Write a product feed and ad copy brief for a B2C brand optimizing Google Shopping listings for [product category, e.g., "skincare serums"].

Deliverables:
- **Product title formula**: [Brand] + [Product type] + [Key attribute] + [Skin concern/use case/size] — 70 chars max. Write 3 title variants for a hero product.
- **Product description** (150–200 words): Start with the consumer's skin concern or use case (not the ingredient list). Include the top 3 benefit statements before listing ingredients or specs. End with who this product is designed for.
- **Custom label strategy**: Suggest 3–5 custom label values for segmenting Shopping campaigns (e.g., "bestseller," "seasonal," "margin-high," "new-arrival").
- **Negative keyword list starter**: 10 negative keywords to exclude irrelevant searches for this category.

### 4. YouTube Pre-Roll Ad Script — 15-Second Unskippable

Write a 15-second unskippable YouTube pre-roll ad script for a [B2C brand] targeting [audience segment] watching [content type, e.g., "fitness and wellness content"].

Script structure:
- **Seconds 0–3**: Pattern interrupt or empathy hook — immediately relevant to what the viewer cares about. No brand intro.
- **Seconds 3–10**: Core message — what the product does and why it matters to this viewer. One clear benefit. No feature list.
- **Seconds 10–15**: Brand name + CTA. Make the CTA action-specific ("Try it free," "Get yours today," "See the full range at [brand].com").
- **Visual direction**: One scene suggestion per 5-second block.
- **Tone**: Confident, direct, consumer-resonant. This ad cannot be skipped — respect the viewer's time by being immediately relevant.
