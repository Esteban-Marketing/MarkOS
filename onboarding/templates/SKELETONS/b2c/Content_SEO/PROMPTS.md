# B2C — Content & SEO Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2C content SEO wins on lifestyle, educational, and purchase-intent queries. The best consumer content makes the reader feel understood before the brand appears — it solves a question or validates a purchase decision before pitching a product. Prompts here focus on content types that drive organic discovery and build brand affinity alongside ranking.

## Prompts

### 1. Lifestyle Buyer's Guide — Commercial Investigation Keyword

Write an SEO buyer's guide for a B2C brand targeting the keyword "[keyword, e.g., 'best running shoes for beginners']."

Structure:
- **H1**: Natural keyword integration in a benefit-led format ("The Beginner's Guide to Finding Running Shoes That Don't Hurt")
- **Intro (150 words)**: Empathy hook — speak to where the reader is in their search, validate their confusion, and preview what the guide covers
- **What to consider (300 words)**: 3–5 evaluation criteria that matter to this buyer persona — written from the buyer's perspective ("You want a shoe that… not 'Look for shoes that feature…'")
- **Product recommendations (2–3 options)**: Brief per-product section: who it's best for, top benefit, one potential downside (builds trust)
- **FAQ (3 questions)**: Common queries the buyer has from the same SERP (look for "People Also Ask" for the target keyword)
- **CTA**: Subtle link to the brand's most relevant product or collection

Tone: Helpful guide, not a product catalog. Write for the buyer who hasn't decided yet, not the one who's already convinced.

### 2. How-To Article — Consumer Problem Solving

Write a how-to article targeting the keyword "[informational keyword, e.g., 'how to remove red wine stains from carpet']."

Structure:
- **H1**: Exact match or semantic variant of the how-to keyword
- **Intro (80 words)**: Acknowledge the problem, validate the urgency ("The longer you wait, the harder it gets to remove"), and preview the outcome the article delivers
- **Materials/What you need** (if applicable): Short bulleted list
- **Step-by-step (5–8 steps)**: Numbered, each step in one sentence + one explanatory sentence. Practical and complete — a person following these steps should succeed.
- **Helpful tip box**: One insider tip or common mistake to avoid
- **CTA/product mention**: Natural integration of the brand's relevant product in one of the steps or in a "Pro version" tip — not forced

Tone: Practical, expert, peer-level. Sounds like advice from someone who has actually done this, not a brand FAQ page.

### 3. Collection Page Copy — Category SEO + Conversion

Write SEO-optimized collection page copy for a B2C brand's [product category, e.g., "organic face moisturizers"] collection.

Deliverables:
- **Above-fold description (120 words)**: Open with the buyer's skin type, concern, or goal — not the product spec. Include the primary category keyword naturally. End with what makes this collection distinct (brand POV, ingredient standard, or philosophy).
- **Filter/sort copy**: 3–4 filter options with short descriptive labels that match how buyers think (e.g., "Best for sensitive skin," "Under $40," "Editor's picks") — not just product attributes.
- **SEO footer text (100 words)**: Informational paragraph targeting long-tail keywords related to this category. Useful to the buyer — not keyword stuffing.

Tone: Consumer-aware, benefit-led, brand-voice consistent. The copy should make a buyer who landed on this page feel like they're in the right place.

### 4. Brand Story / Mission Content Page

Write a brand story page for a B2C company that converts visitors who care about "why" — not just "what."

Structure:
- **Opening (100 words)**: The origin story — what prompted the founder to start this brand? Be specific: a real problem, a specific moment, a gap in the market that felt personal.
- **The tension (80 words)**: What was wrong with what was available? What compromise were consumers forced to make?
- **The approach (100 words)**: How this brand decided to solve it differently — the specific choice, trade-off, or principle that drives product decisions.
- **Evidence of that choice (80 words)**: One product decision that proves the principle is real (ingredients, sourcing, manufacturing, pricing philosophy).
- **Invitation (60 words)**: Why this community of customers matters to the brand — without making it sound like a loyalty program pitch.

Tone: Personal, earned, founder-voiced. Avoid mission-statement genericness ("We believe everyone deserves…"). Make it specific to this brand's actual story.
