# B2C — Social Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2C social media is about brand resonance, community, and earned shareability. The best brand content feels peer-authored — it makes the audience feel seen, not sold to. UGC-feel content, relatable problem-solution moments, and community amplification consistently outperform polished brand posts on Meta, Instagram, and TikTok. Prompts here cover organic social content creation.

## Prompts

### 1. Instagram Carousel — Lifestyle Listicle + Product Integration

Write an Instagram carousel post for a [B2C brand] that provides genuine lifestyle value while naturally integrating the product.

Format — 5–7 slides:
- **Slide 1 (Hook)**: Bold text-forward visual with a curiosity-gap hook (e.g., "5 things you're probably doing wrong with [category]" or "The [lifestyle goal] trick nobody talks about"). No product on first slide.
- **Slides 2–5 (Content)**: One tip/idea per slide — each genuinely useful, short (under 15 words per slide), with supporting visual direction. Product appears naturally in 1–2 slides as part of the solution, not the subject.
- **Slide 6–7 (Wrap + CTA)**: Summary + "Save this for later" or "Tag someone who needs this" + brand logo.

Caption (for the carousel post):
- First line: Mirror the hook from Slide 1
- 3–5 sentences of supporting context
- CTA: Save, share, or comment with their experience
- 3–5 relevant hashtags

Tone: Helpful friend sharing a real tip, not a brand publishing educational content.

---

### 2. Reels / TikTok Script — Problem-Solution Consumer Content

Write a short-form video script (20–45 seconds) for a [B2C consumer product] Reels or TikTok post.

Format:
- **First 2 seconds (Hook text overlay + audio)**: State the relatable problem in the most direct, consumer-honest way. Start mid-thought — no intro, no "Today I'm going to…"
- **Problem show (5–10 seconds)**: Visual of the problem in context — real-life scenario, not staged.
- **Transition (1 second)**: Simple cut or reveal.
- **Solution show (10–15 seconds)**: Product in use, naturally, in the same context. Show the outcome — not the product spec.
- **Close (3–5 seconds)**: Simple brand logo fade + "Link in bio" or "Available now."

Creator direction: Describe the person on camera (not a model — an authentic user who looks like the target audience). Describe facial expression and energy at each stage.

Tone: Real, quick, fun. Feels like a friend showing you something they discovered, not an ad.

### 3. UGC Amplification Post — Customer Story Feature

Write a B2C social post that amplifies a real customer's story, review, or photo.

Requirements:
- Format: Instagram static image or carousel (1–3 slides)
- **Creative**: The customer's photo or quote is the hero — brand graphics are minimal and framing-only
- **Caption structure**:
  - Open with the customer's name and a one-sentence version of their story or outcome
  - 2–3 sentences of context: product used, what they were trying to do, what changed
  - Optional quote: 1 line directly from the customer's review or DM (with permission notation)
  - Close: CTA to share their own story in comments or tag the brand in their own post
- **Tone**: Celebratory, community-first. This is their moment — the brand is the curator, not the subject.

Permission language: "Shared with permission" note at end of caption.

---

### 4. Brand Story Short — Founder / Origin Moment

Write a short Instagram caption or TikTok script (150–250 words / 30–45 seconds) that communicates the brand's origin story in a way that feels personal and earned — not a marketing video.

Structure:
- **The moment**: A specific, personal observation that inspired the brand — one scene, named and concrete, not a broad category observation.
- **The frustration**: What was wrong with what existed — one broken thing the founder couldn't stop noticing.
- **The decision**: The moment they decided to build something differently — named choice, not generic "so we built a solution."
- **What it means now**: One sentence about what this story means for the products made today — how the origin still drives decisions.
- **Invitation**: Why the audience's choice to buy this brand is part of that story — without it sounding like a values pitch.

Tone: Personal, founder-voice, earned. This is not a mission statement. It's a story that happened to one person before it happened to thousands.
