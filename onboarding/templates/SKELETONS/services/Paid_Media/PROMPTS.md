# Services — Paid Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Paid media for services businesses plays a long game. The goal is not direct conversion — it's driving the right audience into a trust-building funnel. LinkedIn is the primary paid channel for most B2B services firms. Retargeting targets people who engaged with thought leadership content. Urgency and "act now" framing are strictly counter-indicated. Prompts here focus on LinkedIn thought leadership distribution, mid-funnel content ads, and retargeting for long sales cycles.

## Prompts

### 1. LinkedIn Thought Leadership Sponsored Content

Write a LinkedIn Sponsored Content thought leadership ad for a professional services firm.

Context: Practice area or expertise: [e.g., "financial operations consulting", "brand strategy", "supply chain optimization"]. ICP: [Job title(s), company size, industry]. Problem being addressed: [The specific business problem the ICP faces].

Ad format: Single image + copy or document carousel.

**Copy (150–200 words, written in first-person brand voice or practitioner voice):**
- Opening hook: A question, counterintuitive insight, or uncommon perspective on the ICP's problem.
- 2–3 sentences on why the conventional approach to this problem misses something important.
- Insight or framework summary: "Here's how we [characterize the right approach]."
- CTA: "We wrote a [short guide / case breakdown / teardown] on this — [read it here]." (Not "Book a call." Thought leadership earns a read, not a pitch.)

**Audience targeting note (for brief documentation, not ad copy):**
- Job titles: [specify]
- Company size: [specify]
- Industry: [specify]
- Optional: Exclude current clients in retargeting setup.

Tone: Authoritative but approachable. Shares a genuine perspective. Never uses words like "innovative," "cutting-edge," "world-class."

### 2. LinkedIn Retargeting Ad — Mid-Funnel Warm Lead

Write a LinkedIn retargeting ad targeting people who have interacted with the firm's organic content or visited the website (mid-funnel, considers-but-hasn't-converted).

Key objective: Move a consideration-stage prospect closer to a discovery conversation without pressure.

**Ad copy (120–150 words):**
- Acknowledge the implicit awareness: "If you've been thinking about [problem area], here's something specific from our work."
- Offer a case study, methodology guide, or industry-specific insight — not a sales pitch.
- One specific outcome metric or client context to establish proof: "We helped a [company type] in [industry] [outcome] in [timeframe]."
- CTA: "Read the case breakdown." or "See how we approached it." — discovery-oriented, not commitment-oriented.

**Alternative format — Document Carousel (if case study asset exists):**
- Card 1: "How a [company type] solved [problem]"
- Card 2–5: Case study key frames (situation → approach → outcome)
- Card 6: "Exploring something similar? Talk to our team." + link.

Tone: Peer-to-peer. Doesn't say "Book now." Says "Here's relevant information for what you're working on."

### 3. LinkedIn Lead Gen Form Ad — Discovery Call Offer

Write a LinkedIn Lead Gen Form ad for a professional services firm offering a free discovery call or consultation.

Context: Offer type: [Free 30-min strategy call / Free audit / Free assessment]. Scope: What the prospect gets from it. Qualification criteria: [e.g., "relevant for teams of [X] or above managing [scope]"].

**Ad headline (under 70 chars):** Describes the value of the call, not just its existence.
- Good: "How [industry] firms are reducing [problem metric] — discuss your situation."
- Bad: "Book a free consultation."

**Ad body copy (100–130 words):**
- Sentence 1: Who this is for (specific).
- Sentence 2: What the call covers / what the prospect will get from the 30 minutes.
- Sentence 3: Low-commitment framing. "No pitch. Just a look at your current situation and honest guidance on what would move the needle."
- CTA: "Request a call." (not "Get started" — that's a purchase signal, not a services signal)

**Form fields (Lead Gen Form):** First name, Last name, Company name, Job title, Company size. Keep under 6 fields — every additional field reduces form completion rate measurably.

**Qualifying question (optional, 1 field):** "What's your primary challenge with [area]?" — freeform or multiple choice.

Tone: Helpfulness signal, no pressure. The ad should make the prospect think "this might actually be useful for my specific situation."

### 4. Google Search Ad — Expertise / Category Intent

Write Google Search ad copy for a professional services firm targeting problem-specific or expertise-category search queries.

Keyword intent: [e.g., "[service type] consultant for [industry]", "how to [fix specific business problem]", "[practice area] agency"].

For 2 keyword themes, write RSA-formatted ad copy:

**Per theme:**

Responsive Search Ad headlines (5 headlines, 30 chars max each):
- 2 expertise-signaling: "[Practice area] specialists" / "Trusted by [N] [industry] firms"
- 2 outcome-signaling: "[Outcome you deliver]" / "Built for [company size/type]"
- 1 low-commitment CTA: "Free discovery call" / "Talk to a specialist"

Descriptions (2 descriptions, 90 chars max each):
- Description 1: Expertise claim + proof. "[Firm name] helps [company type] [achieve outcome]. [X] clients across [N] industries."
- Description 2: Process confidence + next step. "No-pitch intro call. We'll assess your situation and tell you what would genuinely help."

Tone: Authoritative, approachable, specific to the search query context. Never "best" superlatives without provable support.
