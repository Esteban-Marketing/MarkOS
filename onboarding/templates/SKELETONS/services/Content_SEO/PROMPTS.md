# Services — Content & SEO Starter Prompts

> **Context:** SEO content for services businesses earns trust from search-intent buyers who are researching solutions to specific problems. The priority is expertise demonstration, not keyword volume. Prompts here cover thought leadership articles, case study pages, methodology / how-we-work pages, and industry-specific landing pages designed to rank and convert consideration-stage research.

## Prompts

### 1. Thought Leadership Article — Problem Diagnosis / Expert Take

Write a thought leadership article for a professional services firm targeting a specific audience problem.

Context: Expertise domain: [practice area]. Article angle: [the specific problem, misconception, or underappreciated dynamic in the ICP's space]. Target reader: [Job title and context].

Article structure:

**H1 / SEO title (under 60 chars):** Problem-framed, search-intent-aware headline.
- Good: "Why [Industry] [Roles] Keep Underestimating [Problem]"
- Bad: "A Comprehensive Guide to [Broad Topic]"

**Introduction (150–200 words):**
- State the specific problem and who it affects.
- Name the conventional wisdom that's incomplete or wrong.
- What this article will reveal: the alternative framing or insight.

**Body (3–5 H2 sections, 200–250 words each):**
- Each section: A discrete component of the argument or insight.
- Include: A real pattern the firm has observed across engagements (anonymized if needed).
- Include: Specific implication for the reader — "what this means for [their role]."

**Closing (100–150 words):**
- The through-line of the argument, stated plainly.
- What the thoughtful reader should do differently.
- Soft CTA: "If you're working through this in your organization, we're happy to share how we've approached it — [link to contact or discovery call]."

Tone: Practitioner-peer voice. This article should read as if the lead practitioner wrote it from accumulated client experience, not as a content marketing piece.

### 2. Case Study Page — Specific Client Outcome

Write a case study page for a professional services firm documenting a specific client engagement and its outcomes.

Context: Client type: [industry, company size, anonymized or named]. Challenge: [Specific problem the client faced before the engagement]. Engagement scope: [What the firm was hired to do]. Outcomes: [Specific measurable results — quantified where possible].

Page structure:

**H1:** "[Client Type] reduces [metric] by [X]% with [methodology or service type]" — or equivalent outcome-first headline.

**At-a-glance summary (sidebar or intro card):**
- Client: [industry + company type]
- Engagement: [Service type, duration]
- Outcome: [Top 2–3 metrics]

**The challenge (150–200 words):**
- Situation faced before the engagement. Specific constraints and stakes.

**The approach (200–250 words):**
- Methodology applied, phases, what made the approach distinctive for this client's situation.
- Include: What was decided during the engagement (not just planned), and why.

**The outcome (100–150 words):**
- Quantified results. Client context for why these results matter.
- Direct quote from the client if available (specific to outcomes, not generic praise).

**What this demonstrates:**
- The broader principle or capability this case illustrates — relevant to prospects facing a similar challenge.

Tone: Factual, specific, confident without self-congratulation. The client's outcome is the subject; the firm's work is the mechanism.

### 3. Methodology / How We Work Page

Write a "How We Work" or methodology page for a professional services firm.

Context: Practice area: [e.g., "change management consulting", "brand strategy", "financial planning services"]. Engagement types: [What the firm typically scopes]. Key differentiator in approach: [What's genuinely different about how this firm works].

Page structure:

**H1:** "How we work" or "Our approach to [practice area]" — direct, not clever.

**Philosophy section (100–150 words):**
- The conviction that drives how this firm works.
- Not "we put clients first" (assumed). The specific principle that shapes how they approach the problem differently.

**Our process (3–5 phases, 75–100 words each):**
- Per phase: Name, what happens, what the client experiences during it, what it produces.
- Real specificity: "By the end of [phase], you will have [specific deliverable or clarity]."

**What you can expect from us (bullet list, 4–6 points):**
- Communication cadence, decision involvement, what "project completion" means, what happens if scope shifts.

**FAQ (3 questions relevant to how-we-work concerns):**
- "How long does a typical engagement take?"
- "How involved will our team need to be?"
- "What happens if our needs change mid-engagement?"

**CTA:** "Curious if this is the right fit? [Let's talk]." — Not "Ready to get started?"

Tone: Transparent, specific, designed to help buyers self-qualify. A prospect who reads this page should know if this firm is for them before they ever pick up the phone.

### 4. Industry-Specific Service Landing Page — SEO

Write an industry-specific landing page for a professional services firm targeting a specific vertical.

Context: Service: [service type]. Industry/vertical: [specific industry]. Target role: [ICP job title]. Primary challenge in this industry the firm solves: [be specific].

Page structure:

**H1 (under 60 chars):** "[Service type] for [industry]" — direct, keyword-complete.

**Opening section (100–150 words):**
- Name the specific dynamic in this industry that makes [problem] hard.
- "Most [industry] firms we talk to describe it this way: [pain point framed from the buyer's vocabulary]."
- Transition: "Here's how we work with [industry] teams."

**What we bring to [industry] (3 bullets):**
- Each: Specific expertise, pattern observed across engagements in this industry, resulting capability.

**Case study snippet (1 client story, 75–100 words):**
- "[Company type] in [industry context] came to us with [challenge]. [How we approached it]. [Outcome metric]."

**Industries-specific FAQ (3 questions):**
- Built from real objections or uncertainties specific to this industry's buyers.

**CTA:** "Schedule a 30-minute call to discuss your situation." — Industry-specific CTA is more credible than generic "Contact us."

Tone: Industry-informed. The page should feel like it was written by someone who has spent time working in or alongside this industry — not by a generalist using the industry's name as a keyword.
