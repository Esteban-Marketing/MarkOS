# Services — Landing Pages Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Landing pages for professional services must build trust, not drive a transaction. The primary conversion is a low-commitment action: a discovery call, a free audit, or a content download. Process transparency, case study proof, and specificity about who the firm serves best are the highest-trust signals. Prompts here cover service pages, case study pages, consultation offer pages, and expertise/homepage sections.

## Prompts

### 1. Core Service Page — Trust-Led Structure

Write a core service page for a professional services firm.

Context: Service type: [e.g., "brand strategy", "operations consulting", "executive coaching"]. Target client: [ICP — company size, industry, role]. Primary outcome delivered: [Specific business result]. Engagement format: [Retainer / project-based / workshop / ongoing advisory].

Page structure:

**H1 (under 60 chars):** Outcome-first service description. Example: "Brand strategy for consumer companies scaling to $50M+"

**Opening paragraph (75–100 words):**
- The client situation this service is designed for. Not a description of the service — a description of the client's problem.
- "If you're [situation], this is the engagement we designed for you."

**How we approach it (3 phases or principles, 75 words each):**
- Phase name + what happens + what the client produces/receives.
- Real specificity: "By the end of [phase], you will have [deliverable or decision]."

**Who this is right for (and who it isn't — optional but powerful):**
- "This engagement works best for [specific criteria]. It's not the right fit for [disqualifying criteria]." Explicit scope helps serious buyers self-select.

**Client results section:** 2–3 outcome snippets (50–75 words each), with industry context and metric.

**CTA:** "Explore if this is the right fit — schedule a 30-minute discovery call." (Not "Buy now" or "Get started.")

Tone: Specific and transparent. This page should tell a serious buyer exactly what they're signing up for.

### 2. Free Consultation / Audit Offer Page

Write a landing page for a professional services firm offering a free consultation, assessment, or audit.

Context: Offer: [Free 30-min strategy call / Free marketing audit / Free financial review]. What the prospect gets: [Specific value: analysis, custom recommendations, benchmark report]. Who it's for: [ICP description].

Page structure:

**H1 (under 60 chars):** Describes what the prospect gets, not the format. "Get an honest assessment of your [practice area] situation."

**What you get from this call / audit (3–4 bullet points):**
- Specific, not generic. "A read on three areas where [problem] typically hides in [company type]." Not "Valuable insights tailored to your needs."

**Who runs it (50–75 words):**
- Named practitioner credential. Not a generic "our experts." "This session is led by [name], who has [specific credential relevant to the problem]."

**What this is not:**
- Explicit expectation-setting: "This isn't a pitch or discovery call for a sale. We use this to see if we're actually the right fit for your situation."

**Booking form / calendar embed directions:**
- Default: Calendar embed (Calendly, Cal.com) with a short qualifier field: "What's the main challenge you want to address?" (free text, under 150 chars).

**Social proof strip:** 1–2 one-line client quotes specific to the firm's credibility.

Tone: Generous and transparent. "We're offering this because we want to see if we're the right fit, and this is the best way to find out."

### 3. Case Study Landing Page — SEO + Proposal Support

Write a case study landing page for a professional services firm. This page serves double duty: SEO for problem-aware search and proposal support for active prospects.

Context: Client type: [industry + company size, anonymized or named]. Challenge: [Specific problem]. Approach: [Methodology or engagement type]. Outcome: [Metrics and qualitative result].

Page structure:

**H1:** "[Client context] overcomes [challenge]: a [service type] case study."
- Example: "Mid-market logistics firm reduces operational cost 22% through supply chain restructure."

**Summary card (above fold):**
- Challenge / Approach / Outcome — 2–3 bullet each — maximum 15 words per point.

**Full narrative (5 sections):**
1. **The situation (150 words):** Client context, what they were dealing with, stakes.
2. **What wasn't working (100 words):** Why existing approaches hadn't resolved it.
3. **Our approach (200 words):** Methodology, key decisions, how we worked with the client team.
4. **Results (100 words):** Quantified outcomes + qualitative description of what changed.
5. **What this demonstrates (75 words):** Generalizable insight for prospects facing a similar challenge.

**Related service link:** "Working through something similar? See our [service type] page."

**Bottom CTA:** "Let's discuss your situation. [Schedule a call]."

Tone: Factual, specific, confident. The case study earns the CTA on the strength of its specificity.

### 4. Homepage Expertise Section / Hero Module

Write the expertise and credibility section for a professional services firm's homepage (above-the-fold hero or "Who We Are" module).

Context: Firm type: [e.g., boutique strategy firm, independent agency, specialized consultancy]. Primary practice area: [service type]. ICP: [company size, industry, role]. Core differentiator: [What makes this firm meaningfully different from alternatives].

**Hero section deliverables:**

**H1 (8–14 words, outcome-led):**
- For the right ICP, names both their situation and what changes.
- Example: "Strategy and execution for consumer brands navigating scaling. Without the overhead."

**Subheadline (1–2 sentences):** Who this is for + what kind of work the firm does + why they're trusted.

**Proof strip (3 signals):**
- Client count or engagement count (specifics beat "many clients")
- Industry range or notable client types
- One specific outcome metric from client work

**CTA block:**
- Primary: "See our work" (→ case studies)
- Secondary: "Let's talk" (→ discovery call page)

**Positioning statement (50–75 words, below fold):**
- The firm's philosophy in plain language. Not a mission statement — a statement of conviction about the right way to do the work.

Tone: Senior, specific, confident. The homepage hero earns the discovery conversation by making the right buyer feel understood in the first 20 seconds.
