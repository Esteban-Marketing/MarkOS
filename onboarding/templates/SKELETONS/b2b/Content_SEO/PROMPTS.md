# B2B — Content & SEO Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2B SEO wins on solution-category keywords, original research, and thought leadership that earns links and builds topical authority. The best B2B content arms buyers with the language to justify decisions upward — it doesn't just explain the product. Prompts here focus on high-converting bottom-funnel and mid-funnel SEO content that generates qualified pipeline.

## Prompts

### 1. Original Research Report Outline — Thought Leadership + Link Bait

Outline a B2B original research report for a company in [industry/category, e.g., "HR technology"] targeting the keyword cluster [primary keyword + variants, e.g., "employee onboarding statistics / onboarding process benchmarks"].

The report should:
- Be based on a survey of [N] [ICP title, e.g., "HR leaders at companies with 50–500 employees"]
- Include 5–8 data-backed findings formatted as shareable statistics
- Each finding should connect to a pain point the ICP cares about
- Include a "what this means for [ICP title]" section after each stat
- End with a 3-action recommendations section
- Format: slide-ready executive summary (250 words) + full report outline with section headers

Tone: authoritative, data-led, written as a peer practitioner, not a vendor publishing a sales piece.

### 2. Solution Category Landing Page — SEO + Conversion

Write an SEO-optimized landing page for a B2B company targeting the keyword "[solution category keyword, e.g., 'procurement software for mid-market']."

Page structure:
- **H1**: Primary keyword integrated naturally as a benefit-led headline
- **Above fold (150 words)**: Pain framing + solution category positioning + social proof strip (logo bar or metric)
- **"How it works" section (200 words)**: 3–4 steps showing the solution mechanism — specific, not generic
- **Proof section**: 2 customer quotes (one from a practitioner-level buyer, one from an economic buyer/executive) — both should name the outcome, not just express happiness
- **FAQ (5 questions)**: Address the top 5 objections and decision questions for this category
- **CTA section**: Demo request or free trial — with a trust signal below the form (SOC 2, no credit card, etc.)

SEO requirements: keyword in H1, first paragraph, and at least one subheading. Internal link to 2 related pages.

### 3. Comparison / Alternative Page — Decision-Stage Content

Write a "[Competitor] alternative" or "[X vs. Y]" comparison page for a B2B company targeting buyers who are currently evaluating [competitor name or category alternatives].

Page requirements:
- **Intro (100 words)**: Acknowledge the comparison head-on. Don't be defensive — be confident and fair.
- **Comparison table**: 6–8 criteria important to the ICP (not cherry-picked strengths). Include a row where the competitor is genuinely competitive — this builds credibility.
- **"Who [Product] is best for" section**: Be specific (company size, team structure, use case).
- **"Who [Competitor] might be better for"**: Be honest — this earns trust from buyers who would have found out anyway.
- **Customer migration section**: One mini case study of a customer who switched from [competitor] — what changed, what they gained.
- **CTA**: Schedule a comparison call or start a trial

Tone: transparent, confident, non-defensive. This is a trust-builder, not a hit piece.

---

### 4. Thought Leadership Article — Practitioner POV

Write a bylined thought leadership article for [practitioner name/title, e.g., "Head of RevOps at a mid-market SaaS company"] targeting the keyword [informational keyword, e.g., "how to align sales and marketing in B2B"].

Article structure:
- **Hook (100 words)**: A counterintuitive observation or a widely-held belief the author disagrees with
- **The real problem (150 words)**: Why the conventional wisdom fails — what the author has seen in practice
- **The mechanism (200 words)**: The author's framework, process, or principle — specific, with named examples
- **Evidence (100 words)**: 2 data points or real-world outcomes that support the argument
- **Practical application (150 words)**: What a reader should do differently next week
- **Internal link**: One CTA to a relevant solution page or related article

Tone: named author, peer-to-peer, practitioner-led. Not corporate blog. Not generic "5 tips."
