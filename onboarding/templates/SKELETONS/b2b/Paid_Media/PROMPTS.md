# B2B — Paid Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2B paid media is account-based and intent-driven. LinkedIn dominates for reaching decision-makers and economic buyers. Google Search captures in-market buyers with solution-category intent. Prompts here reflect multi-stakeholder targeting, lead-gen form optimization, and ROI-framed ad copy — not brand awareness.

## Prompts

### 1. LinkedIn Lead Gen Form Ad — Targeting a Committee Member

Write a LinkedIn Sponsored Content ad with a Lead Gen Form for a B2B software company targeting [ICP title, e.g., "VP of Operations at manufacturing companies with 200–2,000 employees"].

The ad promotes a gated asset (research report, ROI calculator, or how-to guide) relevant to a specific operational pain [describe the pain, e.g., "managing compliance documentation across distributed teams"].

Requirements:
- Headline (under 70 chars): benefit-led, specific to the pain
- Intro text (under 150 chars): open with the problem, not the product name
- CTA: "Download now" or "Get the guide"
- Tone: credible peer, not vendor pitch
- The form should capture: first name, last name, work email, company name, job title

---

### 2. LinkedIn Thought Leadership Sponsored Content — Awareness Stage

Write a LinkedIn Sponsored Content post for a B2B company in [category, e.g., "accounts payable automation"] targeting [ICP title] at companies with [size range, e.g., "100–1,000 employees"].

The post should:
- Open with a stat, observation, or industry trend (not a product mention)
- Build a brief "here's what we're seeing" narrative that speaks to the problem without pitching a solution
- End with a soft CTA (e.g., "What's your current approach? Drop it below" or a link to a relevant article)
- Tone: thought leadership, authored as a named practitioner, not a brand account

---

### 3. Google Search Ad — High-Intent Solution Category Keyword

Write a Google Search ad for a B2B company competing on the keyword "[solution category keyword, e.g., 'contract management software']".

Requirements:
- 3 responsive headline variants (30 chars max each):
  - Variant 1: product + primary outcome
  - Variant 2: objection address (e.g., "No IT team required")
  - Variant 3: social proof (e.g., "3,400+ enterprise teams")
- 2 description lines (90 chars max each):
  - Line 1: pain → outcome
  - Line 2: credibility signal + CTA
- Final URL: the solution category landing page
- Tone: confident, specific, direct — matching the buyer's in-market intent

---

### 4. LinkedIn Retargeting Ad — Re-Engage Website Visitors

Write a LinkedIn retargeting ad for B2B software visitors who viewed the pricing page but did not start a trial or request a demo. Goal: nudge them to take the next step.

Requirements:
- Image/creative direction: product screenshot or customer outcome visual (no stock photos of handshakes)
- Headline: acknowledge where they are in the journey without being creepy ("Still evaluating [category]?")
- Body text (under 150 chars): reinforce a key reason to choose this product — use a specific outcome or customer proof point, not a generic claim
- CTA: "See it in action" or "Book a 30-min demo"
- Tone: helpful, low-pressure — not "you were on our site so buy now"
