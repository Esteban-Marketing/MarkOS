# B2B — Landing Pages Starter Prompts

> **Context:** B2B landing pages must build conviction, not manufacture urgency. The primary goal is to convert a skeptical, risk-aware buyer — give them enough proof to take the next step, reduce the perceived risk of a demo or trial, and arm them with the language to justify the decision internally. High-performing B2B landing pages are specific, proof-dense, and CTA-focused without being pushy.

## Prompts

### 1. Demo Request Landing Page — Solution Category

Write a B2B demo request landing page for a company offering [solution category, e.g., "contract lifecycle management software"] targeting [ICP, e.g., "legal ops and procurement teams at companies with 200+ employees"].

Page structure:
- **Headline (H1)**: Outcome-focused, names the category and the ICP's primary desired outcome. 10–14 words max.
- **Subheadline (2 sentences)**: Expand the promise — what changes after a demo, why the demo is worth 30 minutes
- **3 key points** (icons + short labels): Primary outcomes this solution delivers — specific, quantified where possible
- **Social proof strip**: 3–5 company logos + one metric ("Trusted by 1,200+ legal teams" or "4.8/5 on G2")
- **Form fields**: First name, last name, work email, company name, company size (optional), your primary challenge (dropdown — 5 options)
- **Trust signals below form**: No spam. No credit card. Cancel anytime (if applicable). SOC 2 / security badge.
- **One customer quote**: Specific to the demo → value journey ("Within 2 weeks of our demo, we had a clear ROI case to take to the CFO.")
- **FAQ (3 questions)**: What happens after I submit? How long is the demo? Can I include my team?

Tone: Confident, specific, low-pressure. The page should feel like being introduced to a solution, not recruited by a sales team.

### 2. Free Trial Landing Page — Product-Led Signup

Write a B2B free trial landing page for a [product category] product with a self-serve trial (no credit card required). Target persona: [ICP, e.g., "operations manager at a mid-market company"].

Page requirements:
- **Headline**: Clear value proposition — what the trial unlocks in plain language
- **Above-fold (short)**: 3-bullet list of what they can do in the trial RIGHT NOW (specific actions, not generic features)
- **How it works** (3 steps): Sign up → [First value action] → [Activation milestone]. Each step in 8–12 words.
- **Proof section**: One short case study panel — customer name/title, what they built in their first week, and a metric outcome
- **Objection handling (3 lines)**:
  - "Does this work for teams our size?" → answer
  - "Do I need IT help to set this up?" → answer
  - "What happens when the trial ends?" → answer
- **CTA button copy**: Not "Start your free trial." Instead: "[Action that describes first win, e.g., 'Map your first workflow for free']"

Tone: Product-confident, activation-focused. Sell the first win, not the full feature set.

### 3. Case Study Landing Page — Full Narrative

Write a full case study landing page for a B2B company featuring [customer name or type] who achieved [primary outcome, e.g., "40% reduction in invoice processing time"].

Structure:
- **Header**: Customer name, industry, company size, primary outcome metric (hero stat)
- **Challenge section (150 words)**: What the customer was dealing with before — specific, named pain, not "they needed a better solution"
- **Evaluation section (100 words)**: Briefly why they chose this vendor over alternatives — at least one honest trade-off considered
- **Implementation section (100 words)**: What the rollout looked like, who was involved, how long to first value
- **Results section**: 3 quantified outcomes (primary metric + 2 secondary) + one customer quote per outcome
- **Customer bio box**: Name, title, LinkedIn (if available), photo (if available)
- **CTA section**: "See if this is right for your team" → demo request form or "Read another story" link

Tone: Documentary, not marketing. The customer is the protagonist. The vendor is the tool that enabled the outcome.

### 4. Solution Page — Persona-Specific Pain Angle

Write a solution landing page for a B2B company targeting a specific persona ([ICP title, e.g., "CFO at a Series B startup"]) with a pain-specific angle ([pain, e.g., "lack of real-time financial visibility during fundraising"]).

This page is NOT the homepage. It's a lower-funnel page for a buyer who already knows the category and wants to understand if this solution fits their specific situation.

Structure:
- **H1**: The pain or outcome — from the persona's vocabulary, not the vendor's product naming
- **Pain description (100 words)**: What this persona's world looks like when this problem is unsolved — specific scenarios, costs, failure modes
- **Solution mechanism (100 words)**: How the product addresses this specific pain — from the persona's outcome, not the feature spec
- **Proof (2 mini case studies)**: Both from the same persona-type, both with a named quantified outcome
- **Qualification widget**: "This is for you if…" and "This is NOT for you if…" (2 bullets each) — builds trust through specificity
- **CTA**: Specific to this persona — "Book a 20-min CFO walkthrough" not "Request a Demo"

Tone: Persona-fluent. The page should read as if it was written by someone who has had this exact conversation 50 times.
