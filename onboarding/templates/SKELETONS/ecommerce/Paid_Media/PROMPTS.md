# Ecommerce — Paid Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Ecommerce paid media lives and dies by ROAS and conversion rate. Meta Dynamic Product Ads (DPA) and Google Shopping are the dominant channels. Creative must compete in a high-volume feed environment — product visual quality and copy directness beat brand storytelling at this layer. Prompts here focus on SKU-level creative briefs, Google Shopping feed optimization, and retargeting copy for mid-to-bottom funnel.

## Prompts

### 1. Meta Dynamic Product Ad (DPA) — Creative Brief

Write a Meta Dynamic Product Ad creative brief for an ecommerce brand.

Product context: [Product name, category, key differentiator]. Target audience: [ICP — age range, lifestyle, purchase intent signal].

Deliverable (per ad variant):

**Visual direction (for each of 3 variants):**
- Variant A: Product-isolated on white/soft background — clean product hero.
- Variant B: Product in-use context — the buyer's lifestyle moment.
- Variant C: Before/after or problem-solution visual if applicable to the product category.

**Headline copy (25 chars max per Meta spec):**
- Benefit-first or product-specific. Example: "Lasts all week. Tested."

**Primary copy (125 chars):**
- Outcome + product name + credibility signal. Example: "[Product] — [X,000] 5-star reviews. Free returns."

**CTA button:** "Shop now" or "Learn more" — "Shop now" for conversion campaigns; "Learn more" for catalog traffic.

**Tone:** Direct response. No brand manifesto. Compete in-feed on clarity and specificity.

### 2. Google Shopping Feed Copy Optimization

Write optimized Google Shopping product titles and descriptions for a product catalog.

For each product (input: [product name, specs, key benefit, price point]):

**Product title** (max 150 chars, front-load key terms):
- Format: `[Brand] + [Product Type] + [Key Attribute] + [Size/Color/Model if applicable]`
- Example: "Bellroy Slim Wallet — Leather, 12-Card, Brown — RFID Block"

**Product description** (max 500 chars, written for the search buyer):
- Open with the top buyer benefit (not the product category).
- Include 2–3 key specifications relevant to the purchase decision.
- Material, fit, dimensions, compatibility — whatever removes doubt for this product type.
- Close with: "Free shipping" / "Free returns" / "Ships in [X] days" if applicable.

Tone: Search-intent-aware. The buyer searching this term knows what they want. Description should confirm this product solves their specific version of that want.

### 3. Mid-Funnel Video Retargeting Brief — Meta / YouTube

Write a 15–30 second video ad brief for ecommerce mid-funnel retargeting (visitors who viewed a product but didn't convert).

Deliverable — a creative brief document with:

**Audience signal:** Visited [product/category page] in the last [X] days, no purchase.

**Hook (first 3 seconds — on-screen text overlay):**
- Address the hesitation directly. Examples: "Still thinking about it?" / "Here's what 2,400 buyers say." / "Free returns. No risk."

**Content seconds 3–15:**
- Product visual in context (lifestyle or UGC).
- 1–2 overlay text lines: Key objection removed + proof. Example: "Delivered in 3 days. 4.9 stars on 2,400 reviews."

**CTA overlay (last 5 seconds):**
- Clear: "Shop now — free returns." Never use "Learn more" in retargeting.

**Voiceover (optional):** Script for 15s VO if the brand uses voiceover format.

Tone: Warm recall + obstacle removal. The buyer already considered; this ad should complete the conversion by resolving the final doubt.

### 4. Google Search Ad Copy — Brand + Product Intent

Write Google Search ad copy for ecommerce search campaigns targeting product and brand-adjacent keywords.

Campaign type inputs: Keyword themes: [e.g., "[product type] for [use case]", "[brand] [product]"]. Landing page: [product page or category page URL].

For each keyword theme, write 2 responsive search ad (RSA) headline sets:

**Format per set:**
- 5 unique headlines (30 chars max each) — focus on: product benefit, social proof number, delivery speed, return policy, and one promotional headline (if sale is live)
- 2 descriptions (90 chars max each) — one benefit-led, one trust/objection-removal

**Example output format:**
- Headlines: `[Benefit]` / `[X Reviews—Avg 4.9★]` / `[Free Returns, Free Ship]` / `[Sale—20% Off Today]` / `[As Seen In [Publication]]`
- Descriptions: `[Product] that [outcome]. Shop the full collection at [Brand]` / `Free [delivery time] shipping + hassle-free returns. [N]+ happy buyers.`

Tone: Specific and credible. Don't over-trust the buyer's keyword intent — confirm the product's specific fit in ad copy.
