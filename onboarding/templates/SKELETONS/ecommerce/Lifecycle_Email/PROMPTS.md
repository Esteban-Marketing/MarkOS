# Ecommerce — Lifecycle Email Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Email is the highest-ROI channel in ecommerce. The key sequences are cart abandonment recovery, post-purchase experience, and LTV-extending retention flows. Tone is product-specific, personal, and purchase-context-aware. Unlike B2C brand email, ecommerce email is close to a transaction — it should treat the subscriber as a buyer, not a passive audience member.

## Prompts

### 1. Abandoned Cart Recovery — 3-Email Sequence

Write a 3-email abandoned cart sequence for an ecommerce brand.

Context: Cart abandoned by: [customer type — first-time visitor vs. known customer]. Products in cart: [product name(s) and price]. Abandonment typical reasons: [price uncertainty, checkout friction, undecided, comparison shopping].

**Email 1 — Cart Reminder (send at 1–2 hours post-abandonment):**
- Subject line: "[Product name] is in your cart" or "You left something behind"
- Body (100–120 words): Friendly, no pressure. Product image + name, key benefit restatement, CTA button back to cart.
- Tone: Service-first. "Your cart is saved for you."

**Email 2 — Product Proof (send at 24 hours):**
- Subject line: "[X] buyers chose [product] this month. Here's why."
- Body (120–150 words): Feature a specific positive review. Add social proof count. Soft CTA — "Still interested? Your cart is saved."
- Optional add: Return policy or size guide link if applicable.

**Email 3 — Final Nudge / Soft Incentive (send at 72 hours — only if brand uses incentives):**
- Subject line: "Last chance to complete your [product] order" or "[10%] off — just for you" (if incentive used)
- Body (80–100 words): Clear decision moment framing. Incentive code if brand uses them. Hard deadline if sale applies.
- Tone: Warm close, not pushy. "We'd love to have you as a customer."

Three variants per email (subject lines): A/B/C for testing.

### 2. Post-Purchase Welcome Flow — First-Time Buyer

Write a post-purchase first-buyer email sequence (2 emails) for an ecommerce product.

Context: Product purchased: [product name, category]. Delivery timeline: [X–Y business days]. Brand voice: [brief tone descriptor].

**Email 1 — Order Confirmation + "What to Expect" (send immediately):**
- Subject: "Your [product] order is confirmed — here's what's next"
- Body: Order details recap, expected delivery date, key product care/use tip to set up success.
- Tone: Warm and organized. Reinforce the purchase decision without over-celebrating.
- Optional: Include 1 relevant cross-sell (product that pairs with the purchase) — not prominent, below order details.

**Email 2 — First Use / Unboxing (send on estimated delivery day or +1):**
- Subject: "Your [product] just arrived — here's how to [key action/first use]"
- Body (100–130 words): First-use tip or quick-start guide. Product photo in use (lifestyle). Soft ask: "If you love it, leave us a review — it takes 30 seconds." Link to review page.
- Tone: Practical delight. Not over-effusive. This email builds loyalty by being useful on day one.

### 3. Review Request Email — Timed to Product Experience

Write a post-purchase review request email for an ecommerce brand.

Context: Product: [product name]. Days since delivery: [typically 7–14 days for considered products, 3–5 for consumables]. Brand: [name and tone].

**Email (single email, 80–100 words):**
- Subject line options (3 variants): Make it specific to the product ("How are your [product] holding up?"), conversational, not corporate.
- Body:
  - 1 sentence: "We hope you're [using/enjoying] your [product]."
  - Review request: Specific, not generic. "If it's been good, a quick rating helps other [buyer type like you] decide. It takes under a minute."
  - Review link button: "[Rate your [product]]" — not "Leave a review."
  - Sign-off: First name from the brand's customer success or founder — not "The [Brand] Team."

Tone: Casual, personal, non-automated-feeling. Reviews earned by service quality, not volume.

### 4. LTV / Repurchase Flow — Based on Product Lifecycle

Write a repurchase trigger email for an ecommerce brand selling products with a natural reorder cycle.

Context: Product type: [consumables / wearables / seasonal / subscription-eligible]. Average product lifecycle: [X weeks/months]. Customer purchase history: First-time vs. repeat.

**Email (single email, 90–110 words):**
- Subject line: Timing-aware hook. Options: "Time for a refill?" / "Your [product] is almost done, right?" / "We think you're running low."
- Body:
  - Personal opener: "[Product] lasts about [X weeks]. Yours should be getting toward the end."
  - Single CTA: "Reorder in 2 clicks." (Pre-fill cart or one-click reorder if platform supports.)
  - Optional: Subscription upsell at bottom ("Never run out — subscribe and save [10%].")

Three subject line variants for A/B testing.

Tone: Practical and non-intrusive. This email should feel like a reminder app, not marketing.
