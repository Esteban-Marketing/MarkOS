# Ecommerce — Content & SEO Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Ecommerce SEO combines product listing optimization, category page authority, and editorial content that captures early-purchase-intent search. The goal is owning the research and comparison queries that precede transactions. Prompts here cover product listing copy, category pages, gift/buying guides, and review-optimized product content.

## Prompts

### 1. Product Detail Page (PDP) Copy — Conversion-Optimized

Write conversion-optimized product detail page copy for an ecommerce product.

Product context: [Product name, category, key features, intended use, price point]. Target buyer: [Demographics, purchase motivation].

Deliverable:

**Page title / H1 (under 60 chars, keyword-forward):**
- Include product name + primary differentiator. Example: "Merino Wool Running Socks — Anti-blister, Temperature Regulating"

**Above-fold product description (75–100 words):**
- Open with the outcome/use-case, not the product. "For [use case]: [product name] does [key function] so [buyer benefit]."
- Cover: Key differentiator + material/quality signal + fit/compatibility context.
- End with the micro-commitment sentence that pushes to add-to-cart.

**Bullets (5 bullets, benefit + spec format):**
- Format: **[Benefit label]** — one sentence combining what it does and why it matters.
- Include: Primary function, material quality, sizing/fit, care/maintenance, one unique differentiator.

**Story section (150–200 words):** Brand/maker context or craftsmanship narrative — for products where "how it's made" increases perceived value.

Tone: Product-specific, outcome-first. No generic superlatives ("best quality"). Every sentence should help the buyer decide if this product is for them.

### 2. Category Page Copy — SEO Hub

Write a category page copy block for an ecommerce brand.

Category: [e.g., "Women's Running Shoes", "Premium Coffee Beans", "Sustainable Activewear"]. Category URL: [slug].

Deliverable:

**H1 / Category headline (under 60 chars, includes primary keyword):**

**Category intro (100–150 words — above product grid):**
- What this category is for — the buyer context.
- What makes this brand's version of this category different from the alternatives.
- Keywords naturally integrated (avoid stuffing).
- Internal link hook: "Also exploring [related category]? See our [link text]."

**Category subcategory nav blurbs** (if subcategories exist — 2–3 sentences each):
- "Shop [subcategory]: [brief description of what's in it and who it's for]."

**FAQ section (4 questions with answers, max 100 words per answer):**
- Build from actual buyer search queries for this category (buying guide intent, comparison intent, care/use intent, return/size question).

Tone: Helpful and knowledgeable. This page should be the most useful starting point for a buyer new to the category from this brand.

### 3. Gift Guide / Buying Guide — Editorial SEO

Write a gift guide or buying guide article for an ecommerce brand.

Context: Guide type: [Gift guide: occasion/recipient] or [Buying guide: category purchase decision]. Products to feature: [3–8 products from catalog]. SEO title target: [e.g., "Best [product category] gifts for [recipient/occasion", "How to choose [product type]: the [year] guide"].

Article structure:

**H1 and SEO meta title** (under 60 chars, includes year/occasion if relevant):

**Introduction (150–200 words):**
- Who this guide is for + what it helps them decide.
- Brief framing: What separates good choices from generic ones in this category.

**For each product (product blurb, 75–100 words):**
- **[Product Name]** — [Price] — [Who it's best for: use case + buyer type]
- 2–3 sentences on why this specific product makes sense + what makes it stand out in category.
- Social proof signal if available: star rating, review count, publication mention.

**Buying considerations section** (3–4 H3s with 75 words each):
- Cover: Key specs to look for, fit/compatibility considerations, price range expectations, common mistakes.

Tone: Curator's voice — helpful, specific, and honest about which product is for which buyer. Not puff; buyers trust guides that acknowledge trade-offs.

### 4. Product Category Comparison Page — SEO + Consideration

Write a product comparison page comparing 2–3 products within a brand's catalog.

"[Product A] vs [Product B] — Which Is Right for You?" (or "vs [Product C]")

Context: Products being compared: [Product A, Product B (optional: Product C)]. Key differentiating attributes: [Price, use case, materials, intensity level, etc.].

Page structure:

**H1 and meta description** — clear comparison framing, keyword-forward.

**Quick verdict section (100 words):**
- At a glance: [Product A] is best for [buyer type A]. [Product B] is best for [buyer type B].
- No hedging — commit to the recommendation.

**Comparison table:**
| Feature | [Product A] | [Product B] |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| [Attribute] | [Value] | [Value] |
Note: Include 5–7 attributes. Focus on decision-relevant criteria, not full spec sheets.

**Deep dive per product (150 words each):**
- Who it's for, what it's exceptional at, what trade-off it asks of the buyer.

**Our recommendation:**
- "If you're [use case A], go with [Product A]. If you're [use case B], [Product B] is the better fit."

Tone: Confident, impartial judgment. The page earns trust by being willing to say which product wins for which buyer.
