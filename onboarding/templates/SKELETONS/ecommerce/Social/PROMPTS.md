# Ecommerce — Social Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Ecommerce social media converts through product visibility and purchase trust. UGC outperforms brand creative in most categories. The feed is competitive; the visual hook is the first job. Prompts here focus on product demo, UGC amplification, and social proof formats that drive either direct conversion or remarketing audiences.

## Prompts

### 1. UGC-Style Product Demo Brief — Reels / TikTok / Meta

Write a UGC-style video creative brief for a 15–30 second product demo video (Reels, TikTok, or Meta vertical video).

Product: [Product name and category]. Target buyer: [ICP]. Key objection the video should overcome: [e.g., "does it actually work?", "how does it fit?", "is it worth the price?"].

Creative brief document:

**Creator direction:**
- Tone: First-person, authentic. Feels like a recommendation to a friend, not an ad.
- Setting: [specified for product — kitchen counter for food, bathroom shelf for skincare, outdoors for gear].
- Energy level: [calm/neutral, enthusiastic, problem-solving].

**Script structure (3 beats):**
1. **Hook (0–3s):** Problem or surprising product moment. Start mid-use, not with an intro. "I've tried every [category] and this one is the one."
2. **Demo (3–20s):** Show the product doing the key thing the buyer worries about. Don't explain — show.
3. **Close (20–30s):** Outcome statement + where to buy. "Linked in bio" or "Use code [X] at checkout."

**Text overlay copy (3 frames):** Hook frame → Benefit statement → CTA.

### 2. Product Launch Social Post — Instagram / TikTok

Write a product launch social media post for an ecommerce brand launching a new product.

Product: [Product name, what it is, what problem it solves]. Launch type: [New SKU / color / collaboration / category expansion].

Format A — Instagram caption (150–200 words):
- Opening line: Product visual hook — describe what the product looks like in use.
- Context: Why this product, why now, who it's for.
- Product highlight: 2–3 specific features — show them, don't pitch them.
- CTA: "Get it first — link in bio. Limited stock." (Only if stock genuinely is limited.)
- Hashtags: 5–8 relevant, not generic.

Format B — TikTok / Reels caption (50–70 words):
- Opening hook (first 7 words — the only part guaranteed seen before scroll): Must be a question, surprising claim, or visual promise.
- Short context: One sentence on the product.
- CTA: "Wait until you see [X]... Link in bio."

**Alt text description for product image:** Product name + key visual attribute + context description.

### 3. Seasonal Promotion Post — Sale / Limited Run

Write a seasonal promotional social post for an ecommerce brand running a limited-time sale or seasonal product drop.

Context: Promotion type: [e.g., Black Friday, summer sale, capsule collection, holiday bundle]. Products featured: [SKU or category]. Discount or offer: [X% off / bundle price / free shipping threshold]. Sale window: [Start date to end date].

**Post caption — Meta / Instagram (150 words max):**
- Do not open with "We're excited to announce." Open with the buyer's benefit or the product.
- Sale details: Product/category, offer, end date.
- Urgency signal if real: "Once [inventory] is gone, it's gone." or "Sale ends [date]."
- CTA: "Shop now — link in bio." or "Use code [CODE] at checkout."

**Stories variation (3-slide sequence):**
- Slide 1: Product visual + sale hook ("Up to [X]% off — this weekend only")
- Slide 2: Featured product + price/discount
- Slide 3: CTA slide — button + end date

**Caption tone:** Energetic but not frantic. FOMO without manipulation — only real scarcity language.

### 4. Customer Review / UGC Amplification Post

Write a social post amplifying a customer review or user-generated content for an ecommerce brand.

Context: Review content: [Review text or UGC description]. Reviewer type: [verified buyer, content creator, public post]. Product reviewed: [Product name].

**Option A — Pull-quote graphic caption (100–120 words):**
- Quote from reviewer in large text (visual design direction included).
- Caption: 2 sentences — context for who the reviewer is (their use case) + the product being reviewed.
- Soft CTA: "Read 2,000+ reviews at [link in bio]" — not "Buy now."
- Hashtag: One brand hashtag + one category hashtag.

**Option B — Repost/UGC reshare caption (60–80 words):**
- "We love seeing [product] in the wild." + Tag of original creator.
- 2 sentences on what the creator showed in the post.
- CTA: "Add yours — tag us @[brand] for a chance to be featured."

Tone: Genuine acknowledgment. The brand's voice is in the background; the customer's experience is the content.
