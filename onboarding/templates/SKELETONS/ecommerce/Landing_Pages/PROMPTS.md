# Ecommerce — Landing Pages Starter Prompts

> **Context:** Ecommerce landing pages reduce purchase friction and drive conversion from paid traffic, email campaigns, and social CTAs. The priority is product clarity, social proof density, and checkout confidence signals. Long brand stories belong elsewhere — landing pages are for buyers who are close to a decision but need one more push.

## Prompts

### 1. Product Page — Conversion Psychology Framework

Write a complete product detail page conversion copy framework for an ecommerce product being sold through paid traffic.

Product: [Product name, category, price, key benefit, primary buyer concern].

Page copy structure (generate each section):

**Above-the-fold:**
- H1: Product name + primary differentiator (60 chars max)
- Subheadline (1 sentence): The outcome or use case this product delivers
- Star rating + review count as inline trust signal: "★★★★★ [X] verified reviews"
- Add-to-cart CTA: "[Add to Cart — Free Shipping Over $[X]]"

**Product bullets (5 — benefit + spec format):**
- Each bullet: **[Benefit label]** — one sentence combining outcome and technical reason-to-believe

**Social proof section:**
- 3 featured reviews: Specific outcomes, specific buyer context, no generic "great product!" quotes
- Aggregate rating badge: "[X,XXX] buyers give it [X.X] stars average"

**Objection removal section (FAQ, 4 questions):**
- Returns policy, shipping speed, warranty/guarantee, size/fit (if applicable)

**Secondary CTA:** "Not sure? See all [X] reviews or chat with us."

### 2. Sale / Promotions Landing Page

Write a promotional landing page for an ecommerce sale event (Black Friday, sitewide discount, product-specific promotion).

Context: Sale type: [Event name / offer type]. Discount: [X% off / free shipping / bundle deal]. Featured products: [2–4 products or categories]. Deadline: [Date/time].

Page sections:

**Hero (above fold):**
- Headline (8–12 words): Sale benefit first, not brand first. "Up to [X]% off — [specific products or category]."
- Countdown timer description: "[Sale ends in X hours/days]" — only if real. Never fake.
- Primary CTA: "Shop the Sale" (anchors to product grid below)

**Featured product cards (2–4 products):**
- Per card: Product name + original price + sale price + primary benefit + "Add to Cart"
- No creative writing — direct conversion format

**Sale terms microcopy:**
- "Offer valid through [date]. While supplies last." — critical for credibility

**Trust strip (3 icons + labels):**
- Free shipping threshold / Free returns / Secure checkout

Tone: Energetic and specific. Specific products, specific discounts, specific end dates. Vagueness loses conversions.

### 3. Product Category Landing Page — Paid Traffic (Non-SEO)

Write a landing page specifically for paid traffic to a product category (vs. the permanent SEO category page).

Category: [e.g., "women's summer dresses", "outdoor kitchen grills", "noise-canceling headphones"]. Traffic source: [Meta / Google / Email]. Campaign message: [Seasonal angle, promo, or product feature campaign].

Page structure:

**Hero section:**
- H1: Campaign-specific, matches ad message. ("The [season] collection is here" / "Our most-reviewed [product type]")
- Subheadline: Category differentiator for this brand — 1 sentence.
- Trust signal: Review aggregate or "X orders shipped this week."
- CTA: "Shop [category]" — anchors to filtered product grid.

**Product grid intro (50 words):**
- Brief guide to the collection: "Browse by [use case/type/occasion]."

**Urgency/scarcity signal (if real):** "Only [X] left in stock for [product]." — product-level, not page-level fake urgency.

**Collection CTA at bottom:** "Can't find what you need? [Browse full catalog]."

Tone: Campaign-coherent — the page copy should feel like a natural extension of the ad that drove the click.

### 4. Collection Bundle / Product Bundle Landing Page

Write a landing page for a product bundle or kit being sold as a single SKU.

Bundle context: [Bundle name and description]. Component products: [List of included items]. Bundle price vs. individual: [Actual savings amount or % — specific]. Best for: [Use case / buyer type].

Page sections:

**Hero:**
- H1: What the bundle delivers — the combined outcome, not a list of what's in it.
- Example: "Everything you need to [achieve goal] — in one order."
- Price + savings badge: "Save $XX — Bundle price: $XX.XX"

**What's included (component breakdown):**
- List each item with individual value: "[Item] ($XX value) — [1-sentence benefit]"
- Total bundle value vs. bundle price — "Total if bought separately: $XXX. Bundle: $XX."

**Who this is for (50–75 words):**
- Specific use case framing. Buyers converting on bundles are solving a "whole problem" — name it.

**Social proof:** If bundle-specific reviews exist, use them. If not, pull reviews from the most recognizable component product.

**CTA:** "Add bundle to cart — $XX" (price in CTA for high-consideration bundles removes price surprise at checkout).
