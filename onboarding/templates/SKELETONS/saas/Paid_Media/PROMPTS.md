# SaaS — Paid Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** SaaS paid media spans Google Search (high-intent category and comparison keywords), LinkedIn (enterprise and persona targeting), and retargeting (trial visitors who didn't convert). Creative is product-proof-led and trial-CTA focused. Prompts here focus on driving qualified trial starts and demo requests, not brand awareness.

## Prompts

### 1. Google Search Ad — High-Intent Category Keyword

Write a Google Search ad for a SaaS product competing on the keyword "[category keyword, e.g., 'project management software for agencies']."

Requirements:
- 3 responsive headline variants (30 chars max each):
  - Variant 1: Category + primary ICP outcome
  - Variant 2: Trial framing (e.g., "Free 14-Day Trial · No Card")
  - Variant 3: Social proof signal (e.g., "4.8/5 · 12,000 Teams")
- 2 description lines (90 chars max each):
  - Line 1: Pain acknowledgment → product mechanism → time-to-value
  - Line 2: Trust signal + trial CTA
- Sitelinks (4): Pricing · Integrations · Case Studies · Start Free Trial
- Tone: Direct, product-confident, intent-matched.

---

### 2. LinkedIn Sponsored Content — Enterprise Tier, Decision Stage

Write a LinkedIn Sponsored Content ad targeting [ICP title, e.g., "Head of Engineering or VP Product at companies with 500+ employees"] for a mid-market SaaS product.

Context: Target audience is evaluating enterprise tooling and may be comparing with 2–3 alternatives. Goal: position the product as the most credible choice for their team size.

Requirements:
- Intro text (under 150 chars): Start with a problem or friction statement the ICP encounters when using incumbent tools at scale.
- Body (main text, under 600 chars): Address the scale limitation of alternatives, show the product's differentiating mechanism in 2 sentences, and drop a specific proof point (outcome metric or named customer at similar scale).
- CTA: "Request enterprise demo" or "See how [similar company] scaled with us."
- Tone: Peer-credible, confident, zero hype. Sounds like a practitioner recommendation, not a vendor pitch.

---

### 3. Retargeting Ad — Trial Visitor Who Didn't Start

Write a display/social retargeting ad for a SaaS product targeting website visitors who viewed the pricing or features page but did not start a free trial.

Platform: Meta (Facebook/Instagram) or LinkedIn.

Requirements:
- Creative direction: Product screenshot or outcome visual (not stock photography). Thumbnail should communicate "the product works" before they read a word.
- Headline (40–50 chars): Remove the specific friction point that's likeliest blocking trial start — common examples: "Try it free — no credit card" / "2-minute setup, no IT required" / "See your data in 5 minutes."
- Body text (80–100 chars): One specific outcome or customer proof point.
- CTA button: "Start Free Trial" or "See it in 5 minutes."
- Tone: Low-pressure, friction-reducing. Not "You were on our site, buy now."

---

### 4. YouTube Pre-Roll — Product Demo Hook (30 Second)

Write a 30-second unskippable + skippable (after 5 seconds) YouTube pre-roll video script for a SaaS product.

Context: Running against content consumed by [ICP, e.g., "engineers and developers watching technical tutorials"].

Script:
- **Seconds 0–5 (unskippable hook)**: The most specific, felt pain of the ICP — one sentence, no brand. Must be relevant enough that the viewer chooses not to skip.
- **Seconds 5–15**: Product reveal — show the product solving the exact pain from the hook. One clear action, one clear outcome. No feature tour.
- **Seconds 15–25**: One customer outcome or social proof point. Short, specific, credible. Not a testimonial paragraph.
- **Seconds 25–30**: Brand name + "Start free" CTA. Clear URL.
- **Visual direction**: Screen recording-style for seconds 5–15 (show the product). Real-person testimonial cutaway for seconds 15–25.

Tone: Technically credible. Sounds like it was made by someone who uses the product, not an agency.
