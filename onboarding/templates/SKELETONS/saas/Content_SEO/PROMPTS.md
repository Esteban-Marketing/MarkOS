# SaaS — Content & SEO Starter Prompts

> **Context:** SaaS SEO wins on category comparison keywords, use-case pages, integration pages, and long-tail problem-solution queries. The most conversion-effective content for SaaS is decision-stage: "[Product] vs [Competitor]," "[Category] for [Specific Use Case]," and "[Problem] software." Prompts here focus on high-converting content at the bottom and middle of the SaaS funnel.

## Prompts

### 1. Competitor Comparison / Alternative Page

Write a "[Product] vs [Competitor]" or "[Competitor] Alternative" page for a SaaS company targeting buyers who are actively evaluating both products.

Target keyword: "[Product] vs [Competitor name]" or "best [competitor] alternative."

Page structure:
- **H1**: Natural keyword — "[Product] vs [Competitor]: An Honest Comparison for [use case / team type]."
- **Intro (100 words)**: Acknowledge this is a real comparison people have. Don't pretend to be neutral — be honest about what each tool is best for.
- **Comparison table (8 rows)**: Pick 6 criteria where the product is competitive or better, and include 2 rows where the competitor is genuinely strong — this builds trust.
- **"Who [Product] is best for" section (80 words)**: Specific use case, company size, or workflow — not generic.
- **"Who [Competitor] might be better for" section (60 words)**: Be honest. Buyers who reach this page will trust you more for it.
- **Migration section**: 2–3 sentences on how to move from [Competitor] — emphasize ease and any migration support.
- **CTA**: "Start your free trial" + link to migration guide.

Tone: Transparent, confident, non-defensive. Earns trust.

### 2. Use-Case Landing Page — Vertical or Job Title SEO

Write an SEO landing page targeting the keyword "[product category] for [specific use case, e.g., 'project management software for marketing agencies']."

Page structure:
- **H1**: [Category] for [use case] — benefit-led variant of the keyword
- **Above fold (150 words)**: Brief pain framing specific to this use case, solution overview from the angle of the use case (not generic product pitch), social proof strip (logos or stats from this ICP type)
- **"Made for [ICP type]" section**: 3 use-case-specific features or workflows — described from the ICP's operational language, not the product's feature naming
- **Case study mini**: 100-word customer story from the same use case type — problem, what changed, outcome metric
- **Integration section**: 3–4 integrations most relevant to this ICP's stack (e.g., for agencies: Google Analytics, Slack, Harvest)
- **Pricing context** (optional): A single line indicating which plan covers this use case — reduces cognitive load
- **CTA**: Start free trial or "See [use case] setup in action."

### 3. Integration Page — SEO for Ecosystem Keywords

Write an integration landing page for a SaaS product, targeting the keyword "[Product] + [Integration partner, e.g., 'Salesforce integration']" or "[Product] [integration partner] connector."

Page structure:
- **H1**: "[Product] + [Partner] Integration" — straightforward, keyword-matched.
- **What this integration does (100 words)**: Describe the data flow or workflow the integration enables — from the user's operational perspective, not a technical spec. What can they now do that they couldn't before?
- **Key capabilities (3–5 bullets)**: Specific, actionable — each bullet is a use case, not a feature.
- **Setup section**: "How to connect [Product] and [Partner]" — 3-step summary. Confidence that it's easy to configure.
- **Use case examples (2)**: Two concrete workflow scenarios this integration unlocks for users.
- **CTA**: Start free trial or "Connect your [Partner] now."
- **Tone**: Technical confidence without overwhelming. The integration exists, it works, here's what it enables.

### 4. Content SEO Article — Problem-Aware Buyer Education

Write an educational SEO article targeting the keyword "[informational keyword, e.g., 'how to reduce customer churn SaaS']."

Article structure:
- **H1**: Natural keyword variant — framed as answering the question directly.
- **Intro (100 words)**: State the problem, validate the scale ("Churn is the #1 SaaS growth killer — and most teams discover it too late."), and preview the 3–4 specific actions this article covers.
- **Body (3–4 sections, 150 words each)**: Each section covers one actionable strategy or framework — concrete, specific, with one supporting data point or example per section.
- **Product integration**: In the most relevant section, integrate a natural mention of how the product addresses this challenge — not a pitch, a tool in the context of the strategy.
- **Summary + next step**: 80-word summary and CTA to a related resource or trial.

Tone: Practitioner-written, authority-building. Sounds like someone who runs a SaaS product, not a content marketer optimizing for keywords.
