# SaaS — Lifecycle Email Starter Prompts

> **Context:** SaaS lifecycle email is behavior-triggered and funnel-stage aware. The most impactful email sequences are: trial activation (first 24–72 hours), trial-to-paid conversion (before trial expiry), and post-upgrade expansion (30–60 days after first payment). Generic drip campaigns underperform behavior-triggered, milestone-based sequences. Prompts here focus on behavior-informed lifecycle sequences.

## Prompts

### 1. Trial Activation Sequence — First 72 Hours (3 Emails)

Write a 3-email trial activation sequence for a SaaS product. Goal: get the new trial user to their first meaningful product outcome within 72 hours.

**Email 1 (Day 0 — Signup confirmation + first action):**
- Subject: Confirm signup + frame the first win (not "Welcome to [Product]!")
- Body (100 words): Greet them, confirm trial is active, and give EXACTLY ONE action to take right now — the single action that leads to the product's core value fastest. Not a feature tour. One link, one action.
- Tone: Friend helping you get started, not an onboarding tutorial.

**Email 2 (Day 1 — First win or check-in):**
- Subject: If they've completed the Day 0 action → "Nice work — here's your next step." If they haven't → "Quick question: did you get a chance to [Day 0 action]?"
- Body (80 words): If activated: celebrate the progress and introduce the second activation milestone. If not: offer a 5-minute alternative or "start even simpler" path.
- Tone: Responsive, helpful. Never robotic.

**Email 3 (Day 3 — Activation outcome check):**
- Subject: Reference the goal they signed up to achieve (infer from trial source or onboarding question)
- Body (80–100 words): Ask if they've hit their first goal. Share a 2-sentence customer story from a user who started where they are and got to an outcome quickly. CTA: "Book 15 min with our team" OR "Watch a 3-min walkthrough."
- Tone: Genuinely curious about their progress, not a sales email dressed as support.

### 2. Trial-to-Paid Conversion Email — Expiry Approaching (3 Days Out)

Write an email sent to a trial user who is still free-tier with 3 days left in their trial.

Context: The user has logged in at least once but has not upgraded. They have completed [N] of the key activation actions.

Requirements:
- Subject: Acknowledge trial expiry without catastrophizing. Not "Your trial expires in 3 days!" — try: "What happens to your work when your trial ends?"
- Opening (50 words): State clearly what happens to their data or progress when the trial ends (factual, not scary). Address the most likely conversion blocker for someone at this activation depth.
- Middle (80–100 words): Frame the upgrade as unlocking what they've already built — not selling new features. One pricing context line (don't bury the cost).
- CTA: "Continue with [plan name]" → pricing page link.
- P.S.: "If the timing isn't right — you can export your data any time." (Reduces anxiety, paradoxically increases conversion.)

### 3. Post-Upgrade Onboarding Email — Day 14 (New Paid User)

Write a post-upgrade email sent to a newly paid user (subscription active for 14 days).

Goal: Surface the product's next-tier value to increase usage depth and reduce early churn.

Requirements:
- Subject: Reference what they've done in the product (behavioral signal) not a generic "You're 2 weeks in!"
- Opening (50 words): Acknowledge the specific actions they've taken in 14 days — or, if data isn't available, frame it as a "14-day check-in."
- Key value prompt (80 words): Introduce ONE capability they haven't used yet that's correlated with long-term retention for users with their profile. Frame it as "most [similar role] customers unlock this in week 2–3."
- CTA: One link to the feature + a help article or 2-minute video.
- Tone: CSM-adjacent — proactive, specific to them, not a blast email.

---

### 4. Expansion Email — Usage Milestone Triggered

Write a SaaS expansion email triggered when a user or team reaches a usage milestone that signals readiness for the next pricing tier or an add-on.

Context: [Milestone, e.g., "Team has reached 80% of their monthly seats limit" or "User has created 45/50 projects in their plan"].

Requirements:
- Subject: Acknowledge the milestone as a success signal, not a upsell trigger ("You're nearly at capacity — here's what that means")
- Opening (40 words): Name the milestone specifically. This is a good problem to have — frame it as growth.
- Middle (80 words): Explain what unlocks at the next tier. One sentence on value, not feature list. Transparent pricing line.
- CTA: "Upgrade your plan" → upgrade page link.
- Tone: Growth-framing, not urgency-framing. This is a capacity celebration, not a billing pressure point.
