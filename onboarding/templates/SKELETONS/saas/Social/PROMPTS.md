# SaaS — Social Media Starter Prompts

> **Context:** SaaS social content converts through product proof, customer wins, and transparency. Feature announcements that show value-in-action outperform feature announcements that describe capabilities. Twitter/X and LinkedIn are the primary platforms. Prompts here focus on product-led social content — showing the product doing real work for real users.

## Prompts

### 1. Feature Launch Post — LinkedIn / Twitter

Write a LinkedIn and Twitter/X post announcing a new product feature for a SaaS company.

Context: Feature being launched: [feature name and description]. Target users: [ICP]. Key value this adds: [specific outcome or workflow it enables].

Requirements (LinkedIn version — 150–200 words):
- Open with the user problem this feature solves — not "We're excited to announce."
- 2–3 sentences on what the feature does from the user's perspective (not a technical spec).
- One line on availability (which plan, when).
- Closing: What to do next — "Try it in your [Product] account" or "Read the full changelog at [link]."

Requirements (Twitter/X version — under 280 chars):
- Problem or outcome hook in the first sentence.
- Feature name + what it does in 12 words or fewer.
- CTA link.

Tone: Product-confident, user-first. This is an announcement that serves the user, not a press release.

---

### 2. Customer Win Post — Outcome + Process Fragment

Write a LinkedIn post sharing a customer's success result with a SaaS product.

Format:
- **Opening stat**: Lead with the quantified outcome metric ("One of our customers hit a 37% reduction in time-to-dispatch within 6 weeks.")
- **Context (2 sentences)**: Customer type (anonymized or named), what they were doing before, what changed.
- **Quote (1 sentence)**: From the customer — specific to the outcome, not a generic endorsement.
- **Mechanism (1 sentence)**: The specific product workflow or feature that enabled the outcome.
- **Closing**: Optional internal link to full case study or relevant resource.

Length: 120–180 words
Tone: Practitioner-to-practitioner. Feels like sharing a real result, not publishing a press release.

---

### 3. Product Demo Short — "Here's How We [Workflow]" Post

Write a LinkedIn or Twitter post structured around a 60-second product walkthrough video (GIF or short screen recording) of a specific workflow.

Format:
- **Post caption (100–120 words)**:
  - Open with the problem this workflow solves — the friction the ICP knows.
  - 2–3 sentences describing what's shown in the video (don't make them watch to know what they'll see).
  - "Watch time: ~60 seconds" — sets the expectation.
  - CTA: "Try it in your account at [link]" or "DM us if you want to see this for your use case."

- **Video title overlay (for the first frame)**: "[X] done in [time]" or "How [ICP] [achieves outcome] in [Product]."

Tone: Product-literate, efficient. This post respects the viewer's time by being specific about what they'll see and why it matters.

---

### 4. Social Proof Amplification — G2/Capterra Review

Write a LinkedIn or Twitter post featuring a recent G2, Capterra, or Trustpilot review, formatted to build trust without looking like an ad.

Requirements:
- The review excerpt is the hero: use a pull-quote format — the specific outcome or experience the reviewer named.
- Frame the review with 1–2 sentences of context ("We received this review last week from a [team type] using [Product] for [use case].")
- Attribute clearly: "[Reviewer name/title]" + review platform name (G2 or Capterra).
- Closing: A soft signal — "We read every review. [Link to our profile]" or "What would you add? We're listening."
- Visual: Review card with star rating, excerpt, and reviewer info.

Tone: Grateful acknowledgment, not self-promotion. The customer is the endorser; the brand is the medium.
