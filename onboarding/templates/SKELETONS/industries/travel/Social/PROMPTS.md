# Travel & Hospitality — Social Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Travel social media lives on aspiration and social proof. The primary driver of engagement is sensory-vivid content — imagery and video that makes the viewer feel the destination before they've arrived. UGC (user-generated content) is the highest-trust format in travel; guests sharing their own experiences outperform brand-produced content on almost every engagement metric. Seasonal urgency, event tie-ins, and "behind the scenes" content serve different audience stages — the best travel social programs mix all three.

## Prompts

### 1. UGC Aspiration Content Campaign

Write a UGC (user-generated content) collection and amplification strategy for a [hotel / resort / tour operator / destination], including 3 social post templates using guest-submitted content.

UGC strategy:
- **Collection mechanics:** In-property UGC prompt triggers — room key card with branded hashtag, welcome card in room or at check-in, WiFi password card with hashtag and handle; post-stay email UGC invitation (Email 1 in lifecycle sequence); reply to guest posts tagging the property with a brief thank-you and permission request for reposting
- **Permission and rights management:** Reply to guest post: "We love this — would you mind if we shared it on our feed? Full credit to you." Track permission grants in a simple log. Never repurpose without explicit approval.
- **Content selection criteria for amplification:** Authenticity signal (natural lighting, candid moments > staged shots), emotional resonance (experiences > amenities), visual quality threshold (minimum resolution for feed; lower threshold for Stories), diversity representation across guest types (couples, solo, families, groups if property serves all)

Post template 1 — Aspirational guest photo repost (Instagram / Facebook):
- Caption structure: Open with the sensory detail visible in the image (what the guest captured); follow with 2–3 sentences of experience context; close with a soft availability signal ("A few rooms still available for [month] — link in bio to check dates."); credit the original poster (@handle or "shot by our guest [name if shared]"); branded hashtag + destination hashtag

Post template 2 — Video UGC or Reel repost (Instagram Reels / TikTok):
- Add audio or music appropriate to the mood of the content; overlay minimal text (property name + location tag); caption: brief — 1–2 sentences at most; call out the guest by handle; invite viewers to share their own ("Your [destination] moment could be next — tag us with [#hashtag]")

Post template 3 — UGC testimonial story format (Instagram Stories):
- Swipe/slide 1: Guest quote from review in large readable text on property imagery background
- Slide 2: The property feature or experience the quote references — photo + 1-line context
- Slide 3: Booking CTA — "Check our [season] availability → link in bio" or "DM to ask about [dates]"

### 2. Testimonial Showcase Post

Write 3 social post formats for a [travel brand] showcasing guest reviews and testimonials.

**Format 1 — Long-form LinkedIn/Facebook testimonial post:**
- Open with the guest's quote (attributed by first name and origin if permitted, anonymous if not — "a guest from [city]")
- 2-paragraph body: What the guest experienced that led to this response; what makes this experience distinctive vs. similar properties or destinations
- Close with a booking call-to-action: "[Season] availability — link in bio / see comments"

**Format 2 — Star rating milestone announcement:**
- "[Property name] just crossed [N,000] reviews with a [X.X] star average."
- 3-sentence substance: The most common theme in reviews (cite the pattern honestly); one specific thing the team does that repeatedly gets mentioned; a genuine thank-you to guests
- Close: "Every review helps other travelers plan — thank you for sharing yours."

**Format 3 — Review platform trust signal post (Instagram Stories):**
- Slide 1: "[Rating] on [TripAdvisor / Google / Booking.com]. [Review count] guests." — clean design, property logo + platform badge
- Slide 2: Featured excerpt from a recent review (the most specific, emotionally resonant one — not the most generic)
- Slide 3: "See what guests are saying → [link to review platform profile]"

### 3. Seasonal Offer or Limited Availability Post

Write 3 social post formats for a [travel brand] promoting a seasonal offer, package, or inventory alert.

**Post 1 — Seasonal package launch (Instagram / Facebook):**
- Lead with the experience, not the price: "[Season] at [Property/Destination] — [experience description: e.g., 'wildflower meadows, empty trails, and the last warm evenings of the year.']"
- Package details follow: What's included (specific — not "complimentary amenities"), rate, dates, booking deadline
- Social proof signal: "[N] of last year's [season] guests are repeat visitors"
- CTA: "See the [Season] package → link in bio"

**Post 2 — Limited availability urgency post (Stories / Feed):**
- "[N] rooms remaining for [holiday period / event dates]."
- 2-sentence experience hook — what makes these specific dates worth booking
- CTA: "Check availability" → direct booking link
- Note: Use only when genuinely limited; manufactured scarcity is quickly identified by experienced travelers and erodes trust

**Post 3 — Early booking incentive post:**
- "Book [destination / property] for [season] before [date], and [incentive: complimentary breakfast / early check-in / discounted rate]."
- 3-sentence experience preview: What's best about [season] at this destination — sensory and specific
- CTA: "Early booking closes [date] — reserve your dates now"

### 4. Behind-the-Scenes and Team Content

Write 3 social post formats for a [hotel / resort / tour operator] showing the people, preparation, and detail behind the guest experience.

**Post 1 — Meet the team feature:**
- "[Name], [role] at [Property], has been [doing specific part of guest experience] for [N] years."
- 3-sentence human interest: The one detail or ritual they take personal pride in; what they want guests to notice and enjoy; something non-work about them (if comfortable sharing)
- Close: "The experience our guests describe starts here — with [Name] and the rest of our team."
- Tone: Genuine, not promotional. The person is the story.

**Post 2 — Preparation or craft process:**
- Capture a high-effort preparation moment: kitchen mise en place at 5am, room setup for an anniversary, trail maintenance after rainfall, the view from the top of the activity setup
- Caption structure: What's happening in the image (practical detail that readers don't see as guests); why it matters for the guest experience; who does this work and how long it takes
- Close: "The parts guests never see are often the parts that make the experience."

**Post 3 — "A day at [Property]" time-lapse or series:**
- Concept: 5-image or short video series capturing the property at different points in the day — 5am setup, breakfast service, midday leisure, sunset, dinner service
- Caption per image (or single caption for a carousel): 1 sentence per image, capturing the mood of that time of day
- Carousel close or video end card: "Every day here looks a little different. What will yours look like?" + booking CTA
