# Travel & Hospitality — Landing Pages Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Travel landing pages convert buyers who are oscillating between aspiration and anxiety — they want to go, but they haven't committed yet. The primary conversion drivers are: vivid experience imagery (show the guest living the experience, not the property features), social proof density (review count, star rating, traveler testimonials), and friction removal (clear pricing, flexible cancellation, visible availability). The booking widget or CTA must be visible without scrolling on mobile — mobile is where most travel research happens.

## Prompts

### 1. Destination / Property Landing Page

Write a destination or property landing page for a [hotel / resort / tour operator] targeting both organic search and paid acquisition traffic.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Property / Destination Name]: [Experiential Positioning — what the guest actually feels or does here]" — e.g., "Amalfi Cliffside Resort: Waking Up Above the Mediterranean" — not "[Property Name] | Official Website"
- **Above-fold section (must convert on mobile without scrolling):**
  - Hero image or video: Guest perspective, not property exterior — show the experience from inside the guest's vantage point
  - Social proof strip: "[Rating] stars · [N,000]+ reviews · [Platform(s)]" + one UGC photo or guest quote
  - Primary CTA: "Check Availability" with embedded date picker or link to booking engine — visible on mobile without scroll
  - Urgency signal (if honest): "[N] rooms left for your dates" or "Next-available [property type]: [date]"
- **Experience section (200 words):** 3–4 distinct experiences the guest will have — written in second person ("You'll wake to…", "Your afternoon starts with…"); sensory and specific; not a feature list
- **Accommodation overview:** Room/suite types with photos, brief descriptors, and "from $X/night" pricing — price transparency reduces bounce
- **Social proof section:** 6–9 guest reviews displayed — curated for specificity and emotional resonance, not just highest star rating; TripAdvisor or Google feed integration if available; aggregate rating displayed prominently
- **Practical confidence section (removes anxiety):** Flexible cancellation policy stated clearly; payment security signals; FAQ answering 5 most common booking questions (What's included? Is breakfast included? What's the airport transfer option? What's the cancellation policy? Is parking available?)
- **Secondary CTA:** "Browse All Rooms" → accommodation listing page; "See Our Packages" → offers page

### 2. Promotional Package or Limited-Time Offer Page

Write a promotional landing page for a [travel brand] promoting a seasonal package, bundle deal, or limited availability offer.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Season/Occasion] at [Destination]: [What Makes This Package Worth It]" — e.g., "Summer in Santorini: Sunset Suite Package with Daily Breakfast and Sunset Cruise Included"
- **Above fold:**
  - Package hero image: The experience being sold, not the components
  - Package summary strip: Dates, inclusions, rate, and savings (if applicable — show "Value: $[Y] | Package price: $[X]")
  - Primary CTA: "Book This Package" → booking page with package pre-selected; deadline if applicable: "Available through [date]"
- **What's included (specific, visual):** Itemized list with brief context for each inclusion — why this inclusion matters for this experience specifically; images for key inclusions (room, restaurant, activity); avoid vague "complimentary amenities" language
- **Why book now section:** Urgency without manipulation — limited room block, seasonal availability, early booking benefit, or specific event tie-in that makes timing matter; never invent scarcity
- **Guest testimonials:** 3 testimonials from guests who took a similar package — specific to the types of inclusions or experience moments offered
- **Package FAQs:** 5 common package questions answered: What if I want to change dates? Can inclusions be swapped? Is the package bookable direct only? What's the cancellation policy with a package? Can the package be purchased as a gift?
- **Secondary packages:** 3 alternative packages or room types at different price points — prevents abandonment for buyers who want this destination but not this exact package

### 3. Group Booking or Event Inquiry Page

Write a group booking or event inquiry landing page for a [hotel / resort / venue] targeting corporate events, weddings, group travel, or special celebrations.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "Group Bookings and Private Events at [Property Name]: [What Makes This Property Right for Groups]"
- **Above fold:** Group-focused hero image (table setting for a dinner, outdoor event space, group activity); clear group booking CTA: "Get a Group Quote" or "Enquire About Your Event" → inquiry form
- **Group capabilities overview (3–4 sections, 80 words each):**
  - Corporate events: Meeting room capacity, AV capabilities, catering options, team-building activities available on property; what makes this property suited to corporate retreats
  - Celebrations (weddings / milestones): What events the property hosts; capacity; exclusive hire options if available; catering and beverage packages; a brief testimonial from a celebration event host
  - Group travel (travel trade / tour groups): Minimum group size, group rate structure, rooming list management, group transport coordination
  - Bespoke packages: What can be tailored for groups that isn't available on standard booking
- **Inquiry form (primary conversion):** Group type (dropdown: corporate event, wedding, milestone celebration, tour group/travel trade, other); approximate dates; number of guests; budget range (optional but useful for routing); specific requirements (freetext); preferred contact method
- **Trust signals for groups:** Named events or clients (if client permits public mention), industry accreditation (if applicable), group coordinator contact information (named person, not "our team")
- **Process transparency:** What happens after inquiry — timeline for response, what the site inspection or proposal process looks like, when pricing is confirmed

### 4. Loyalty or Membership Sign-Up Page

Write a loyalty or direct booking membership sign-up landing page for a [hotel brand / travel operator], converting OTA bookings or first-time guests into direct, repeat bookers.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Program Name]: The Benefits of Booking Direct with [Brand]"
- **Above fold:** Lead with the primary member benefit (the most financially meaningful one — member rate, complimentary night, or upgrade priority); direct comparison with OTA booking: "Book directly and you'll receive [X] that you won't get on [OTA Name]"; primary CTA: "Join Free — [Benefit in CTA Text]" → membership enrollment form
- **Benefits list (clear, specific, no vague language):**
  - Rate benefit: "Members save [X]% on standard rates" — state the actual savings or minimum percentage guarantee
  - Service benefit: "Early check-in and late check-out subject to availability — requested automatically with every booking"
  - Points or credit program (if applicable): Earn rate, redemption options, and an example ("After 5 stays of [X] nights, earn a complimentary night")
  - Experience upgrade: "Room upgrade requested automatically on every member booking — granted based on availability at check-in"
  - Communications: "Members receive destination guides, exclusive events access, and seasonal offers — no third-party marketing"
- **Enrollment form (friction-minimal):** Name, email, country of residence; optional: home travel preferences (adventure, leisure, business, wellness); immediate benefit on enrollment (a welcome offer triggered by form submission)
- **Comparison table:** Direct member booking vs. OTA booking — rate, cancellation flexibility, inclusions, loyalty credit; make the value difference legible in a single table scan
- **FAQ:** 3–5 enrollment and program questions: Is there a fee? How quickly do benefits apply? Can I apply points to existing bookings? What happens to my points if I cancel?
