# Travel & Hospitality — Lifecycle & Email Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Travel lifecycle email maps to the emotional arc of a trip — from booking excitement to pre-travel anticipation to post-experience nostalgia. Each phase has a distinct communication register and job to do. Post-booking sequences reduce cancellations and increase pre-trip spend. Pre-travel sequences build anticipation and surface upsell. Post-trip sequences capture reviews and convert one-time guests into loyalists. The best travel email programs feel personal and experiential, not operational.

## Prompts

### 1. Post-Booking Confirmation and Anticipation Sequence

Write a 4-email post-booking sequence for a [hotel / resort / tour operator / travel package], starting immediately after a booking is confirmed.

Email 1 — Booking confirmation + first anticipation signal (send: immediately after booking):
- Subject: "You're going to [destination]. Here's everything confirmed."
- Body: Full booking summary (dates, accommodation, included items, total paid); brief warm celebration of the booking — made aspirational/experiential, not transactional ("In [X] weeks you'll be…"); link to destination prep guide or guest hub; single CTA: "Start planning your experience" → destination content hub or experience booking page. Include a clear cancellation/change policy summary — reduces post-booking anxiety.

Email 2 — Destination inspiration and experience surfacing (send: 3–5 days after booking):
- Subject: "What [N,000] guests love most about [destination / property]"
- Body: 3 curated experience recommendations for their destination or property — not a generic top-10 list; framed as "what past guests didn't want to miss" + 1 social proof quote per experience; each experience includes practical booking guidance (book ahead, or walk-in at X — specific); low-friction CTA: "Add to your itinerary" → experience/activity booking page

Email 3 — Upsell + package enhancement (send: 2–3 weeks before arrival):
- Subject: "Enhance your stay — a few options worth considering"
- Body: 2–3 specific upsell options relevant to their booking type (e.g., room upgrade if available, dining package, airport transfer, activity add-on); framed as enhancements, not add-ons: "These are the things guests most often wish they'd arranged in advance"; price transparency for each; single CTA per option; note: offer room upgrade only if genuinely available and priced simply

Email 4 — Pre-arrival practical preparation (send: 5–7 days before arrival):
- Subject: "Your [destination] arrival guide — everything you need for [arrival date]"
- Body: Arrival day logistics (check-in time, transfer options, parking, early check-in process if available); packing tip specific to the season; 1 local tip that makes arrival easier ("The best spot for your first evening is [X] — no booking needed before 7pm"); primary CTA: "View your full arrival guide" → guest hub

### 2. Pre-Travel Preparation and Excitement Series

Write a 3-email pre-travel preparation series for a [travel brand] starting 2 weeks before departure.

Email 1 — Two weeks out: final planning essentials (send: 14 days before departure):
- Subject: "Two weeks to go: [N] things to arrange before [destination]"
- Body: Practical checklist focused on high-forgetting items specific to this destination — travel insurance (if not already arranged), local SIM or international data plan, currency, required vaccinations or entry requirements (with link to official source); experience/activity booking call-out ("These activities book out — confirm your visit plan now"); property-specific preparation note (parking, check-in timing, dietary requirements if restaurant included)

Email 2 — One week out: packing and logistics (send: 7 days before departure):
- Subject: "One week until [destination] — packing guide + your arrival details"
- Body: Weather-appropriate packing checklist for the destination and travel dates; re-confirmation of key logistics (check-in time, address, transfer status if applicable); link to destination guide for last-minute reading; 1 practical local insight appropriate for their travel style; no upsell in this email — it's pure value

Email 3 — Day before departure: recap and excitement (send: 1 day before departure):
- Subject: "Tomorrow's the day. Everything you need for [destination]."
- Body: Ultra-practical: arrival address + how to get there; check-in time + early check-in option if available; emergency contact number for the property; today's weather at destination for context; 1-sentence anticipation-building close — experiential, not operational. No CTAs. This email should feel like a thoughtful confirmation from a trusted travel companion.

### 3. Post-Trip Review Request and Loyalty Conversion

Write a 3-email post-stay sequence for a [hotel / resort / travel experience] targeting recent guests within 30 days of checkout.

Email 1 — Post-stay thank you + review invitation (send: 24–48 hours after checkout):
- Subject: "Thank you for staying at [Property]. We'd love to hear how it went."
- Body: Warm, specific thank-you — reference something about their stay if data allows (e.g., dates, type of room, or season); genuine invitation to share their experience via the primary review platform (TripAdvisor, Google, or Booking.com — link directly to the review submission page, do not link to a general profile); acknowledge that leaving a review is a favor, not an obligation: "It takes about 2 minutes and helps other travelers planning a similar trip."; if the stay had a service issue (if this data is available), escalate directly to recovery protocol — do not send a standard review request

Email 2 — Loyalty or future booking offer (send: 5–7 days after checkout):
- Subject: "A returning guest offer — available through [date]"
- Body: Frame as a recognition of their stay; offer a specific return booking incentive (returning guest rate, complimentary upgrade, included amenity for next booking — not a generic discount); include the offer validity window; CTA: "See your returning guest offer" → booking page with promo code or member rate applied; note: if they already reviewed: acknowledge the review ("Thanks for sharing your experience on TripAdvisor — it genuinely helps other guests plan their trips.")

Email 3 — Loyalty program enrollment or anniversary (send: 14–21 days after checkout, OR on anniversary date of stay):
- Subject: "Your [Property/Brand] guest profile — worth a minute to set up"
- Body: Introduce the loyalty or guest profile program (if applicable); specific benefits for repeat guests (priority booking, member rates, complimentary anniversary night, preference storage); frictionless enrollment: one-click pre-fill with known data if possible; if no formal loyalty program: frame as a guest preference profile that makes future stays more personalized; CTA: "Set up your guest profile"

### 4. Lapsed Traveler Win-Back Campaign

Write a 3-email win-back series for a [travel brand] targeting past guests who have not returned within 12–24 months.

Email 1 — "We miss you" + what's changed (send: day 1 of campaign):
- Subject: "It's been a while. Here's what's new at [Property / Destination]."
- Body: Acknowledge the time gap without dwelling on it — a single warm sentence; then pivot to what has genuinely changed: new experiences, renovated spaces, new offerings, or seasonal highlights not available on their last visit; 2–3 specific updates with imagery or experiential descriptions; low-commitment CTA: "Take a look at what's new" → refreshed destination or property page

Email 2 — Win-back incentive (send: 7 days after Email 1 if no booking action):
- Subject: "An offer for past guests — available until [date]"
- Body: Lapsed guest-exclusive offer — be specific (e.g., "20% off your next stay in [season]," "complimentary room upgrade on bookings of 3+ nights," "return guest rate: $[X]/night through [date]"); framed as recognizing their past stay, not as a desperation discount; offer must have a genuine expiry date; clear booking CTA with offer code or pre-applied member rate

Email 3 — Final touchpoint (send: 14 days after Email 2 if no booking action):
- Subject: "Last chance: your returning guest offer expires [date]"
- Body: Brief — 3 sentences maximum; recap the offer; include the expiry date; single CTA to book; no guilt language, no re-listing of property features; if no response, remove from active win-back campaign and move to low-frequency seasonal newsletter
