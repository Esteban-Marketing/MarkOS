# Travel & Hospitality — Content & SEO Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Travel content SEO targets buyers in two very different intents: destination inspiration (early, emotional, unstructured) and trip planning (late, practical, high-intent). The highest-performing travel content matches the buyer's planning stage — aspirational narrative for discovery, structured practical guides for planning. Review content and UGC amplification are the primary off-page SEO levers in travel; building review volume and responding to reviews increases organic visibility and conversion rates simultaneously.

## Prompts

### 1. Destination Guide (Pillar Content for SEO)

Write a destination travel guide for [destination / travel product] targeting the keyword "[destination] travel guide" or "things to do in [destination]."

Guide structure:
- **H1:** "The Complete [Destination] Travel Guide: [Year] — What to See, Do, and Experience"
- **Intro (150 words):** Sensory and experiential lead — open with a scene or experience that captures the destination's essential character; avoid "Located in…" or geographic framing; establish who the destination is best suited for (solo, couples, families, adventure, luxury — be specific); end intro with a "what makes this guide different" statement
- **Essential experiences section (5–8 items, 50 words each):** For each: experience name, what it is, why it matters for this destination specifically, when to go (if time-sensitive), and practical logistics note (how to get there, booking required or walk-in)
- **Practical planning section:**
  - Best time to visit: Month-by-month breakdown — weather, crowd levels, pricing, what's happening (events, seasonal attractions)
  - How to get there: Primary transport options with honest tradeoffs
  - How to get around: Ground transport options with practical guidance
  - Where to base yourself: 2–3 area options with honest characterization (e.g., "Central district: walkable, noisy, best for nightlife access")
- **Insider tips section (5 tips):** Non-obvious advice that rewards the reader — local knowledge, common tourist mistakes to avoid, hidden alternatives to overcrowded spots
- **SEO structure:** H2s for each major section; internal links to property or booking pages where relevant; schema markup (FAQPage for common destination questions)

### 2. Seasonal Travel Article ("Best Time to Visit" / "[Month] in [Destination]")

Write a seasonal travel article for a [destination / travel product] targeting keywords like "best time to visit [destination]" or "[month] travel [destination]."

Article structure:
- **H1:** "Best Time to Visit [Destination]: Month-by-Month Guide to [Weather, Crowds, and Experiences]"
- **Intro (100 words):** Honest lead — acknowledge that the "best" time depends on what the traveler is optimizing for (weather, cost, crowds, specific events); brief orientation to the destination's annual rhythm
- **Month-by-month breakdown (12 sections, 80 words each):** For each month: weather summary (temperature range, precipitation probability), crowd level (low/medium/high relative to annual average), pricing level (budget, shoulder, peak), what's happening (seasonal events, natural phenomena, local festivals), best suited for (type of traveler)
- **"Best for…" quick-reference grid:**
  - Best for weather: [months]
  - Best for value: [months]
  - Best for avoiding crowds: [months]
  - Best for families: [months]
  - Best for a specific experience (scuba, ski, cultural festivals): [months/seasons]
- **Top seasonal events section:** 5–8 named events or seasonal phenomena with dates, what they are, and why they're worth timing a trip around
- **Booking timing guidance:** How far in advance to book for each season — practical framing: "For peak December travel, book accommodation 3–6 months ahead; for shoulder season, 4–8 weeks is typically adequate."

### 3. Destination Comparison Article ("[Destination A] vs [Destination B]")

Write a destination comparison article for a [travel brand / OTA / travel agency] targeting the keyword "[Destination A] vs [Destination B]" or "choosing between [Destination A] and [Destination B]."

Article structure:
- **H1:** "[Destination A] vs [Destination B]: Which Is Right for Your Trip?"
- **Intro (100 words):** Frame the decision genuinely — both destinations have merit; the right choice depends on what the traveler is optimizing for; establish the comparison criteria upfront so the reader knows what they'll get
- **Quick verdict grid (above-the-fold table):**
  | | [Destination A] | [Destination B] |
  |---|---|---|
  | Best for | [traveler type] | [traveler type] |
  | Value for money | [rating] | [rating] |
  | Best season | [months] | [months] |
  | Crowd factor | [low/med/high] | [low/med/high] |
  | Getting there | [transport] | [transport] |
- **Head-to-head comparison sections (5–7 comparison dimensions, 100 words each):**
  - Landscape and experiences: What each offers vs. what the other lacks; who cares about this difference
  - Climate and best seasons: Where each wins across different travel goals
  - Budget and value: Accommodation and food cost comparison; which is better value at what budget level
  - Ease of travel: How easy each is to navigate, entry requirements, transport quality
  - Unique-to-each experiences: What you can do in A that you can't in B, and vice versa
- **"Choose [Destination A] if…" / "Choose [Destination B] if…" decision section** — give a clear recommendation per traveler type; do not hedge into "both are great"

### 4. Post-Booking Destination Content Hub

Write a post-booking content and email resource hub strategy for a [hotel / resort / tour operator], delivering practical destination preparation content to confirmed guests.

Hub strategy and content structure:
- **Purpose:** Reduce anxiety, build anticipation, and reduce service load on the property — travelers who arrive well-prepared ask fewer questions, engage more with local experiences, and generate more positive reviews
- **Hub URL structure:** `/[property-slug]/guest-guide/` — gated but friction-low: accessible with booking confirmation number; sends a welcome email with hub link automatically on booking confirmation
- **Content sections (each: 300–500 words):**
  - Arrival guide: How to get to the property from primary airports/transport hubs; transfer options with honest tradeoff (cost vs. time vs. independence); check-in time + early check-in process; what to bring that the property does not supply
  - Local area guide: Restaurant recommendations within 15-minute walk/drive — 5 options across 3 price points; nearest supermarket/convenience; pharmacy; ATM. Avoid generic TripAdvisor top-10; curate a genuine local list with specific practical notes
  - Activities and experiences calendar: What's happening during their specific stay dates (if personalization is possible); how to book activities (in-advance recommended or walk-in available); property-exclusive vs. independent booking
  - Packing guide: Weather forecast for their stay dates (if dynamic content is possible) or seasonal packing recommendations; property-specific items to bring or leave home
  - Local currency and payment: What's accepted locally, approximate conversion, where to exchange; typical tipping norms
- **SEO value:** Hub pages rank for "[property name] guide," "[destination] travel tips," "[destination] what to pack" — generating pre-arrival organic traffic that builds brand familiarity before the paid acquisition decision
