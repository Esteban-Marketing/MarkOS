# Travel & Hospitality — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Travel paid media must balance aspiration with conversion — imagery and copy that trigger desire, landing pages that remove booking friction. Seasonal demand peaks create bidding pressure where efficiency matters; off-peak periods require demand-generation positioning, not just conversion optimization. Review count and star rating are trust anchors for travel buyers: use them in ad copy wherever platform policies allow.

## Prompts

### 1. Destination Search Campaign (Google Search + Shopping)

Write a Google Search campaign brief and ad copy for a [hotel / resort / tour operator / OTA listing] targeting travelers actively searching for [destination] or [travel category] keywords.

Campaign structure:
- **Keyword strategy:** 3 keyword tiers — (1) High-intent booking keywords: "[destination] hotel," "[destination] resort," "book [destination] vacation," "[destination] all-inclusive" — bid aggressively; (2) Destination research keywords: "[destination] travel guide," "things to do in [destination]," "[destination] best time to visit" — bid for consideration ads with stronger content offer; (3) Competitor / alternative destination keywords: "[competing destination] vs [destination]" — bid with comparison angle
- **Ad copy (3 RSA headlines + 2 descriptions):**
  - Headline 1: Destination + primary differentiator ("[Destination] [Property Type] | [Differentiator: Beachfront / Award-Winning / Adult-Only]")
  - Headline 2: Social proof anchor ("[N,000]+ Guests · [Rating] Stars · [Review Count] Reviews")
  - Headline 3: Booking incentive ("[X]% Off Early Booking" / "Free Breakfast Included" / "Flexible Cancellation")
  - Description 1: Sensory-vivid experience lead + offer ("[Experience description]. Book direct for [exclusive benefit]. Rate from $[X]/night.")
  - Description 2: Trust signal + urgency ("[N] rooms available this [season]. Check your dates — live availability.")
- **Ad extensions:**
  - Sitelinks: Book Now (landing on booking widget), Rooms & Suites, Dining, Local Experiences, Special Offers
  - Callout extensions: Flexible cancellation, Free WiFi, Best price guarantee, Award/certification
  - Structured snippets (Amenities): Pool, Spa, Beachfront, Restaurant, Fitness Center
  - Price extension: Room category + rate (if dynamic feed available)
- **Quality score considerations:** Ad relevance, expected CTR, landing page experience — landing page must load in < 2 seconds with a visible booking widget on mobile

### 2. Seasonal Bidding Strategy and Campaign Adjustment Framework

Write a paid media seasonal bidding and campaign adjustment plan for a [travel brand / hospitality property] managing demand volatility across peak, shoulder, and off-peak periods.

Seasonal campaign framework:
- **Peak season (12–16 weeks pre-departure peak):** Objective shifts to conversion efficiency — maximize ROAS on high-intent keywords; reduce brand-awareness spend; increase bid adjustments for mobile (travelers often book on mobile during inspiration moments); activate price extensions if inventory is limited; add urgency messaging ("Limited availability — [month] booking window closing")
- **Shoulder season (8–12 weeks out):** Objective shifts to demand stimulation — keep awareness campaigns active with experience-first messaging; introduce package deals or bundled offers that increase perceived value without rate discounting; retargeting becomes high-priority (previous website visitors who didn't book)
- **Off-peak creation:** Create demand rather than capture it — campaigns focus on "Why [destination] in [off-peak month]?" + activities/experiences unique to that season; "hidden gem" and "escape the crowds" positioning; price-forward only if rate is genuinely compelling
- **Budget allocation rules by season:**
  - Peak: 50% brand/booking, 30% retargeting, 20% prospecting
  - Shoulder: 35% brand/booking, 35% retargeting, 30% prospecting
  - Off-peak: 20% brand/booking, 20% retargeting, 60% prospecting + content distribution
- **Bid modifier guidance:** Device (mobile +15–30% in peak), Daypart (Friday evening +20% — planning intent), Geographic (origin markets with highest booking conversion +15%), Audience (in-market for travel +25%)

### 3. Retargeting Campaign (Abandoned Research and Cart Recovery)

Write a retargeting campaign structure for a [hotel / resort / tour operator] recovering travelers who visited the property or product page but did not complete a booking.

Retargeting audience segments and message:
- **Segment A — Homepage or destination page visitors (no dates searched):** Message: Aspiration reinforcement + ease entry. Creative: destination lifestyle imagery + short copy ("Still dreaming about [destination]? Here's what [N,000] guests said about staying here.") CTA: "Start planning" → landing page with a flexible search widget, not a booking form
- **Segment B — Date search completed, booking not started:** Message: Availability urgency + proof. Creative: "[N] rooms left for your dates" (if accurate) + 2-sentence social proof excerpt + star rating. CTA: "Check your availability" → direct to the search results page with their dates pre-populated (if available via URL parameter)
- **Segment C — Booking started, not completed (cart abandonment):** Message: Friction removal + incentive if warranted. Creative: Dynamic ad showing the specific room/package they viewed + "[Offer: free upgrade / free breakfast / flexible cancellation]" as incentive. CTA: "Complete your booking" → restored booking session. Note: Do not offer a discount as the first lever — a service-value incentive (upgrade, included amenity) has lower margin impact
- **Segment D — Past bookers (repeat booking prompt):** Message: Loyalty and preference recognition. Creative: "Welcome back, [first name]" + seasonal or experience-specific offer. CTA: "Book your [season/year] visit" → member rate or returning guest offer page
- **Platform guidance:** Google Display + Meta for Segments A and B; Dynamic Product Ads (Meta) for Segment C; Email retargeting + Meta Custom Audiences for Segment D. Frequency cap: Segments A–B max 4 impressions/day; Segment C max 6/day for 5 days then reduce to 2.

### 4. Meta-Search and OTA Bidding Strategy

Write a meta-search and OTA channel paid media strategy for a [hotel / resort / hospitality brand] managing distribution across Google Hotel Ads, TripAdvisor, Kayak, and Trivago.

Meta-search strategy:
- **Google Hotel Ads:**
  - Integration: Connect property management system (PMS) or channel manager to Google Hotel Center via a certified connectivity partner; ensure rate parity compliance (match or beat OTA best available rate for direct booking to win the "Book via Google" placement)
  - Bid strategy: Commission-per-stay (CPS) vs. cost-per-click (CPC) — CPS recommended for smaller properties (only pay on confirmed checkout); CPC more efficient for high-traffic, high-conversion properties
  - Direct booking incentive: Display an exclusive direct benefit in the Hotel Ads panel ("Book Direct: Free Room Upgrade + Flexible Cancellation") — must be a genuine offer not available via OTA
  - Recovery from OTA displacement: If OTA rates appear lower in meta-search due to member pricing, use a "Direct Price Match Guarantee" call-to-action in the property panel description
- **TripAdvisor / Trivago / Kayak:**
  - These platforms are strong for upper-funnel travel research; less conversion-efficient than Google Hotel Ads; use primarily on high-value segments (luxury, all-inclusive, unique experiences)
  - Sponsored Placement (TripAdvisor): Bid for top-3 placement in destination category + manage reviews actively (respond within 24 hours; response rate and sentiment affect algorithm placement)
- **OTA management (Booking.com, Expedia, Airbnb):**
  - Use OTAs for demand fill in off-peak; do not compete with direct booking at peak
  - Rate parity audit: Run weekly check to ensure direct rate is always competitive vs. OTA display rate
  - Billboard effect strategy: OTA traffic often results in direct brand searches before booking — maintain active Google Brand campaign to capture this intent at lower CPC than OTA commission
