# Professional Services — Content & SEO Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Professional services content must first establish credentialing — this buyer is evaluating a firm before they read anything else. SEO in this vertical competes on methodology, practitioner authority, and specific problem framing, not traffic volume. A single high-credibility piece of content — a named methodology page, a regulatory analysis by a named practitioner, a rigorous case study — outperforms a high-volume blog content calendar. Formal tone is expected at awareness; it signals competence, not stiffness.

## Prompts

### 1. Practice Area / Methodology SEO Page

Write a practice area landing page and SEO content piece for a [professional services / consulting / advisory firm] targeting buyers searching for [practice area or methodology] expertise.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Practice Area / Methodology Name]: [Outcome-Oriented Framing for Target Buyer]" — e.g., "Organisational Restructuring Advisory: From Strategic Decision to Operational Stability"
- **Practitioner credential lead (100 words):** Name the discipline, name the methodology, and reference 1–2 specific practitioner backgrounds that give the firm authority to practice in this area — "Our [practice] team brings [N] years of practitioner experience across [specific engagement types], including engagements at [named or anonymised reference companies]"
- **The challenge in client language (150 words):** Describe the business problem this practice area solves — in the language of the buyer's internal conversations, not consulting jargon; include what typically goes wrong when firms attempt this without specialist support
- **Methodology overview (200 words):** Named framework or approach — 4–5 phases or components; what happens in each; what the firm's specific intellectual contribution is (not generic process)
- **Outcomes (100 words):** 3–4 specific measurable outcomes from client engagements — anonymised or attributed; include leading and lagging indicators where possible
- **Related content links:** 2–3 linked pieces (case study, thought leadership article, related practice area)
- **CTA:** "See if this practice area applies to your situation" → consultation request

### 2. Industry Trend / Regulatory Update Credentialing Content

Write a thought leadership article for a [management consulting / advisory / professional services firm] on a major trend or regulatory development affecting [target sector or buyer function].

Format: Long-form analysis piece (1,200–1,800 words), formal register, practitioner-authored.

Structure:
- **Byline and credentialing:** Named practitioner — title, practice area, years of relevant experience; firm affiliation
- **Executive summary (150 words):** The development, why it matters, and the 2–3 strategic implications for [target function / sector] leaders — sufficient context to justify reading the full piece
- **Background and context (300 words):** What changed, why now, and the scope of impact — cite regulatory body or market data source; note: do not quote specific data without a cited source; use "[insert regulatory body / market data]" as placeholder where data not available
- **Strategic implications section (3 sections, 200 words each):** Each implication from the executive summary unpacked — what it means for [function or role], what the typical wrong response is, and what the right response looks like
- **Practitioner commentary:** A brief first-person paragraph from the bylined practitioner — what they have observed in client engagements on this topic; 1 anonymised client example where applicable
- **What to do next (100 words):** 3 concrete actions for the reader before their next planning cycle
- **Firm CTA:** Offer a briefing or diagnostic conversation for readers facing this challenge

### 3. Client Success Case Study (SEO Format)

Write a client success case study for a [professional services firm] structured for both organic search and direct proposal use.

Deliverables:
- **SEO title and meta description:** Keyword-optimised for [practice area + outcome type + sector] searches; title should describe the outcome, not the firm
- **H1:** "How [anonymised: 'a global logistics group'] [Achieved Outcome] in [Timeframe] with [Practice Area]"
- **Above-fold summary:** 3–4 key outcomes in metric/stat format; 1 framing sentence; "View full case study" anchor to body
- **Client context (100 words):** Company type, scale, operating context; the event or condition that brought this engagement to the table
- **The challenge (150 words):** Specific, detailed description of the problem; include what the client had already attempted internally and why it hadn't worked; note what was at risk if unresolved
- **Our approach (200 words):** Step-by-step methodology — named phases or framework; what the firm did that a generalist or internal team could not have done; team composition (practitioner seniority note, not just "our team")
- **Outcomes (100 words):** Primary and secondary metrics; what changed for the client's team and business; timeline from engagement start to measurable outcome
- **Client testimonial:** Direct quote from named or anonymised senior sponsor; reference the outcome, the working relationship, and the firm's approach — not generic praise
- **Schema markup guidance:** Include breadcrumb, article, and FAQ schema placeholder notes for implementation team

### 4. Buyer's Guide: How to Evaluate a Professional Services Firm

Write a buyer's guide or evaluation framework document for [C-suite or senior function leaders] who are selecting a professional services or consulting firm for a [type of engagement].

Format: Long-form guide (1,500–2,000 words), formal register, structured for download or in-page reading.

Structure:
- **Introduction (150 words):** Acknowledge that evaluating consulting/advisory firms is not straightforward — credentials are not standardised, pricing is opaque, and outcomes are hard to compare; state that this guide provides a structured framework for the selection process
- **Section 1 — Define the engagement type (200 words):** 4–5 different engagement types (diagnostic, strategy, implementation, change management, ongoing advisory); which type fits the buyer's current situation; why misclassifying the engagement type leads to the wrong vendor selection
- **Section 2 — Credential and team composition questions (200 words):** 6–8 questions to ask every firm in evaluation; what a strong vs. weak answer looks like for each; include "Who will actually be on our account?" and "What is your escalation path if the lead practitioner is unavailable?"
- **Section 3 — Methodology and IP questions (200 words):** 5 questions about the firm's named methodology or framework; why methodology matters beyond process; red flags to watch for (e.g., no named framework, "we tailor to each client" as deflection from lack of IP)
- **Section 4 — Reference and referral validation (150 words):** How to structure a reference call with a previous client; 5 specific questions to ask; why peer referrals are more reliable than firm-provided references
- **Section 5 — Engagement model and pricing (200 words):** What to expect in a transparent pricing conversation; scope definition questions; how to identify a firm that understands its own cost structure vs. one that will scope-creep
- **Closing CTA:** Offer a consultation conversation where the firm walks through how they would answer these questions — no pressure, just transparency
