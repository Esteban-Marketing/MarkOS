# Professional Services — Landing Pages Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Professional services landing pages face a specific credibility challenge: the buyer is evaluating whether the people they would work with are genuinely expert enough to be trusted with a significant, complex, and often sensitive engagement. Generic "we deliver results" positioning fails completely in this vertical. What converts is practitioner specificity — named methodologies, named practitioners, specific engagement types, and transparent scope and pricing. Pages that feel like marketing copy convert worse than pages that feel like practitioner-written documentation.

## Prompts

### 1. Practice Area / Expertise Overview Page

Write the primary practice area landing page for a [management consulting / strategy advisory / professional services firm] for [practice area — e.g., operating model redesign, post-merger integration, go-to-market strategy].

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Practice Area]: [Outcome-Oriented Framing]" — e.g., "Post-Merger Integration Advisory: Operational Stability and Cultural Continuity From Day One"
- **Practitioner authority lead (above fold, 100 words):** Name the specific expertise the firm brings; reference named practitioners or recognised methodology directly; 1 credentialing stat or anchor ("Our integration team has supported [N] transactions across [N] sectors in the last [N] years")
- **The challenge this practice solves (150 words):** Describe in the language of the senior sponsor who owns this problem; include what happens when this challenge is managed poorly — specific operational or financial consequences; this section should make the reader feel recognised, not educated
- **Our methodology (200 words):** Named framework or approach — 4–5 phases or principles; what distinguishes this from generic approaches; what the client's team does vs. what the firm's team does (role clarity builds trust in PS)
- **Client outcomes section:** 3–4 anonymised outcome references — specific metrics or deliverables; ideally includes outcomes at different company types or scales
- **Team section:** Named practitioners who lead this practice — photo, 2-sentence bio focused on practice depth, not career history; "Who you'd work with" heading
- **CTA:** "Request an initial conversation" or "Send us your challenge" → consultation form; note that the first call is diagnostic, not a pitch

### 2. Team and Credentials Page

Write the team / about us / credentials page for a [professional services / consulting / advisory firm] that convinces a senior buyer the people they would work with are worth their trust.

Page structure:
- **Lead (100 words):** A brief statement of the firm's founding principle or practice philosophy — 3–4 sentences on why the firm was established and what it deliberately does differently from larger generalist firms; written in practitioner voice, not marketing copy
- **The team section (for each listed practitioner, 100–150 words):**
  - Name, title, practice specialism
  - Practitioner background: specific prior roles, organisations, or engagements that give them authority in this practice (not a CV list — a focused narrative of relevant credentials)
  - Methodology contribution: what named framework, approach, or body of work they have contributed to; link to any published work
  - Current focus: what types of engagements they lead now; types of organisations they typically work with
- **Credentials and affiliations section:** Certifications, recognised frameworks, academic affiliations, regulatory body memberships — brief list with 1-sentence relevance note per item
- **Named references (if available):** 2–3 named client references who are willing to be contacted — with role, company type, and engagement context; this section converts better than any testimonial in professional services
- **CTA:** "Meet with a practitioner" → 30-minute introductory call offer; no form — direct calendar or email link

### 3. Consultation Request / Initial Contact Page

Write a consultation request or initial contact landing page for a [professional services firm] that reduces friction and pre-qualifies the engagement before the first call.

Page design principle: This page should feel like the beginning of a professional engagement, not a sales form. The buyer is being invited to describe their challenge to a peer, not to submit a lead form to a vendor.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "Tell Us What You're Navigating" or "Request an Initial Conversation"
- **Lead (80 words):** Brief description of what happens in the first conversation — "This is a 30-minute call. [Practitioner name or team] will spend 20 minutes understanding your situation and 10 minutes offering an initial view on how firms like ours typically approach it. There is no proposal on the call unless you want one."
- **What to tell us (short form, 5 fields maximum):**
  - Your name and organisation
  - Your role
  - Brief description of the challenge or question (free text, 100–200 words — not a dropdown)
  - Preferred format: call / in-person / written response first
  - How you found us (referral / search / event / content)
- **What happens next:** 3-step process: (1) We review your note within [N] business days; (2) We confirm whether this is the right type of engagement for us; (3) If yes, we'll confirm a call time; if not, we'll tell you who might be better suited
- **Credibility reinforcer:** 1 brief testimonial from a previous client about what the first engagement experience is like — not about outcomes, about the process and quality of the conversation
- **No aggressive CTA language** — "Submit" is acceptable; avoid "Get started now" or "Claim your session"

### 4. Engagement Model and Rate Transparency Page

Write an engagement model and pricing transparency page for a [professional services / consulting / advisory firm] that answers the senior buyer's "how much and how does it work" question honestly and without deflection.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "How We Engage: Scope, Team, and Typical Investment"
- **Lead (100 words):** Acknowledge that pricing conversations are often opaque in professional services; state that the goal of this page is to help the reader determine whether [Firm] is the right fit before investing either party's time in a scoping call; be direct — "If our engagement model and typical investment levels don't fit your situation, we'll know immediately and can say so."
- **Engagement types (3–4 models, 150 words each):**
  - For each: name, scope (brief description of what it covers), typical structure (e.g., 4-week diagnostic, 3-month implementation), team composition, deliverables, and typical investment range (ranges are acceptable; do not refuse to give ranges entirely)
  - Include: "Best suited for" and "Not well-suited for" for each model — the honest self-selection guide
- **Team composition and billing transparency:**
  - Practitioner seniority on delivery: who leads, who supports, how senior team time is allocated
  - Note on sub-contracting or associates if applicable — buyers ask this; pre-answer it
  - Rate card transparency: "[Firm] does not publish a standard rate card because engagements are scoped by project, but our typical day rates for [senior / lead] practitioners range from [£X–£Y] for planning purposes."
- **What is never included:** Travel and accommodation (billed at cost); third-party data or licensing; legal or compliance review (refer to firm's terms)
- **FAQ (5 questions):**
  - "Can we engage on a fixed-fee basis?"
  - "What if our situation doesn't fit one of these models?"
  - "What happens if the engagement scope changes?"
  - "Do you work with clients outside [geography]?"
  - "What if we don't see the results we expected?" — answer this honestly
- **CTA:** "If our approach seems like a fit, tell us what you're working on." → consultation request form (link to the consultation request page)
