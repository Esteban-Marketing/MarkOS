# Professional Services — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Professional services paid media operates in a high-trust, low-click-volume environment. C-suite and senior buyers are not receptive to lead gen form ads on cold LinkedIn — they need to see practitioner credibility before they will engage. Paid channels work best at awareness and retargeting stages; rarely at direct conversion on cold audiences. RFP culture means the buyer's journey is long and committee-driven — paid media's job is to establish presence and credibility, not close deals.

## Prompts

### 1. LinkedIn C-Suite Awareness Campaign

Write a LinkedIn paid media campaign targeting C-suite and senior buyers at [target company profile] for a [management consulting / strategy advisory / professional services] firm.

Campaign objective: Brand awareness and practitioner credibility — not lead gen form conversion on cold audiences.

Deliverables:
- **Audience targeting spec:** Title, seniority, company size, industry; exclude current clients and competitors; include retargeting segment from website visitors (practice area pages and team pages); note: do not use lead gen form objectives for cold awareness — use website click or brand awareness
- **Ad creative brief (3 variants):**
  - Variant A (Practitioner insight): Named practitioner + diagnostic observation from client work — "In 14 engagements this year, we saw the same pattern: [specific finding]." Short, credible, no CTA except "See the full analysis"
  - Variant B (Methodology frame): Named practice/framework + 1 outcome example — formal framing; targets buyers who recognise the methodology
  - Variant C (Peer signal): Client quote or anonymised case reference from a peer role — "A [role] at a [company type] said: [specific quote about the engagement]." Link to case study page
- **Budget recommendations:** Cold audience awareness (CPM target), warm retargeting (CPC target), frequency cap to avoid overexposure with small senior audience pools

### 2. Google Retargeting by Audience Segment

Write a Google display and search retargeting campaign for a [professional services firm], targeting buyers who have visited the website but not converted.

Segment-based strategy — create separate bid adjustments and creative for:
- **Practice area / service page visitors:** Highest intent; retarget with specific practice outcome proof; search retargeting on firm name + practice area keywords; display with case study or methodology content
- **Team / credentials page visitors:** Researching who they'd be working with; retarget with named practitioner content; "meet [practitioner name]" display or responsive search ads
- **Contact/consultation page visitors (non-converted):** Highest intent; retarget with friction-reducing content — "How we scope engagements" or "What happens on a first call"; direct response CTA

For each segment:
- Audience definition (GA4 audience or RLSA segment)
- Ad format recommendation (responsive display / responsive search / both)
- Creative direction (50-character headline, 150-character description, image concept)
- Bid adjustment rationale

### 3. Competitive Positioning vs. Larger or Generalist Firms

Write a LinkedIn paid campaign targeting buyers who are evaluating [our firm] against larger generalist consulting firms or big-4 adjacent practices.

Context: The buyer may be considering a global firm for scale or name recognition, but the [boutique / specialist / partner-led] firm offers deeper practitioner engagement, faster access to senior talent, and more specific experience in [practice area or vertical].

Campaign brief:
- **Targeting:** Buyers at companies of [size range] where a broader platform is plausible competition; include competitor followers on LinkedIn where possible; retarget website visitors who also visited competitor comparison terms on search
- **Message positioning:** Do not mention competitors by name; position by contrast — "When every meeting is with a partner, not a junior analyst" or "Specialist doesn't mean smaller scope — it means the right expertise every time"
- **Ad variants (3):**
  - Variant A: Partner-access / seniority of delivery team
  - Variant B: Specific practice depth vs. generalist breadth
  - Variant C: Speed to value and agility — "No resourcing lag, no 6-week onboarding"
- **Landing page:** Link to team/credentials page or engagement model overview — not homepage

### 4. Executive Briefing or Webinar Lead Gen

Write a LinkedIn lead gen campaign for a [professional services firm] promoting an invitation-only executive briefing or thought leadership webinar on [topic relevant to practice area].

Context: Cold outbound form ads do not convert senior buyers in professional services. Invitation-to-a-table works. The event or briefing is the lead magnet — it offers peer exchange, not a sales pitch.

Deliverables:
- **Event positioning:** Invite-only or limited-seat framing; topic derived from a genuine diagnostic insight from recent client work — not marketing fluff; practitioner-hosted (named partner or principal), not "join our team"
- **Ad creative brief:**
  - Headline: "[Practitioner Name], [Firm], is hosting a private briefing for [job function] leaders on [specific topic]: [what attendees will take away]"
  - Body: 2–3 sentences — the specific problem this briefing addresses, who should attend (be specific), and what they will leave with
  - CTA: "Request an invitation" (not "Register now" — scarcity and selectivity signal credibility)
- **Post-event nurture:** 3-email sequence: (1) Attendee follow-up with key takeaway summary, (2) Non-attendee missed insights with recording or summary, (3) Peer follow-up with invitation to a follow-on conversation
- **Targeting:** Title, seniority, company type; exclude current clients from cold targeting but include in warm sequence
