# Professional Services — Lifecycle Email Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Professional services email operates in a long-cycle, relationship-driven buying process. A typical engagement is multi-stakeholder, committee-evaluated, and often RFP-gated. Email in this context is rarely the conversion channel — it is the credibility and trust maintenance channel. The job of lifecycle email is to keep the firm present in the buyer's mind with genuinely useful and authoritative content until the buying event occurs. Urgency and aggressive CTAs are counterproductive with senior professional services buyers.

## Prompts

### 1. RFP Inquiry Acknowledgment and Qualification Sequence

Write a 3-email sequence for a [professional services / consulting / advisory firm] responding to an inbound RFP inquiry or initial contact request.

Sequence:
- **Email 1 — Acknowledgment and credentials (send within 24 hours of inquiry):**
  - Objective: Confirm receipt, establish firm credibility immediately, set expectations for next step
  - Open: Personalised acknowledgment of their specific inquiry (reference their stated challenge or engagement type if provided)
  - Body: 3–4 sentences on the firm's specific experience in [their practice area or sector] — not generic "we're pleased you reached out"; include a named practitioner who typically leads this type of engagement and their background in 1 sentence
  - Social proof: 1 brief reference to a similar engagement — "We recently completed an engagement with a [comparable organisation type] on a similar challenge"
  - Next step: Offer a scoping call — propose 2 specific times or a scheduling link; explain what the call covers (30 minutes, no pitch — we learn about your situation, you learn whether we're the right fit)

- **Email 2 — Social proof and fit validation (send if no response to Email 1, day 3–5):**
  - Objective: Provide a reason to engage through peer evidence
  - Body: Share a relevant case study or client reference story — specific outcome, specific engagement type, anonymised where needed
  - Include: 1 client testimonial quote from a buyer in a similar role
  - Restate the offer: "If [challenge described in inquiry] resonates with what we described, a 30-minute scoping call is the right next step. We'll answer your questions directly — including questions about our methodology, team composition, and how we price engagements like this."

- **Email 3 — Final follow-up with practitioner direct note (send day 8–10 if no response):**
  - Objective: Give a genuine last attempt before marking as not ready
  - Format: Short, practitioner-signed note — not a marketing email; 4–6 sentences max
  - Message: Acknowledge they may be at an early stage or not ready for a formal conversation; offer an alternative lower-commitment touchpoint (a brief piece of content, a short diagnostic question); leave the door open without pressure

### 2. Proposal Follow-Up and Differentiation Sequence

Write a 3-email proposal follow-up sequence for a [professional services firm] after a proposal has been submitted but no decision received within the expected timeframe.

Sequence:
- **Email 1 — Proposal follow-up with differentiation reinforcement (send on expected decision day +2):**
  - Avoid "just checking in" — add value
  - Restate the 1–2 most differentiating aspects of the proposal; offer to clarify methodology or team composition if the decision team has questions
  - Attach or link a supporting credential — team bio, methodology overview, or relevant case study not previously shared

- **Email 2 — Committee support offer (send day +7 if no response):**
  - Context: Proposal decisions in PS often involve multiple stakeholders — offer to support the internal champion's business case
  - Body: "If it would be useful, I'm happy to prepare a brief one-page summary or comparison document that addresses likely questions from [function, e.g., finance, legal, the board] reviewing the proposal. Many of our clients have found this helps move internal review forward."
  - Offer: 15-minute call to understand the decision process and how to support it — not a re-pitch

- **Email 3 — Final position statement (send day +14 if no response):**
  - Short and direct: "We remain interested in supporting [project/challenge]. If the decision timeline has shifted or the scope has changed, I'm available to discuss."
  - One credibility anchor: "In the meantime, [link to relevant practitioner article / thought leadership content relevant to their challenge]."
  - Close: "I'll assume you'll reach back when the time is right and won't follow up further unless you'd like me to."

### 3. Project Kickoff and Onboarding Email Series

Write a 3-email onboarding sequence for a new client of a [professional services / consulting / advisory firm] from contract signature to engagement launch.

Sequence:
- **Email 1 — Welcome and team introduction (send within 24 hours of signature):**
  - From: Lead practitioner / engagement partner
  - Tone: Formal and warm; establishes working relationship with care
  - Content: Welcome + brief restatement of the engagement goal in their language (not the contract language); introduce the core team with practitioner backgrounds (2–3 sentences each); explain what happens in the first 2 weeks (preparation, kick-off meeting, data gathering); state who their primary contact is for day-to-day questions
  - Attach: Team bios and engagement overview document

- **Email 2 — Kickoff prep and logistics (send 3–4 days before kickoff meeting):**
  - Content: Kickoff meeting agenda; any pre-reading or materials to review; data or stakeholder access they need to prepare; logistics (format, attendee list, expected duration)
  - Tone: Organised and anticipatory — signals the firm is prepared and values their time
  - Note: Include a brief statement on what the client can expect from the firm in terms of communication cadence, report delivery, and escalation path

- **Email 3 — Kickoff recap and first milestone confirmation (send within 48 hours of kickoff meeting):**
  - Content: Summary of key decisions made in kickoff (5–7 bullets); confirmed milestone dates and owners; any open items with resolution deadline; next scheduled touchpoint
  - Tone: Clear, efficient, professional
  - Close: Affirm the partnership and invite any questions before the first interim milestone

### 4. Post-Engagement Referral Request, Case Study Capture, and Relationship Continuity

Write a 3-email post-engagement sequence for a [professional services firm] following the completion of an engagement.

Sequence:
- **Email 1 — Engagement close and outcome summary (send within 1 week of formal completion):**
  - From: Lead practitioner
  - Content: Thank client for the engagement; summarise the core outcomes delivered (3–5 bullets, specific metrics or deliverables); acknowledge the client team's contribution (specific names if appropriate — not generic "your great team")
  - Referral ask: "If you know of other [function or sector] leaders who are navigating similar challenges, we'd be grateful for an introduction. The best way for us to grow is through the clients we've served."
  - Case study ask: "With your permission, we'd like to document this engagement as a case study — anonymised unless you'd prefer to be credited. It helps us show prospective clients what this type of work looks like in practice. Would you be open to a 20-minute call to capture your perspective?"

- **Email 2 — Thought leadership content follow-through (send 6–8 weeks post-engagement):**
  - Objective: Maintain the relationship through genuine value, not forced "check-ins"
  - Content: Share a recently published article, framework, or event invitation relevant to the challenge the client has just navigated — personalise with 1 sentence connecting the content to their specific situation
  - Close: "No action needed — just thought this was useful given what we worked through together. Please do reach back if a related challenge surfaces."

- **Email 3 — Annual relationship touchpoint and next engagement seed (send at 9–12 months post-engagement):**
  - Objective: Keep the relationship warm for expansion or repeat engagement
  - Content: Brief update on firm developments (new practice area, practitioner addition, relevant methodology publication); reference the prior engagement in 1–2 sentences — acknowledge any visible progress the client's organisation has made; invite a brief conversation if they are navigating a new challenge
  - Tone: Peer relationship, not vendor re-engage; this is a practitioner colleague reaching out to a respected former client
