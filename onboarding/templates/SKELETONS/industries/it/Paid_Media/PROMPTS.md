# IT & Technology — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** IT paid media must earn credibility before claiming it. The IT buyer — CTO, IT Director, Security Lead, or technical procurement — evaluates vendor marketing with a technical skeptic's lens: vague benefit language signals a weak product; architectural specificity and proven security posture signal a trustworthy vendor. Search intent in IT is research-heavy: buyers are comparing architectures, reading security white papers, and evaluating TCO — not impulse-buying. Match the content depth to the buyer's actual research behavior.

## Prompts

### 1. Google Search Campaign for Technical Intent Keywords

Write a Google Search campaign brief and ad copy for a [IT solution / managed service / security product] targeting technical decision-maker queries around [solution category].

Campaign structure:
- **Keyword strategy (3 tiers):**
  - High-intent evaluation keywords: "[solution category] software," "[solution category] for [company size/industry]," "best [solution category]," "[specific technology] management platform," "[compliance standard] compliance tool" — bid aggressively; these buyers are in active vendor evaluation
  - Comparison and alternative keywords: "[competitor name] alternative," "[competitor name] vs [solution category]," "compare [solution category] tools," "[solution category] pricing" — bid with comparison content; these buyers are already solution-aware and shortlisting
  - Problem-symptom keywords: "[pain point] solution," "how to [solve specific IT problem]," "[risk or issue] prevention" — bid with problem-framing content; these buyers are problem-aware but not yet solution-specific
- **Ad copy for high-intent tier (3 RSA headlines + 2 descriptions):**
  - Headline 1: Solution category + differentiator ("[Solution Category] for [Company Type] | [Key Technical Differentiator]")
  - Headline 2: Compliance or security anchor ("[SOC2 Type II / ISO 27001 / FedRAMP Authorized]")
  - Headline 3: Proof or offer ("Free [POC / Security Audit / Technical Assessment]" / "[N]+ Enterprise Clients")
  - Description 1: Technical specificity — how the product works at a high architecture level + specific outcome metric ("Our [architecture approach] reduces [metric] by [X]% for organizations migrating from [current approach].")
  - Description 2: Trust signal + friction removal ("No lock-in contract. Replaces [competitor/legacy system] in [timeframe]. [N]-week implementation average.")
- **Negative keyword list (critical for quality score):** Generic consumer IT terms, job board queries, free software terms, tutorial/learning intent

### 2. LinkedIn ABM Campaign for IT Leadership (CTO, CIO, IT Director)

Write a LinkedIn Account-Based Marketing (ABM) campaign brief and ad creative for a [IT vendor / MSP / security firm] targeting C-level IT and VP/Director-level IT decision-makers at [target company size / industry].

ABM campaign structure:
- **Target audience definition:**
  - Job titles: CTO, CIO, VP of IT, IT Director, IT Manager, Head of Infrastructure, CISO, VP of Engineering (depending on solution type)
  - Company targeting: Named account list (upload to LinkedIn Matched Audiences) + industry + company size; optionally layer technology targeting (companies using [specific tech stack] if solution integrates)
  - Exclusions: Current customers, active trial users, competitors
- **Campaign stages (3-stage ABM sequence):**
  - Stage 1 — Awareness: Serve technical thought leadership content (white paper, architecture guide, security research); no CTA to demo or trial yet; objective: reach + content engagement; measure: content download rate, video view rate
  - Stage 2 — Consideration (re-engage Stage 1 engagers): Serve case study content matched to their industry vertical; the case study should include same-stack technical detail; CTA: "Read the full case study" or "Technical deep-dive available — request access"
  - Stage 3 — Decision (re-engage Stage 2 engagers or inbound form leads): Serve POC or assessment offer; CTA: "Request a technical assessment" or "Start your POC" — with friction-appropriate landing page (not just "Contact us")
- **Ad creative for Stage 1 (Sponsored Content — Document Ad):**
  - Document title: "[Technical Topic]: Architecture Guide for [Title/Role] Evaluating [Solution Category]"
  - Intro text (600 chars): Open with the technical problem the guide addresses — in the buyer's language; establish the depth of the document (what architecture decisions it helps make, what compliance questions it answers); CTA: "Download the architecture guide — no form required"
  - Removing the form for Stage 1 content improves quality reach; capture at Stage 2/3

### 3. ROI Retargeting Campaign

Write a retargeting campaign for an [IT vendor] targeting website visitors who viewed pricing, solution, or case study pages but did not convert.

Retargeting audience segments:
- **Segment A — Solution page visitors (no demo/trial action):** Served retargeting ad with first meaningful proof element: same-industry case study with specific ROI metric (not generic "many customers saved money" — specific: "A [company type] of [size] reduced [metric] by [X]% in [timeframe]"). CTA: "See the full case study" → case study page. Goal: deepen credibility before asking for a demo commitment.
- **Segment B — Pricing page visitors (no form, no demo):** Message: ROI context + comparison clarity. Ad copy: "At $[price point], most [company type] see ROI within [timeframe]. Here's the TCO breakdown your finance team will ask for." CTA: "Get the TCO comparison" → ROI calculator or TCO white paper. Do not push demo immediately — provide the business case building tool the buyer needs.
- **Segment C — Case study readers + white paper downloaders (no next step action):** Message: Technical depth + POC offer. Ad copy: "Seen enough to warrant a controlled technical evaluation? Our [N]-week POC is scoped for [company type] with [specific stack or compliance requirement]." CTA: "Start your POC" → POC sign-up page with clear scope, timeline, and commitment level stated
- **Platform guidance:** LinkedIn for title-targeted retargeting (higher CPM but exact role targeting); Google Display for broader retargeting across web; frequency cap: 3–4 impressions/day; retargeting window: 30 days for Segments A and B; 14 days for Segment C (higher intent, faster decision cycle)

### 4. Competitive Displacement Campaign

Write a paid search and social campaign for a [IT vendor] targeting buyers actively evaluating or unhappy with a competing solution.

Campaign structure:
- **Google Search — competitor name and "alternative" keywords:**
  - Keywords: "[Competitor name]," "[Competitor name] alternative," "[Competitor name] pricing," "[Competitor name] reviews," "[Competitor name] problems," "switch from [Competitor name]"
  - Ad copy (competitive displacement framing, not disparagement):
    - Headline 1: "[Your Product] — [Competitor name] Alternative"
    - Headline 2: "[Key differentiator vs. competitor: data residency, pricing model, integration depth]"
    - Headline 3: "Migration Support Included" or "Switch in [N] Weeks"
    - Description 1: Name the specific thing the competitor doesn't do well (based on publicly known weaknesses, not speculation): "Teams switching from [Competitor] often cite [issue]. [Your product] solves this through [specific technical approach]."
    - Description 2: Risk removal: "Migration from [Competitor] handled by our engineering team. [N] [competitor] migrations completed. [Outcome: average time, data integrity guarantee]."
  - Landing page brief: Dedicated comparison page (you vs. competitor); include a candid comparison table — include dimensions where the competitor is comparable, not just where you win; this builds credibility
- **LinkedIn — competitor customer ABM list targeting:**
  - Upload a list of known competitor customers (from firmographic data, review sites, or public case studies); target with a Stage 2/3 message: "If you're re-evaluating your [solution category], here's what [N] companies who switched found." CTA: "Read the migration guide" or "Request a migration assessment"
