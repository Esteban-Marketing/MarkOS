# IT & Technology — Social Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** IT social content lives primarily on LinkedIn — decision-makers in IT procurement (CISOs, CTOs, VP Engineering, IT Directors) spend professional reading time there. Twitter/X retains some engineering community presence but deal influence is LinkedIn-dominant. Social content that performs with IT audiences demonstrates technical competence, not capability lists. Thought leadership, practitioner insight, compliance positioning, and peer evidence are the formats that earn credibility. Promotional social content is one of the IT sector's most common trust-destroyers.

## Prompts

### 1. LinkedIn Thought Leadership Post

Write 3 LinkedIn thought leadership post variants for an [IT vendor / SaaS platform] targeting [CISOs / CIOs / VP Engineering / IT Directors].

Variant A — Technical trend observation with original analysis:
- Open with a specific, verifiable observation — not an opinion statement: "In [N] enterprise deployments this quarter, [specific technical pattern / anomaly / behavior] appeared in [X]% of environments." (Anonymized / aggregate is acceptable; invented is not)
- 2–3 sentences that interpret the pattern — why it appears, what it signals about how the market is configuring these environments; demonstrate understanding that comes from deployment experience, not product marketing
- 1 clear implication for the reader: "If you're running [configuration type], this is worth reviewing before [specific trigger event]"
- CTA (light): "We wrote up the methodology — link in comments" or "Curious what's driving this in your environment? DM me."
- No self-promotion, no product mentions in the post itself. If the product is relevant, it appears in the comments or a linked piece.

Variant B — Practitioner insight from anonymized deployment data:
- "Something we see consistently in [customer segment / deployment type]…" — set up the pattern
- Describe the specific technical situation without identifying the customer — the pattern is the story, not the account
- What the team did, what changed, what the outcome was (measured — not "improved significantly")
- Generalize: "This pattern shows up in environments that have [X characteristic] — worth checking if you fit that profile"
- No CTA required — this post is a credibility signal, not a lead generation mechanism

Variant C — Security or compliance commentary on a named regulatory or standards update:
- Name the regulation, standard, or framework update specifically (e.g., "NIST CSF 2.0," "SEC cybersecurity disclosure rule," "PCI DSS v4.0 migration deadline")
- What changed, stated precisely — what new requirements exist, what compliance teams are now being asked to demonstrate
- 1–2 practical implications for the audience's specific role (CISO, IT Director, etc.)
- Where [product category / your product] intersects with this requirement — stated as relevant context, not as a sales point
- Optional CTA: "We've mapped [product] to [framework] — if that's useful for your compliance documentation, see the link."

### 2. Case Study Snippet Post (3 Formats)

Write 3 case study social post formats for an [IT vendor] — versions for LinkedIn feed post, before/after architecture narrative, and carousel.

Format 1 — Metric-led LinkedIn post:
- Lead with the number: "[X] engineer hours recovered per quarter. [Y] incidents prevented in [Z] months. [Organization type, anonymized if needed, with real name if approved]."
- 3 sentences of context: what they were dealing with before, what changed, what the outcome data shows; no adjectives — only verbs and metrics
- 1 CTA line: "Full case study [link]" or "Similar environment? Talk to us [calendar link]."
- Word count target: 100–150 words max. Shorter is better for metric-led posts.

Format 2 — Before/after architecture narrative:
- Frame: "Before [Product]: [state of environment, specific technical description — stack fragmentation, manual process, compliance gap]"
- After: "After [X weeks / months of deployment]: [specific technical change — what now runs automated, what integration is live, what alert/audit trail exists]"
- Outcome: "[Metric] result achieved. [Compliance certification / audit outcome] unlocked."
- Technical specificity is the engagement signal for IT audiences — vague before/after posts are ignored
- Optional: architecture diagram or screenshot if approved; images on LinkedIn improve reach for technical audiences

Format 3 — Carousel (5 slides):
- Slide 1: Challenge — "[Industry / Company type] faced [specific technical problem]" — 1 headline, 1 image/icon
- Slide 2: Approach — What they chose to implement and why — key decision criteria (security posture, integration ease, compliance coverage)
- Slide 3: Result — The 3 key outcome metrics — visual representation preferred (bar chart, number callout)
- Slide 4: What it enables now — The capability they have today that they didn't have before: "Today, [company type] can [specific action] in [timeframe] without [old constraint]"
- Slide 5: Similar scenario? — "Running a similar environment? [CTA button — 'Start your POC' or 'Talk to a solutions engineer']"

### 3. Product or Feature Announcement Post (3 Formats)

Write 3 product and feature announcement social post formats for an [IT vendor].

Format 1 — Capability launch LinkedIn post:
- Open with the outcome, not the feature: "Starting today, [Product] can [specific capability] — [what that means for the user's workflow]."
- 2–3 sentences: what the feature does, what problem it solves, and — critically — who it's most relevant to (job role, company size, stack type)
- 1 specific compliance, security, or scale implication if applicable: "This matters particularly if you're running in [AWS GovCloud / regulated industry / high-volume API environment]"
- CTA: "Documentation: [link]" + "Request a demo of [feature] in your environment: [link]"
- Do NOT lead with your company's name. Do NOT use feature names that require prior product knowledge to understand.

Format 2 — Integration announcement:
- "[Product] now integrates natively with [Tool / Platform]."
- The specific workflow it enables: "When [trigger event], [Product] now automatically [action in integrated tool] — no manual [old step] required."
- Who benefits most — named by role and use case: "Particularly useful for security operations teams using [SIEM tool] alongside [Product]."
- Link to integration documentation (not a sales page — technical buyers will verify before they engage)
- Secondary CTA: "Already using [integrated tool]? Your admin can enable this in [configuration path]." — reduce friction to adoption for existing customers

Format 3 — Release notes distilled in plain language:
- Subject line format: "What's in [Product] [version / release name] — the 3 things that change your workflow"
- List format: 3 items, each 2 sentences: [what changed] + [what it means for you if you're doing X]
- One item can be infrastructure / performance / compliance — it signals that the team is actively maintaining the security and compliance posture, which matters to IT buyers even if it's not a "feature"
- Link to full release notes for those who want depth
- Tone: engineer-to-engineer, not marketing-to-customer. "We fixed the [known issue]. You no longer need to [workaround]."

### 4. Compliance Milestone Announcement Post (3 Formats)

Write 3 compliance milestone and certification social post formats for an [IT vendor].

Format 1 — Certification or accreditation achieved:
- "[Product] is now [SOC2 Type II / ISO 27001 / FedRAMP Authorized / PCI DSS compliant]."
- What the certification covers: scope of systems, time period of audit, auditor firm named if permitted
- What it means for customers: "What this means for your procurement and security team: [specific: vendor risk form can be updated with [certification] evidence; security questionnaire section on [access controls / data residency / incident response] can be answered with this report]"
- How to access: "Request the [report / certificate / evidence pack] from your CSM or via [documentation portal link]."

Format 2 — Annual audit completion transparency post:
- "Our annual [SOC2 Type II / ISO 27001 surveillance] audit is complete."
- Brief: auditor name, audit period, scope covered; outcome stated simply: "No material findings." or "[N] minor findings addressed during the audit period." (transparency builds trust more than unqualified clean reports)
- Availability: "The [summary report / full report for qualified customers] is available on request to current customers and qualified prospects under NDA through [CSM / document request portal]."
- No marketing framing — this is an operational announcement. The trust signal is in what you're disclosing, not in how you describe it.

Format 3 — Framework mapping and alignment post:
- "[Product] maps to [NIST CSF 2.0 / CIS Controls v8 / ISO 27001:2022 / CMMC 2.0]."
- A brief table or list: [Framework control domain / requirement] → [Product capability that addresses it]
- 3–5 mappings is the right length for a social post — full mapping available in linked documentation
- Framing: "If you're using [framework] as your security or compliance structure, here's where [Product] fits — and where it doesn't." (Honest scoping is a trust signal in IT; overclaiming is a procurement red flag.)
- CTA: "Full compliance mapping document: [link to documentation or request form]"
