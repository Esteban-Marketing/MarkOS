# Marketing Services — Landing Pages Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Marketing agency landing pages convert a sophisticated, high-skepticism buyer. The CMO or marketing director evaluating an agency has been pitched before — many times. The single most important conversion factor is trust through specificity: specific results, specific methodology, specific client types, specific next step. Generic agency landing pages ("We drive results. Let's talk.") convert poorly because they fail to differentiate at all. Pages that show proof, scope, and process convert.

## Prompts

### 1. Agency Services Overview Page

Write the primary services overview landing page for a [marketing agency / performance firm], serving as both the main conversion page and an SEO target.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Agency Specialty]: [Outcome-Led Positioning for Target ICP]" — e.g., "B2B SaaS Performance Marketing: Paid + SEO + CRO That Drives Pipeline, Not Just Leads"
- **Above fold (150 words):** The performance problem the target CMO is currently experiencing — in their language, not agency language; the agency's positioning in 2 sentences; 3-metric proof strip (average ROAS improvement %, average CPL reduction %, client count or retention rate); primary CTA: "Get a free audit" (not "Contact us")
- **Service lines (3–5 sections, 80 words each):** For each service: the problem it solves (CMO language), the agency's methodology angle, expected outcome with benchmark metric, optional mini case stat
- **ICP clarification:** "We work with [specific company profile: industry, size, revenue stage, current marketing maturity]" — explicit; include "We're probably not the right fit if..." to pre-qualify and build credibility
- **Proof section:** 3 aggregate metrics (specific, not vague); 3 client testimonials from marketers (not executives); 3 logo strip (or "our clients include companies like...")
- **Primary CTA section:** Free audit offer — what it covers, what they receive, how long it takes; form or calendar embed

### 2. Case Study Landing Page

Write a case study landing page for a [marketing agency] targeting a specific vertical or challenge, optimized for paid and organic traffic.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "How [anonymized: 'a 400-person B2B SaaS company'] Grew Pipeline by [X]% with [Service] in [Timeframe]"
- **Result summary strip (above fold):** 3–4 key metrics in large format: e.g., "143% pipeline lift · $18 CPL (down from $74) · 9 months · $0 increase in media budget"
- **Client context (100 words):** Company type, growth stage, team structure, and the specific marketing performance challenge before engagement — be specific enough that a similar company self-identifies
- **The challenge in detail (150 words):** What they had tried already; why the in-house approach hit a ceiling; 2–3 specific diagnostic findings from the initial audit
- **Our approach (200 words):** Methodology in plain language — not buzzwords; step-by-step: what we changed, why, in what order; show the thinking, not just the actions
- **Results breakdown (100 words):** Primary KPI + 3 secondary metrics; timeline to first meaningful result; what the client's team was freed up to do that they couldn't before
- **Client quote:** One direct quote from a named or anonymized marketing lead — specific outcome reference, not generic praise
- **Related case studies:** 2–3 related case study links (same vertical or same challenge type)
- **Primary CTA:** "See if this approach fits your situation" → free audit offer

### 3. Free Audit or Consultation Offer Page

Write a landing page for a [marketing agency] offering a free [paid media / SEO / lifecycle email / full-funnel] audit.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "Free [Channel] Audit: See Exactly What's Costing You Performance"
- **Above fold:** H1 + 3-bullet what the audit covers (specific deliverables — not vague "we'll review your account"); form or CTA to start; trust signal: "[N] audits completed. Average finding: [X% improvement opportunity identified]."
- **What the audit includes (200 words):** 4–5 specific audit components — for each: what we analyse, what outputs you receive, and what a typical finding looks like
- **Sample audit output preview:** Blurred or anonymised screenshot of an actual audit output; brief explanation of what it shows and why it matters
- **"This audit isn't for everyone" section:** Who the audit is designed for (specific profile); and who it isn't for — this builds credibility and qualifies leads
- **What happens after the audit:** 3-step process: (1) We deliver the findings report in [N] business days, (2) We offer a 30-minute walkthrough call — no pitch, just findings, (3) If there's a fit, we discuss options; if there isn't, we'll say so
- **Form:** Work email, company size, monthly [channel] budget, current challenge (dropdown)

### 4. Pricing Transparency / Engagement Model Overview Page

Write an engagement model or pricing overview page for a [marketing agency] that answers the "how much does it cost / how do you work" question for CMO-level buyers.

Page structure:
- **H1:** "How [Agency Name] Works: Engagement Models, Typical Investment, and What You Get"
- **Lead (100 words):** Acknowledge that most agencies hide pricing and why that's frustrating for CMOs who need to build a business case; state that the goal of this page is to help them determine whether [Agency] is the right size and fit before getting on a call
- **Engagement model overview (3 tiers or formats, 100 words each):**
  - Tier 1 (e.g., Project / Audit): Scope, typical duration, cost range, deliverables, best for
  - Tier 2 (e.g., Monthly retainer): Scope, included hours or deliverables, cost range, minimum term, best for
  - Tier 3 (e.g., Performance or revenue-share): How it works, qualification criteria, what the agency takes on, best for
- **"What's included" transparency:** Tool costs (included or excluded), reporting frequency, team structure on their account, communication SLA
- **What's NOT included (important for trust):** Media budget (agency manages it, client funds it); ad platform fees; third-party tool subscriptions if not bundled
- **FAQ section (5 questions):** Common CMO questions about agency relationships — answer each honestly; include "What if results don't meet targets?" with a real answer, not a deflection
- **CTA:** "Not sure which model fits? Tell us what you're solving for." → free consultation or audit
