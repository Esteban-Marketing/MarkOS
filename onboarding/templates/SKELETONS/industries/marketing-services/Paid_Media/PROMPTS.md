# Marketing Services — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Paid media for marketing agencies sells performance to a buyer who understands performance. The biggest trap is using generic "we drive results" language to an audience that can immediately identify weak claims. CMOs and VP Marketing buyers evaluate agency paid media the way a chef evaluates a restaurant: they know exactly what they're looking at. Lead with proof density, channel-specific fluency, and a differentiated offer that reduces trial risk.

## Prompts

### 1. LinkedIn Campaign Targeting CMO, VP Marketing, and Growth Leaders

Write a LinkedIn paid campaign brief and ad creative for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Director, and Head of Growth roles at [target company size / industry].

Campaign structure:
- **Audience targeting:** Job title list (CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Director, Head of Growth, VP Demand Generation) + company size (50–5,000 employees depending on ICP) + industry filter (if vertical-specialist agency); optionally company list upload for named-account ABM
- **Campaign objective:** Lead gen form (for audit/consultation offer) or website conversions (for case study or benchmark report downloads)
- **Ad format:** Single image ad or document ad (for distributing a benchmark report)
- **Ad creative (Single Image + CTA):**
  - Image direction: Performance dashboard screenshot or chart showing impressive result — avoid stock photos; this audience spots them immediately
  - Headline (150 chars): Result-first framing ("How [anonymized client type] achieved [specific metric] in [timeframe] — no creative rebrand required")
  - Intro text (600 chars): Lead with the client's performance problem in CMO-native language (CPL, CAC, ROAS, pipeline coverage); state specific outcome from a real engagement; describe the offer (free paid media audit, benchmark analysis, or strategy session); include why response is warranted now (benchmark data, seasonal opportunity, or regulatory shift relevant to their industry)
- **Lead gen form fields:** Work email, company name, company size (dropdown), primary performance challenge (dropdown: paid media efficiency, lead quality, attribution, scaling, agency transition), monthly paid media budget range
- **Follow-up:** Form submission → immediate delivery of audit framework or benchmark report + SDR outreach within 4 hours with personalized message referencing their stated challenge

### 2. Case Study-Led Google Search Campaign

Write a Google Search ad campaign for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting CMO-level intent keywords around agency selection, performance marketing audits, and channel-specific growth.

Campaign parameters:
- **High-intent keyword examples:** "performance marketing agency," "paid media agency [city/region]," "marketing agency for [industry]," "hire marketing agency," "marketing audit," "Google Ads agency," "LinkedIn ads agency," "agency results case study"
- **Ad structure (3 RSA headlines):**
  - Headline 1: Agency specialty + result lead ("Performance Marketing Agency | [N]% avg ROAS lift")
  - Headline 2: ICP or proof signal ("B2B SaaS · [N]+ Clients · Case Studies Available")
  - Headline 3: Low-friction offer ("Free Paid Media Audit" / "See Our Results" / "Talk to a Strategist")
- **Descriptions (2, 90 chars max each):**
  - Description 1: Specific outcome + methodology hint ("We rebuilt [client type]'s paid media from $[X] CPA to $[Y] CPA in [timeframe]. See how.")
  - Description 2: ICP signal + offer friction reduction ("No retainer required for audit. No lock-in contract in year one. [CTA]")
- **Extensions:** Sitelinks to case studies (by industry vertical), services overview, pricing/engagement model, and free audit offer; callout extensions for named certifications (Google Premier Partner, Meta Business Partner, HubSpot Diamond)

Tone: Confident and specific. Every claim backed by a case study link or methodology reference.

### 3. Performance Audit or Assessment Lead Gen Ad

Write a paid social ad campaign (Meta/LinkedIn) for a [marketing agency] offering a free [paid media / SEO / lifecycle / full-funnel] audit as a lead generation hook.

Ad strategy:
- **Why audits work for agency acquisition:** They demonstrate competence before commitment; they are high-perceived-value for the prospect; they naturally surface the problems the agency solves; they create a transition from marketing to sales with a warm, specific conversation
- **Audience:** CMO, VP Marketing, in-house marketing team leads with budget responsibility; retarget website visitors who browsed the agency's case studies or services pages
- **Ad creative (Meta Primary Image Ad):**
  - Visual: An audit findings example — blurred-out client data with visible score or issue categories
  - Primary text (125 chars): "Is your [channel] account leaving money on the table? [N] of [N] accounts we audit have at least one structural efficiency leak. Find yours — free."
  - Headline: "Free [Channel] Audit — See What You're Missing"
  - Description: "No pitch. Just findings. We'll send you a full report."
- **Landing page alignment:** The ad must land on an audit-specific page (not the homepage); must show a sample audit output; must make the process frictionless: "Submit account access via a read-only link — we deliver findings in [X] business days"
- **Disqualification copy:** Include language about who the audit is NOT for (pure startup, pre-revenue, or < $[X]/month budget) — this improves lead quality and signals confidence

### 4. Competitive Positioning vs. In-House Marketing Team

Write a paid search and social ad campaign for a [marketing agency] competing against the "build it in-house" objection from emerging or scaling marketing buyers.

Campaign structure:
- **Search campaign — "in-house vs. agency" intent keywords:** "in-house marketing team cost," "hire marketing manager vs agency," "agency vs in-house ROI," "cost of in-house paid media manager"
- **Ad copy for search (3 RSA headlines + 2 descriptions):**
  - Headline 1: "In-House vs. Agency: The True Cost"
  - Headline 2: "Full Team at [N]% of the Hiring Cost"
  - Headline 3: "No Ramp Time. See Results in 30 Days."
  - Description 1: "A mid-level marketing manager costs $[X]–$[Y] + benefits + tooling. Our agency includes [N] specialists, all tools, and [metric-led outcome guarantee]. Read the breakdown."
  - Description 2: "We've helped [N] [company type] scale faster by agency than in-house — without the hiring risk. Free audit to prove it."
- **Social ad for consideration-stage buyers (LinkedIn):**
  - Visual: Side-by-side comparison graphic (in-house cost components vs. agency retainer with equivalent team capabilities)
  - Intro text (600 chars): Acknowledge the in-house vs. agency decision is a real strategic question; provide a framework for making the right choice (not a push toward agency); include a specific breakeven point — "For most [company type] at [$revenue stage], the crossover is [X] — here's how we calculate it"
  - CTA: "Download the In-House vs. Agency Cost Calculator"
