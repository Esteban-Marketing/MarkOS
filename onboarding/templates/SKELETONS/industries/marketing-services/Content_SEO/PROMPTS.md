# Marketing Services — Content & SEO Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Content SEO for marketing agencies targets buyers who are actively evaluating agency partners or trying to solve a marketing performance problem they can't solve in-house. The highest-performing content demonstrates competence — benchmark reports, methodology guides, and case studies that show how the agency thinks, not just what it does. The buyer is a marketing expert; surface-level content immediately signals low-value agency positioning.

## Prompts

### 1. Agency Capability / Services Overview Page

Write an SEO-optimized services overview page for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting the keyword "[agency type] services" or "best [marketing channel] agency."

Page structure:
- **H1:** "[Agency Name] — [Specialty] Marketing Agency: [Outcome-Led Positioning]" — e.g., "Performance Marketing Agency for B2B SaaS: Paid Media, SEO, and CRO That Drives Pipeline"
- **Intro (100 words):** Lead with the performance problem the agency solves (in CMO language: "Most B2B SaaS companies are generating leads — but less than [X]% convert to pipeline. The problem is rarely the channel; it's the targeting, creative, and conversion path."); state the agency's approach in 2 sentences; establish credibility (client count, years, vertical focus)
- **Service lines (3–5 sections, 80 words each):** For each service: problem it solves, the agency's methodology angle, measurable outcome to expect, and a client metric example
- **Proof section:** Aggregate performance stats (average ROAS improvement %, average CPL reduction %, average pipeline contribution lift for clients in [niche]) + 3 mini client logos or anonymized case metrics
- **ICP clarity section:** "We work best with [company profile]" — specific: revenue stage, team size, channel mix, growth stage; include "We're probably not the right fit if..." — builds credibility through honesty
- **Primary CTA:** "Get a free audit" or "Talk to a strategist" — not "Contact us"

### 2. Industry Benchmark Report or State-of-the-Market Content

Write a benchmark report outline and executive summary for a [marketing agency] producing original data or curated analysis on [specific channel or marketing discipline] performance benchmarks.

Report structure:
- **Title:** "The [Year] [Channel/Discipline] Benchmark Report: [What the Data Reveals About Top-Performing [Company/Campaign Type]]"
- **Executive summary (300 words for the SEO-landing version):** Key finding lead (the single most surprising or actionable data point); 4–5 punchy finding bullets; who collected this data and why you can trust it; what the reader should do with it
- **Report sections (outline for full report):** (1) Benchmark methodology — data sources, sample size, date range, definitions; (2) Industry-wide performance benchmarks — key metrics by company size, vertical, and spend range; (3) Top-quartile vs. median performance — what separates top performers; (4) Channel-specific deep dive; (5) Forecast or trend for next 12 months; (6) Recommendations
- **SEO landing page for report download (CTA gate):**
  - H1: Report title
  - Lead paragraph: The most compelling finding — specific number that makes the reader want the full report
  - 3 bullet must-read findings
  - Form: First name + work email + company size + primary channel focus (dropdown)
  - Trust signal: "Used by [N] marketing leaders. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."

### 3. Client Results Case Study in SEO Format

Write a case study in SEO content format for a [marketing agency], targeting keywords like "marketing agency case study [industry]" or "[agency type] results [company type]."

Case study SEO format:
- **H1:** "How [Anonymized: 'a 150-person B2B SaaS company'] Reduced CAC by [X]% with [Agency Service] in [Timeframe]"
- **Client context (100 words):** Company profile (industry, size, revenue stage, team structure) — enough for readers to self-identify as similar; the specific marketing performance challenge they faced before engaging the agency
- **Why they chose [Agency] (80 words):** What differentiated the agency in the selection process — be specific (methodology, vertical expertise, contract terms); don't just say "they liked our approach"
- **What we did (200 words):** Specific actions taken, in order of implementation — include the reasoning behind key decisions (why this channel, why this audience, why this creative direction); show that this is a strategy, not a template
- **Results (100 words):** Primary KPI improvement with exact figures; secondary metrics; timeline to first result; comparison to pre-engagement benchmark
- **What [Client] says:** Direct quote from CMO or marketing lead — specific to the outcome, not generic praise
- **Related resources:** Link to relevant service page; link to related case study in same vertical; CTA to request a free audit

### 4. "Why Outsource [Marketing Channel]?" Decision-Support Guide

Write an SEO decision-support article for a [marketing agency] targeting the keyword "when to outsource [paid media / SEO / email marketing / social media]" or "in-house vs. agency [channel]."

Article structure:
- **H1:** "When to Outsource Your [Channel]: A Practical Framework for Marketing Leaders"
- **Intro (100 words):** Frame this as a genuine decision, not a sales pitch — acknowledge that in-house can be the right answer; your goal is to help the reader make the right decision for their situation
- **The decision framework (3 factors, 100 words each):**
  - Factor 1: Current performance gap — what does your [channel] currently cost per outcome vs. industry benchmark? If you're 2x the benchmark, you need a diagnosis before deciding build vs. buy.
  - Factor 2: Internal capability and bandwidth — do you have the specialist expertise, tooling access, and time needed to close the performance gap in-house within [timeframe]?
  - Factor 3: Stage and speed — at what revenue stage does in-house become more cost-effective than agency? (Include a rough breakeven model.)
- **When agency beats in-house (specific scenarios):** 3 concrete situations where agency is the dominant choice; include the financial case for each
- **When in-house beats agency (specific scenarios):** 2 concrete situations — do not omit this; it builds trust and self-selects toward better-fit agency clients
- **How to evaluate an agency once you've decided (100 words):** 5 questions to ask; what good answers look like
- **CTA:** Link to free audit offer; link to agency services comparison page
