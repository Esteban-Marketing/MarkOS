# Marketing Services — Social Industry Overlay Prompts

> **Context:** Social media for marketing agencies is a live credibility demonstration. Every post signals the agency's marketing quality to a buyer who evaluates marketing for a living. The highest-performing marketing agency social content shows results, demonstrates strategic thinking, and builds the team's individual authority — not just the agency brand. Agencies that post generic marketing tips or self-promotional content are signaling a lower quality floor; agencies that post specific data, original analysis, and practitioner-voice content demonstrate what clients are buying.

## Prompts

### 1. Campaign Results Post (Performance Metrics Showcase)

Write 3 social post variants for a [marketing agency] sharing real or representative campaign outcome data.

**Post 1 — Specific result, LinkedIn format:**
- Lead with the number: "[Metric result]: [e.g., $14 CPL on LinkedIn for a [industry] SaaS company running [campaign type].]"
- 3-sentence context: What the benchmark was before; what changed and why (1-sentence methodology mention — not a full breakdown); why this result matters for this client type
- Close: "Happy to walk through how we got there — DM if [problem this result solves] sounds familiar."

**Post 2 — Aggregate outcome post (for Facebook or LinkedIn company page):**
- "[N] paid media campaigns audited in [timeframe]. Here's what the data shows."
- 3–4 data findings from across the audited set (specific percentages, common issues found)
- Close: "The pattern we keep seeing: [insight]. If you think this might apply to your account, we're offering free audits through [date]."

**Post 3 — "Results vs. expectation" format:**
- "Client came to us saying [expectation]. Here's what we found — and what we actually fixed."
- Brief then/now: the briefed goal vs. the underlying problem discovered; what changed in the approach; outcome metric
- Close: "The brief rarely tells you where the real lever is. That's what audits are for."

### 2. Thought Leadership Post (Marketing Trend or Strategic Insight)

Write 3 LinkedIn thought leadership post variants for a [CMO / Head of Strategy / Channel Lead] at a [marketing agency].

**Variant A — Data-led trend observation:**
- Open with a surprising data point or pattern from client data or industry research (cite source)
- 3-paragraph body: What the data shows; what agencies and in-house teams are getting wrong about this trend; what the response should be (specific — not "focus on quality content")
- Close: "Curious whether you're seeing this with your channels. What's your read?"

**Variant B — Contrarian take on a common marketing practice:**
- Open with the common practice ("Most [X] campaigns start with [Y]. We stopped doing that.")
- Explain why the common practice produces a specific failure mode (with an example)
- What works instead (specific approach, not vague advice)
- Close: "Happy to discuss in the comments — I know this is a polarizing take."

**Variant C — Framework or process share:**
- "The [3/5/7]-part framework we use to [diagnose / audit / fix] [specific marketing problem]."
- Walk through the framework in plain language (one sentence per step)
- "This took us [N] engagements across [N] company types to develop. Feel free to use it."
- Close: "If you're working through [this problem] and want to see it applied, I'm happy to do a quick call — no agenda."

### 3. Client Win or Milestone Announcement

Write 3 social post variants for a [marketing agency] announcing a client win, milestone, or award.

**Post 1 — New client announcement:**
- "[Agency Name] is now working with [Client Name or 'a new client in [industry]']."
- 2-sentence context: Why this engagement; what the agency will be doing for them
- Note: if not naming the client, describe the type of company and challenge instead — still more specific than "excited to announce a new partnership"

**Post 2 — Performance milestone:**
- "Q[N] results: [X clients] hit their target [metric: ROAS, CPL, pipeline contribution target]."
- 2-sentence breakdown: What drove the outcomes; one standout result (anonymized if needed)
- CTA: "Benchmark report available if you want to compare against your own numbers — link in comments."

**Post 3 — Industry award or recognition:**
- Agency award announcement: Name of award, awarding body, specific achievement (best campaign, fastest growing, etc.)
- 2-sentence authentic context: What the team did that led to this; the one thing the agency is most proud of from the work recognized
- Avoid pure celebration-speak; keep at least one sentence that tells the reader what this signals about the agency's approach

### 4. Team Expertise / Culture Post

Write 3 social post variants for a [marketing agency] showcasing team expertise, certifications, or agency culture.

**Post 1 — Individual team member expertise spotlight:**
- "[Name], [Role], has [certification / milestone / achievement]."
- 3-sentence context: What this enables for clients; one example of where this expertise recently made a difference in a campaign; what [Name] would tell clients who are skeptical about [relevant topic]
- Tone: Genuine, not promotional; let the expertise speak

**Post 2 — Agency methodology or process post:**
- "Why [Agency] tests [N] creative variants before scaling paid media spend."
- Walk through the testing philosophy in 3 steps — show that this is a system, not a default
- Close: "The agencies that skip this step are the ones clients switch away from. We built the process around that."

**Post 3 — Team certification or platform partnership announcement:**
- "[Agency] has achieved [Google Premier Partner / Meta Business Partner / HubSpot Diamond Partner / etc.] status."
- What the certification requires (3 specific criteria) — demonstrate understanding, not just possession
- What it means for clients (access, support tier, beta features)
