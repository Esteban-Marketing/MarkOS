# Reference Example: **Marketplace** (Freelance Creative Platform)

## Channel Priority Stack (Dual Side)

**1. SEO / Content (Demand Side — Buyer Acquisition)**
- Why: Buyers who are actively looking to hire freelancers search Google. Ranking for "hire freelance graphic designer," "UX designer for startup," and "brand design services" puts us in front of high-intent buyer traffic at zero marginal cost.
- Content format: Long-form comparison guides ("Fiverr vs. Upwork vs. [Platform]"), skill-specific landing pages (one per creative discipline), success story case studies.
- Posting frequency: 2 SEO articles/week, landing pages built once and maintained.
- KPI: Organic traffic growth, top-3 rankings for 10 target keywords, buyer sign-up rate from organic.

**2. Designer Communities (Supply Side — Seller Acquisition)**
- Why: Experienced freelancers congregate in designer Slack communities, Behance, Dribbble, and Reddit's r/freelance. Authentic presence (not ads) in these spaces drives trusted recommendations.
- Content format: Sponsored community posts, "Featured Freelancer" spotlights shared back into communities, transparent posts from founders in designer forums.
- Posting frequency: Ongoing community participation + 2 sponsored placements/month in top-3 designer communities.
- KPI: Supply-side sign-up rate, quality score of new creative profiles, community referral rate.

**3. Google Ads (Demand Side — Performance)**
- Why: Buyer acquisition keywords ("hire graphic designer online," "freelance design agency") have clear commercial intent. Paid search can accelerate buyer supply while organic builds.
- Content format: Search ads targeting job-to-be-done phrases, remarketing to visitors who viewed creative profiles but didn't post a project.
- Posting frequency: Always-on with weekly creative refresh.
- KPI: CPA for first project posted, ROAS, buyer-to-project conversion rate.

**4. LinkedIn (Demand Side — Startup and SMB)**
- Why: Marketing managers and startup founders at 10–250 person companies — the ideal buyer — disproportionately use LinkedIn. Decision is often made by the person posting, not a procurement team.
- Content format: Sponsored InMail for targeted buyer outreach, thought leadership content ("How to brief a designer — the 3 things that guarantee success"), project success case studies.
- Posting frequency: 3–4 organic posts/week + 1 paid campaign active.
- KPI: LinkedIn-sourced first projects, cost-per-buyer from LinkedIn vs. other channels.

**5. Email Triggered Sequences (Both Sides)**
- Why: Activation is the hardest problem in marketplaces. A buyer who signs up but doesn't post a project = zero revenue. A creative who joins but doesn't complete their profile = supply problem.
- Content format: Buyer: 5-email "post your first project" drip. Seller: 5-email "complete your profile and win your first client" drip. Both are value-driven, not promotional.
- Posting frequency: Fully automated, triggered by signup events.
- KPI: Buyer activation rate (first project posted in 14 days), seller activation rate (profile complete + first proposal submitted in 7 days).

## Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

1. **Manually recruit 50 elite supply-side creatives**: Reach out directly via Behance/Dribbble to designers in your target disciplines. Offer a white-glove onboarding, elevated profile badge, and first 60 days without platform fees. Supply quality drives demand quality.
2. **"Brief Builder" Tool Launch**: Build a free public tool that helps buyers write a proper creative brief. Drives SEO traffic, captures emails, and builds trust with the buyer before they post a project.
3. **Reddit AMA**: Have the founder do an AMA in r/freelance answering questions about the platform economics honestly (fee structure, dispute resolution, quality filtering). Transparent conversation builds supply-side trust faster than any ad.

## Channels to Explicitly Avoid (For Now)

- **Influencer Marketing**: No clear influencer vertical covers both sides of this marketplace effectively. ROI is hard to track.
- **TikTok / Instagram (Supply Side)**: Creative professionals on these platforms are in consumption mode, not business development mode.
- **Programmatic Display**: Low intent signals, poor creative for B2B-adjacent buyer audience. Not until monthly budget allows for retargeting-only campaigns.
