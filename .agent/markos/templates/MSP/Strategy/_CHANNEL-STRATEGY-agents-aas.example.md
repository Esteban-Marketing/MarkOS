## Channel Priority Stack

**1. Founder-Led Content on LinkedIn + Twitter/X**
- Why: AI tool adoption in marketing is driven by peer trust more than vendor marketing. When a founder authentically documents their agent platform's results, failures, and learnings, it drives more qualified pipeline than any ad. This audience follows AI builders, not AI marketers.
- Content format: "What happened when our agent ran X" result threads, honest failure posts ("Our agent made this mistake — here's the guardrail we added"), behind-the-scenes architecture threads, LinkedIn articles on AI adoption challenges.
- Posting frequency: Daily on Twitter/X (8–10 posts/week), 4–5 posts/week on LinkedIn founder + company pages.
- KPI: Follows by ICP-matching personas (CMOs, VPs Marketing, AI leads), inbound demo requests attributed to social content, share/reply engagement rate.

**2. AI Community (Discord, Slack, Twitter/X Spaces)**
- Why: VPs Marketing who are early AI adopters congregate in specific AI communities (marketing AI Slack groups, Latent Space Discord, Nathan Lands AI newsletter community). These are the early adopters who drive referrals.
- Content format: AMA sessions in marketing AI Discord servers, "How we built the agent workflow" contributor posts, Twitter/X Spaces participation on AI marketing topics.
- Posting frequency: 2–3 meaningful community interactions/week. Consistent, not spammy.
- KPI: Community-sourced demo requests, member advocacy (being recommended by community members unprompted).

**3. Email Nurture + Demo Sequence**
- Why: The B2B sales cycle for agentic AI is 3–8 weeks at the lower end. Email keeps the brand present during the evaluation period and delivers the technical information security/IT reviewers need.
- Content format: 8-email demo nurture sequence (Day 1: product overview video, Day 3: security/compliance docs, Day 5: case study with metrics, Day 7: integration list + API docs, Day 10: comparison vs. building in-house, Day 14: ROI estimator, Day 21: social proof from peer companies, Day 28: trial offer).
- Posting frequency: Triggered from demo booking.
- KPI: Demo → trial conversion rate (target >40%), email open rate by sequence step, "forward to colleague" rate.

**4. Partner Ecosystem (HubSpot, Salesforce AppExchange, Google Ads API)**
- Why: Martech platform integrations are a distribution channel. Being listed in HubSpot's App Marketplace or Salesforce's AppExchange surfaces us to buyers already in purchasing mode for tools that connect to their CRM.
- Content format: Marketplace listing with integration walkthrough video, co-marketing webinar with HubSpot partner team, joint case study featuring mutual customer.
- Posting frequency: Quarterly partner content pushes, ongoing listing optimization.
- KPI: Marketplace-sourced trials, integration page traffic, co-marketing pipeline influence.

**5. Analyst Relations + Thought Leadership Reports**
- Why: CMOs evaluating a $50K–$300K agentic AI contract will look for third-party validation. Being cited in G2, Gartner, Forrester, or in respected CMO podcasts shortens the enterprise due diligence process significantly.
- Content format: Analyst briefings, contributing data to "State of AI in Marketing" reports, podcast appearances on CMO/RevOps-focused shows.
- Posting frequency: 1–2 analyst briefings/quarter; 2–3 podcast appearances/month.
- KPI: G2 reviews (target 50+, avg 4.5+), analyst mention tracking, podcast-attributed inbound.

## Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

1. **"Live Agent Session" Demo Video**: Record a 12-minute unedited screen capture of the agent autonomously executing a real marketing workflow (research → copy → scheduling). Publish on LinkedIn with no edits except titles. Authenticity converts skeptics — polished demos don't.
2. **G2 Review Campaign**: Email your top 20 most engaged customers asking for an honest G2 review. Offer to give them early access to a new agent capability in exchange. Target: 20 reviews in 30 days to cross the G2 category visibility threshold.
3. **Marketing AI Slack Community AMA**: Reach out to the moderator of the top 2 marketing AI Slack communities and offer the CEO for a live AMA. Frame it as "What agentic marketing actually looks like in production — including what went wrong." Honesty and transparency generate the most positive response.

## Channels to Explicitly Avoid (For Now)

- **Broad Meta Paid Social**: Our buyer is not being reached through B2C-style social advertising. Waste of budget.
- **Cold Email at Volume**: AI companies sending mass automated emails actively undermine their credibility. High personalization, human-led outreach only.
- **Instagram / TikTok**: Not where our CMO/VP Marketing audience is making enterprise software decisions.
- **Google Display Remarketing** (before significant organic traffic): Too early — not enough traffic volume to make retargeting cost-effective.
