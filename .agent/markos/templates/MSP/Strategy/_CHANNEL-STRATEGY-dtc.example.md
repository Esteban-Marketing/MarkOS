## Channel Priority Stack

**1. Instagram (Organic + Paid)**
- Why: Primary discovery channel for skincare consumers. Visual-first, aspirational, and drives purchase intent through atmosphere and skin result imagery.
- Content format: Before/after reels (verified, FTC-compliant), ingredient education carousels, founder-led stories ("Why we use X instead of Y"), user-generated content reposts with permission.
- Posting frequency: 1 Reel/day, 4–5 Stories/day, 1 educational carousel/week.
- KPI: Reel reach, saves rate (high saves = high intent), Instagram-attributed revenue, ROAS on paid (target >3.5x).

**2. TikTok (Discovery + Education)**
- Why: Dermatologist-led and ingredient-education content goes viral on TikTok. Our audience uses it to research products before buying. An authentic explanation of "why we use ceramides" drives more trust than a polished ad.
- Content format: Un-edited ingredient explainers, "How I fixed X skin concern" narratives (customer-led), product texture reveals, founder Q&A.
- Posting frequency: 4–5 videos/week. Consistency over production value.
- KPI: Views per video, comment sentiment, TikTok-attributed traffic to DTC site.

**3. Email + SMS (Owned Channels)**
- Why: DTC brands that over-depend on Meta get punished by ROAS fluctuations. Owning the customer relationship through email/SMS is the margin-protector and LTV driver.
- Content format: Educational email (ingredient of the month), routine builder (quiz → personalized email sequence), post-purchase onboarding (how to use in sequence), replenishment reminders at Day 60/90.
- Posting frequency: 1 content email/week + behavioral automations (abandon cart, post-purchase, replenishment).
- KPI: Email revenue % of total (target >35%), SMS click rate >7%, list growth rate.

**4. Reddit + Community (Organic Seeding)**
- Why: r/SkincareAddiction is where purchasing decisions for skincare are authentically validated. Being organically recommended in Reddit threads converts at significantly higher rates than paid ads.
- Content format: Founder or team participates in genuine community discussions (not promotional). Seed free samples to established community contributors for honest reviews.
- Posting frequency: Ongoing community participation, not broadcast.
- KPI: Organic Reddit mentions mentioning brand, referral traffic from Reddit to site, subreddit sentiment.

**5. YouTube (Conversion + SEO)**
- Why: High-intent "best serum for X skin concern" searches drive purchase-ready traffic. YouTube videos rank in Google Image and Video search. The payoff is slow but compounding.
- Content format: Full skincare routine tutorials, product deep-dive reviews (with full ingredient commentary), dermatologist partnership videos.
- Posting frequency: 1 video/week.
- KPI: Watch time, video-attributed conversions, SEO ranking for target keywords.

## Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

1. **Reddit Sampling Program**: Identify top 10 contributors in r/SkincareAddiction who match the ICP profile. DM with a transparent outreach: "We're a small brand and would love your honest opinion — no strings attached." 1–2 honest positive threads can move needle significantly.
2. **Post-Purchase Email Sequence**: Build a 5-email onboarding sequence for new customers (day 1, 3, 7, 14, 30). Each email: one usage tip + one educational ingredient explainer. Goal: Day 30 repurchase rate >20%.
3. **TikTok Ingredient Series**: Produce 7 short videos (60 seconds each) — one per hero ingredient. Script: "Most serums use X but it causes Y — here's why we use Z instead." Batch produce in one shoot. Published over 2 weeks.

## Channels to Explicitly Avoid (For Now)

- **LinkedIn**: Our consumer does not make personal care purchasing decisions in professional mode.
- **Influencer Mega-Deals (1M+ followers)**: High CPM, low trust signal, hard to attribute. Micro-influencer (10K–100K) seeding outperforms at 1/10th the cost for skincare specifically.
- **Google Shopping**: Competitive at this stage — established brands with 10,000+ reviews dominate. Revisit when review volume is >500.
