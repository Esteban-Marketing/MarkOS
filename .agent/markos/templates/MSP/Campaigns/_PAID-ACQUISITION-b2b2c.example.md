# Reference Example: **B2B2C** (Property Management Platform)

---

## Phase Budget & Parameters (Dual Funnel)
- **B-Side CAC Limit:** $2,800 per property manager account (drives tenant volume)
- **C-Side CAC Limit:** $0 (tenants are acquired through property managers — no paid C-side acquisition)
- **B-Side CPA Bounds:** $320 MQL; $1,800 SQL
- **LTV Goal (B-Side):** $22,000 ARR per property manager account (avg 180 units, $10/unit/month, 12 months)
- **Daily Spend Threshold:** $240 B-side; $0 direct consumer

## B-Side Acquisition: Property Managers

### 1. Audience Matrix

**LinkedIn (Primary):**
- Titles: "Property Manager", "Asset Manager", "Director of Property Operations", "Portfolio Manager"
- Company Type: Real estate management firms, REITs, HOA management companies
- Company Size: 10–200 employees (independent firms); also target job title + 1–10 employee range (boutique owner-operators)
- Geographic precision: Tier 1 and Tier 2 US metros first (higher unit density = higher LTV)

**Google Search (Intent Capture):**
- "property management software for landlords"
- "tenant communication platform"
- "maintenance request software property"
- "[Competitor] alternative" — Buildium, AppFolio, Propertyware
- Negative: "commercial property management" (different product fit), "property management course"

### 2. Pipeline: LinkedIn Ads

**Sprint 1 — TOF:**
- Format: Insight-style document ad — "2025 Property Management Tech Survey: What 500 Managers Really Use"
- Angle: Benchmark report with email gate → MQL
- Budget: 60% LinkedIn
- KPI: Download rate >7%, CPL <$95

**Sprint 2 — MOF:**
- Format: Video testimonial — a named property manager talking about tenant satisfaction improvement
- Copy: "How [Manager Name] reduced maintenance response time from 4.2 days to 18 hours"
- CTA: "See how it works" → product demo page
- KPI: Demo page visits with >3 min session, demo request rate >2%

**Sprint 3 — BOF:**
- Format: InMail to demo attendees who haven't converted
- Copy: Specific to portfolio size (reference their LinkedIn profile if possible) — "For a 150-unit portfolio, here's your exact ROI calculation"
- CTA: "Start your 30-day free trial — no credit card"
- KPI: Reply rate >7%, trial activation rate >4%

### 3. Pipeline: Google Search

- **Competitor Conquest:** "$8–$14 CPC, 'Buildium alternative for growing portfolios'"
- **Problem-Intent:** "$5–$9 CPC, 'reduce tenant maintenance requests'", "automated rent collection app"
- **Branded Defense:** $20/day flat

## C-Side Strategy (Tenant App Adoption)

**No paid acquisition for tenants.** Tenants are onboarded through each property manager's activated account. The growth lever is:
1. Increase property manager activation rate (their tenants follow)
2. Improve app store rating to drive organic tenant discovery
3. In-app viral loops (tenant referral for rent credit when friend's property uses the platform)

## 4. Optimization Loop

- Week 1: Kill LinkedIn ad variants with CTR <0.35%; scale top-performing document ad format
- Week 2: Review demo-to-trial conversion rate — if <3%, landing page needs CRO work before adding budget
- Week 3: Evaluate LinkedIn vs Google CAC by channel — reallocate to lower-CAC channel at 60/40 split

## Quick Wins (First 14 Days)

1. **Build "ROI calculator" for property managers** — input: # of units; output: time saved, maintenance request reduction %. Gate with email. High MQL quality.
2. **Launch Buildium/AppFolio competitor keywords Day 1** — fastest intent signal for pipeline velocity
3. **Ask 3 customers for a 60-second video review** — property management is relationship-driven; authentic peer reference beats any ad creative

## Channels to Avoid

- Meta/Facebook for B-Side: Property managers aren't buying software during leisure browsing
- Consumer social ads for tenants: Zero ROI — tenants adopt through their landlord, not through independent discovery
- Programmatic display: B-side audience too niche for cost-effective targeting via programmatic
