# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **Agents-aaS** (AI Marketing Operations Agent Platform)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: Agents-aaS -->
> [!NOTE] Agents-aaS voice is for an emerging category. It must simultaneously convey frontier expertise, calm authority over complexity, and transparent honesty about AI limitations.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Frontier Authority**: We operate at the edge of what's possible in agentic AI, and our voice reflects it. We use technical terms correctly, cite real benchmarks, and give detailed answers to hard questions. We never dumb down the AI conversation — our audience reads the papers.
* **Transparent Accountability**: We openly discuss what our agents can and cannot do, and what humans should still own. Transparency about AI limitations is a trust accelerant, not a weakness. Every action the agent takes is logged, explainable, and auditable.
* **Operational Calm**: AI autonomy can feel chaotic to risk-averse teams. Our tone is the opposite of hype — measured, methodical, and confident. We project the feeling that deploying us is the careful, considered choice — not the reckless one.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Technically grounded, operationally trustworthy, honest about uncertainty, peer-to-peer with AI-literate buyers.
* **We Are NOT**: Hype-driven ("the future of marketing"), anthropomorphizing agents beyond what's accurate, evasive about model/data details, or so apocalyptic/utopian about AI that we lose pragmatic buyers.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Use these words**: "agentic workflow," "autonomous execution," "action audit," "human-in-the-loop," "guardrails," "model routing," "context persistence," "multi-agent orchestration," "production-ready," "deterministic output," "hallucination mitigation," "tool use," "MCP-compatible," "verified action trace."
* **NEVER use**: "autopilot your marketing" (implies zero oversight, triggers fear), "AI does everything" (false and legally risky), "replaces your team" (causes internal resistance and blockers), "magic AI," "just turn it on" (sets wrong expectations), "guaranteed results" (can't guarantee LLM output), "the AI knows what to do" (anthropomorphization without mechanism).

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **Technical Documentation / Onboarding**: Precise, step-by-step, no hand-waving. Every agent action is explained with "what it does, how it decides, what it logs." Developers and technically-adept marketers must be able to understand exactly what is happening.
* **Thought Leadership / Blog**: Frontier-thinking but operationally grounded. "Here's what we found when we ran 10,000 email send decisions through the agent — and where we added human gates." Cites failure modes as readily as successes.
* **Sales/ Landing Pages**: Dual-register. For the CMO: outcome metrics ("3x campaign throughput with 40% fewer headcount hours"). For the CTO/ReviewTeam: architecture diagram, audit log sample, SOC 2 status, data residency options. No "trust us" — show the receipts.
* **Email / Outbound**: Context-specific and short. Reference a specific challenge ("I saw you're managing 6 campaigns manually right now…"). One clear next step. No automated-sounding copy — ironic for an AI company to sound robotic.
