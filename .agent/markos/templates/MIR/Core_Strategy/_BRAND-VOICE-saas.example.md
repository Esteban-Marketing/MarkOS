# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **SaaS** (Developer Observability Platform)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: SaaS -->
> [!NOTE] SaaS voice targets technical buyers. It must demonstrate depth over polish — engineers trust precision, not hype.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Engineering-Depth**: We write for people who will read our documentation, not just our headlines. Every claim must hold up under scrutiny. We lean into technical specificity — cardinality limits, query performance benchmarks, ingestion rates. This signals credibility to our audience.
* **Honest About Trade-offs**: We acknowledge where other tools do things well. We state clearly what we are optimized for and what we're not. Engineers respect intellectual honesty and distrust anything that sounds too good to be true.
* **Engineers-First, Buyers-Second**: Our content strategy leads with value for the practitioner. The economic buyer comes in after the engineering team is convinced. We do not lead with "ROI" — we lead with "how it works."

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Technically precise, intellectually honest, peer-level, efficient, benchmark-driven.
* **We Are NOT**: Hype-driven, abstract, corporate ("leverage synergies"), condescending to non-technical readers, or so technical that the VP Engineering can't understand a case study.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Use these words**: "observability," "MTTR," "distributed tracing," "cardinality," "SLO/SLI," "alert fatigue," "correlation," "root cause," "OpenTelemetry-native," "real-time," "query performance," "incident context," "on-call rotation," "no lock-in," "open-source compatible."
* **NEVER use**: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "AI-powered" (without specifics), "magic," "simple" (arrogant to engineers who know nothing is simple), "enterprise-grade" (vague), "best-in-class" (unprovable and cliché), "seamless" (engineers know integrations are never seamless).

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **Technical Blog / Docs**: Dense, accurate, show-your-work style. Links to GitHub. Code examples. Benchmark methodology disclosed. This is the primary trust-building surface — treat it as the most important marketing channel.
* **LinkedIn / Social**: Shorter-form insight. "Here's what we learned after analyzing 10 million alerts across 200 production environments." Data-led, opinionated, peer-to-peer. Engineers will share content that makes them look smart.
* **Product / Landing Pages**: Dual-register. Above the fold: outcome for the buyer ("Reduce MTTR by 40% — with proof."). Below the fold: technical depth for the evaluator (architecture diagrams, integration list, pricing calculator).
* **Outbound Email (SDR-Assisted)**: Short, specific, referenced. "I saw you're using Grafana — we integrate natively and add X that Grafana doesn't do." Specific over generic at all times.
