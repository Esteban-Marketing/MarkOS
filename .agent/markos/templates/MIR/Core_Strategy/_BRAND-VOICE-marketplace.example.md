# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **Marketplace** (Freelance Creative Platform)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: Marketplace -->
> [!NOTE] Marketplace voice maintains a trust-building neutral register across both sides. It must feel equally credible to the creative selling and the business buying.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Impartial Connector**: We are the platform — not a recruiter, not an agency. We create the conditions for great work to happen between the right people. Our voice is neutral enough that both sides feel represented.
* **Quality-Signal**: Everything in our communication signals that low-quality doesn't survive here. Language is chosen carefully, not dashed off. This gives both sides confidence in the marketplace standard.
* **Transparent**: Pricing, fees, and processes are communicated without fine print surprises. Transparency builds both-side trust and is a direct competitive moat against platforms known for opaque payouts.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Clear, fair, confident, professional, encouraging of craft, specific.
* **We Are NOT**: Overhyped ("explosive growth!"), patronizing to either side, jargon-heavy, biased toward buyers or sellers in our marketing.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Supply Side Vocabulary — Use**: "your portfolio," "your rate," "your terms," "vetted clients," "quality projects," "creative brief," "protect your work," "on-time payment guaranteed."
* **Demand Side Vocabulary — Use**: "vetted creatives," "matched to your project," "on-brief delivery," "revision rounds included," "protected payment," "brief-to-delivery," "your brand standards."
* **NEVER use**: "cheap creative," "affordable talent," "low-cost" (devalues supply side), "guaranteed results in X days" (overpromise), "unlimited revisions" (operationally untenable), "world-class" (generic).

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **Supply Side Acquisition (Social/Communities)**: Peer-to-peer and respectful of craft. "Built for freelancers who are serious about finding the right clients — not just any clients." Focus on quality of buyers and protection of their rates.
* **Demand Side Acquisition (LinkedIn/Search)**: Efficiency and reliability narrative. "Find a vetted graphic designer who gets your brand. Brief-to-delivery in 5 days." Lead with speed and quality assurance, not price.
* **Platform Emails (Both Sides)**: Clear action-prompts, minimal friction language. Project update emails are status-of-fact, not marketing copy.
* **Support Responses**: Impartial, swift, and solution-oriented. Never take "sides" in a dispute before hearing both. Escalation language is neutral, not defensive.
