# ICPs.md — Ideal Customer Profiles (Firmographics)
# Reference Example: **Agents-aaS** (AI Marketing Operations Agent Platform)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: Agents-aaS -->
> [!NOTE] Agents-aaS ICP spans both a firmographic profile AND a stakeholder readiness assessment — the buyer must be organizationally ready for autonomous agent deployment.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile Overview

The ideal customer is an **AI-forward B2B marketing organization** that has already experimented with LLM tooling (ChatGPT, Jasper, etc.) and hit the ceiling of what prompt-based tools can accomplish. They are not evaluating AI — they have adopted it. They are now evaluating which *agentic* approach to bet on: build internally vs. deploy a production-ready platform.

---

## 2. ICP Attributes

| Attribute | Ideal Value | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Industry | B2B SaaS, Fintech, Consulting, Professional Services | Any B2B company with multi-channel marketing motion |
| Company Size | 75 – 1,500 employees | 20 – 5,000 employees |
| Annual Revenue | $5M – $200M | $2M – $1B |
| Marketing Team Size | 5 – 50 marketers | 3+ dedicated marketing staff |
| AI Maturity | "Experimenter+" — using LLM tools weekly, has an internal AI task force or "AI champion" | Any team with 50%+ AI tool adoption |
| Tech Stack Readiness | CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce), Email ESP, Paid Ads accounts with API access | Any martech stack with documented API access |
| Annual Contract Value | $30K – $300K | $12K – $1M+ |
| Sales Cycle | 3 – 8 weeks (fast) to 3 – 6 months (enterprise) | 2 weeks – 12 months |
| Decision Maker | CMO, VP Marketing, or Head of Marketing Ops (champion); CTO for technical gate | Any exec with marketing budget authority |

---

## 3. Organizational Readiness Criteria

The customer must meet at least 3 of these 5 readiness signals to be a high-probability close:
1. **Has an "AI champion"** — at least one person whose job description or OKRs include AI adoption.
2. **Has martech API access** — can grant the agent platform credentials to CRM and email systems.
3. **Has complained about LLM limitations** — specifically about context loss, no memory, or inability to take actions.
4. **Has an upcoming high-volume initiative** — product launch, event series, or campaign sprint that agents can demonstrably accelerate.
5. **Has a clear ROI metric** — knows what "success" looks like (MTTR for campaigns, leads generated with X% less headcount, etc.).

---

## 4. Disqualifiers

* Companies with no existing LLM/AI tool usage — change management overhead is too high; education-first, sell later.
* Companies where IT prohibits API integration with any third-party AI vendor — requires custom enterprise procurement process.
* Companies where the CMO is skeptical of AI autonomy — champion-less deals stall; wait for leadership transition.
* Companies in heavily regulated industries (healthcare, financial advice, legal) without a compliance review process in place — legal risk to both parties.
* Companies expecting the platform to replace their entire marketing team on day one — expectation mismatch creates churn at 90 days.
