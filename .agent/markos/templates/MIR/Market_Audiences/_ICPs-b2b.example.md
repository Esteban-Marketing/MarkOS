# Reference Example: **B2B** (HR Technology — Applicant Tracking & Onboarding)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: B2B -->
> [!NOTE] This is a completed B2B ICP example. The ICP section is firmographic — it defines the ideal company, not the individual.

---

## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Overview

The ideal client for this business is a **mid-market US-based company** that has outgrown spreadsheet-based recruitment but is not ready for a full enterprise HRIS suite. They are in a rapid growth phase (20%+ YoY headcount increase), have a dedicated HR or People Ops function, and have experienced at least one visible hiring failure or compliance incident in the past 18 months that has motivated change.

---

## 2. ICP Attributes

| Attribute | Ideal Value | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Industry | Technology, Healthcare, Professional Services | Any industry with professional hiring (exclude retail/hospitality hourly) |
| Annual Revenue | $10M – $150M | $5M – $300M |
| Headcount | 100 – 1,500 employees | 50 – 5,000 employees |
| Geography | United States (English-language) | US, Canada, UK |
| Technology Stack | BambooHR, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday (integration-ready) | Any HCM with API access |
| Maturity Level | Series B / Series C / Growth-stage private | Post-seed through pre-IPO |
| HR Team Size | 3 – 15 dedicated HR/People Ops staff | 2+ full-time HR staff |
| Active Hiring Volume | 20 – 200 open roles per quarter | 10+ open roles quarterly |
| Budget Cycle | Annual SaaS budget approved, Jan or July | Quarterly budget flexibility |

---

## 3. Disqualifiers (Do Not Pursue)

* Fewer than 50 employees — not enough hiring volume to justify the platform cost.
* Hourly/shift labor as primary workforce (restaurants, retail, logistics) — different workflow entirely.
* Companies with a recently signed, multi-year enterprise HRIS contract (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors) — switching cost is too high.
* Public companies with procurement cycles exceeding 12 months — sales cycle economics don't work at current ACV.
* IT departments that prohibit new SaaS vendors without FedRAMP certification (unless we pursue FedRAMP).
