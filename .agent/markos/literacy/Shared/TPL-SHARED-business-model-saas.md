# SaaS Tone and Naturality Guidance

The `TPL-SHARED-overlay-saas.md` doc establishes the core SaaS tone posture: operator-like, efficient, product-literate, and trust-building through product-led proof. This document extends it with funnel-stage detail for self-serve and mid-market SaaS where trial conversion is the primary growth mechanic.

**Key SaaS distinction:** The product IS the proof. Buyers self-qualify through hands-on product experience. Marketing's job is to maximize trial quality and reduce activation friction — not to push a traditional sales narrative.

## EVIDENCE BASE
- Product-Led Growth Collective: SaaS companies with trial/freemium experiences have 45% shorter average sales cycles than demo-only models. The first 24 hours in a trial determine 60%+ of eventual conversion outcomes.
- G2 SaaS Buying Study: 65% of B2B SaaS buyers check peer review sites (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot) before requesting a demo. Review recency and response rate are weighted review quality signals.

## CORE TACTICS

### Awareness
Surface the problem the product solves — not the product. Target category-level search intent ("best tool for X", "how to automate Y") and LinkedIn thought leadership on the problem, not the solution. Content that helps potential buyers understand the category earns trust before the trial.

### Consideration
This is where product-led proof does its heaviest lifting. Comparison pages, transparent pricing pages, integration pages, and "how it works" walkthroughs reduce the buyer's perceived risk. G2/review excerpts here build social trust. Free trial or freemium CTA should be visible and frictionless — no sales call required to start.

### Decision
Remove friction from the trial start. No credit card, clear data security posture, testimonials from similar company profiles, and integration proof for the buyer's existing stack. The trial CTA should emphasize time-to-first-value ("See your first [outcome] in 5 minutes"), not feature depth.

### Onboarding
The first 24–72 hours are the most important in the SaaS lifecycle. Activation messaging should be relentlessly focused on the first win — one specific, achievable action that gives the user a taste of the product's core value. Avoid overwhelming new users with the entire feature set.

### Retention
Expansion is earned through usage depth and proactive success signals, not upsell emails. Identify the behaviors that correlate with long-term retention and ensure those actions are celebrated and reinforced. QBR-style communication for higher-tier accounts; usage milestone emails for self-serve.

## COUNTER-INDICATORS
- Feature-list-heavy content before the buyer has experienced the product (feature fatigue).
- Requiring a credit card or sales call before trial access.
- Onboarding that presents 10 features on day 1 instead of one activation win.
- Expansion emails that feel like new-customer acquisition campaigns — they erode trust in the vendor relationship.
