# Ecommerce Business Model

This tone doc extends `TPL-SHARED-overlay-ecommerce.md` with funnel-stage tactical breakdown for ecommerce brands. Ecommerce is distinct from general B2C (brand relationship) — the primary metric is transaction: conversion rate, AOV, cart abandonment recovery, LTV, and ROAS. Write for purchase intent, not aspiration.

## EVIDENCE BASE

The ecommerce purchase funnel is defined by transaction mechanics that don't apply to other B2C models: cart abandonment (industry average 70–75%), dynamic retargeting by product SKU, post-purchase email as the highest-converting channel in the stack, and review volume as primary trust proxy. B2C brand relationship models focus on lifestyle resonance; ecommerce focuses on "will this product do what I need, arrive fast, and be returnable if it doesn't."

Buyer psychology shifts rapidly from discovery to purchase-mode and back. The decision window is short. Trust must be established in the product detail page, not through long brand storytelling.

## CORE TACTICS

### Awareness
- **Discovery via product visual** — social and paid content with the product as the hero, in-use context
- **Format-specific hooks** — Meta/IG Reels: pattern-interrupt + product in first 2 seconds; Google Shopping: feed copy that front-loads key differentiators
- **Category-level education sparingly** — "Here's what to look for in [category]" works when generic competition is high and product quality is the differentiator

### Consideration
- **Review-led social proof** — star ratings, review counts, specific buyer outcomes ("I was skeptical, here's what changed")
- **Comparison framing** — direct comparison against category alternatives when the brand can win on reviews, price-to-quality, or specificity
- **Objection pre-emption** — Size guide, returns policy, shipping speeds directly on product pages and in retargeting creative

### Decision
- **Credible urgency** — "3 left in stock" (if real), "sale ends [date]" (if real), "X people are viewing this right now" (if real)
- **Abandoned cart recovery** — Email sequence: Cart reminder (1hr) → product proof/reviews (24hr) → soft incentive if used (72hr)
- **Checkout friction removal** — Trust badges, PayPal/Apple Pay presence, single-page checkout framing in copy

### Post-Purchase / Onboarding
- **Order confirmation delight** — Reinforce the purchase decision with product tips, what to expect
- **Unboxing / first-use content** — Email or SMS with setup guide, styling tips, or product care — creates peak satisfaction for reviews
- **Review request** — At the right window (product delivered + 48–72hr): specific, not generic ask

### Retention
- **LTV sequences** — Repurchase reminders timed to product lifecycle (consumables, seasonal, wear patterns)
- **Loyalty anchoring** — Points, early access, "you + this brand" identity framing for repeat buyers
- **Win-back** — 90-day lapsed: new product drop, "you haven't been back" with specific product recommendation

## COUNTER-INDICATORS

- Fake urgency ("Don't miss out!") without a real supply or time constraint
- Long brand story introductions when the buyer is in decision mode
- Generic "Shop now" CTAs with no specific product or category anchoring
- Over-explaining product features instead of outcomes and fit indicators ("Does this work for [use case]? Yes, here's why")
- Trust signals that reassure the wrong buyer (B2B-style credentials for consumer products)
