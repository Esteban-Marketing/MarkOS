## Channel Priority Stack

**1. Instagram (Organic + Paid)**
- Why: Primary discovery and aspiration channel for 30–45 fitness audience. They follow fitness influencers, home gym accounts, and wellness brands here. Visual format matches our product.
- Content format: High-production lifestyle reels (morning workout ambiance), product-in-use videos, before/after customer stories (with consent), carousel "workout of the week."
- Posting frequency: 1 Reel/day, 3–4 Stories/day, 1 carousel/week.
- KPI: Reel reach, DM inquiry volume, profile-to-website click rate, ROAS on paid (target >3x).

**2. Meta Paid Social (Facebook + Instagram Ads)**
- Why: Highest-volume performance channel for B2C physical product. Lookalike audiences from email list + customer LTV data outperform cold interest targeting.
- Content format: 15–30 second product-in-home videos, social proof carousels (5-star reviews + real customer photos), limited-time offer creative (seasonal drops).
- Posting frequency: Always-on campaigns, creative refresh every 2–3 weeks.
- KPI: ROAS >3.5x, CPA below $X (set from COGS + margin target), add-to-cart rate.

**3. Email / SMS (Owned Channels)**
- Why: Highest-ROI channel for existing customers and warm leads. LTV expansion happens through email — replenishment prompts, accessories cross-sell, and community content.
- Content format: Weekly value email (workout plan, recovery tip, featured community member), cart abandonment sequence (3-email), post-purchase onboarding sequence.
- Posting frequency: 1 content email/week + transactional automations.
- KPI: Revenue attributed to email (target >30% of total revenue), open rate >35%, SMS click rate >8%.

**4. TikTok (Discovery)**
- Why: Growing segment of our secondary audience (25–35) is discovering fitness products through TikTok. High organic reach for authentic, non-polished content performs well.
- Content format: "Day in my life" style home workouts, product unboxing and honest reviews, "This vs. that" equipment comparisons. UGC-style over production value.
- Posting frequency: 3–4 videos/week.
- KPI: Views, follower growth, TikTok Shop conversion rate (if applicable).

**5. YouTube (Education + Long-Tail SEO)**
- Why: Fitness buyers research extensively before major purchases. A 10-minute honest review video outperforms a product page for high-intent shoppers comparing options.
- Content format: Full home workout programs (30-day challenge), product deep dives, expert interviews (trainers, physiotherapists).
- Posting frequency: 1 video/week.
- KPI: Watch time, subscriber growth, YouTube-attributed conversions.

## Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

1. **Gift 5 units to micro-influencers** (10K–100K followers) in the home gym/wellness space. Brief them with 3 talking points — let them create authentic content. Measure story views + link clicks to PDP.
2. **Launch cart abandonment email sequence**: 3 emails over 5 days. Email 1: reminder. Email 2: social proof (customer photos). Email 3: 10% offer with expiry. Target >15% recovery rate.
3. **Publish one hero YouTube video**: "I replaced my $200/month gym membership with one piece of equipment — honest 30-day review." Optimize title and description for "home gym" search terms.

## Channels to Explicitly Avoid (For Now)

- **LinkedIn**: Our customer is not making personal purchase decisions during their professional browsing mode.
- **Twitter/X**: No meaningful fitness product discovery or purchase intent signal for our audience segment on this platform.
- **Google Search Ads (Brand terms only)**: Bid defense only — brand campaign is defensive, not growth. Non-brand search for "home gym equipment" has extremely high CPCs and broad competition; not cost-effective at this stage.
