# ICPs.md — Ideal Customer Profiles
# Reference Example: **B2B2C** (Property Management Platform)

<!-- mgsd-token: MIR | model: B2B2C -->
> [!NOTE] B2B2C requires dual ICP definition: one for the B (business buyer) and one for the C (end consumer profile that influences retention/renewal).

---

## 1. B Side ICP Overview (Primary Buyer — Property Manager)

The ideal business client is a **regional or national property management firm** with 500–10,000 units under management, at least one dedicated operations/technology contact, and a recent track record of adopting SaaS for property management. They are typically evaluating the switch because their current solution (often legacy) can't consolidate maintenance, communications, and payments in a single interface.

## 2. B Side ICP Attributes

| Attribute | Ideal Value | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Portfolio Size | 500 – 10,000 units | 200 – 50,000 units |
| Property Types | Multifamily residential | Mixed-use, student housing, HOA |
| Geography | US Sun Belt metros (TX, FL, AZ, GA) | Any US metro with active rental market |
| Annual Revenue | $2M – $50M | $500K – $200M |
| Current Tech Stack | AppFolio, Buildium, Yardi (open to switching) | Any property management software |
| Decision Maker | VP Operations, Director of Technology, or Owner/Principal | Any stakeholder with software budget authority |
| Annual Contract Value | $12K – $120K | $6K – $500K |
| Sales Cycle | 3 – 6 months | 1 – 12 months |

---

## 3. C Side Profile (End Consumer — Tenant)

The end consumer segments that are most commercially valuable to acquire through the B side relationship:

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Age | 22 – 45 (primary renter cohort) |
| Income | $40K – $120K household |
| Lifestyle | Urban professional, young family, digital native |
| Tech Adoption | High — expects app-based rent payment, digital maintenance requests |
| Retention Driver | Fast maintenance response, transparent communication, frictionless payment |
| NPS Impact | High NPS tenants → lower vacancy → B side renewal |

---

## 4. Disqualifiers

* Single-family rental operators with fewer than 50 units — support cost exceeds ACV.
* Commercial property management (office, retail) — completely different workflow.
* Property managers in markets with heavy rent control regulation who cannot implement dynamic pricing features.
* Operators with no internet-capable tenant base (rural, senior housing without tech adoption).
