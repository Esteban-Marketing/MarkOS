# ICPs.md — Ideal Customer Profiles (Consumer Persona)
# Reference Example: **DTC** (Premium Skincare Brand)

<!-- mgsd-token: MIR | model: DTC -->
> [!NOTE] DTC ICPs are demographic + psychographic + behavioral — no firmographic fields apply.

---

## 1. Ideal Consumer Profile Overview

The ideal customer is a **skincare-educated consumer** who has moved beyond basic moisturizers and is actively building a results-driven routine. She reads ingredient labels, follows dermatologists on social, and is willing to pay $80–$200 for a single product if the clinical evidence backs it up. She is not brand loyal — she is *results loyal* — and will switch to any brand that demonstrably outperforms her current routine.

---

## 2. Consumer Profile Attributes

| Dimension | Ideal Value | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Age | 28 – 42 | 22 – 55 |
| Gender | Female-identifying (primary); expanding via inclusive positioning | All genders |
| Household Income | $80K+ | $60K+ |
| Location | US coastal metros + major suburban markets | US, Canada, UK, Australia |
| Skincare Engagement | Active routine (5+ steps), ingredient-aware, Reddit/TikTok skincare communities | Any established skincare habit |
| Spend on Skincare | $300–$2,000/yr | $100+ per year on skincare |
| Skin Concerns | Hyperpigmentation, fine lines, large pores, acne-prone | Any specific clinical skin concern |
| Content Consumption | Dermatologist-led TikTok, SkincareAddiction Reddit, long-form YouTube reviews | Any research-oriented skincare media |
| Purchase Channel | DTC website (primary), Sephora/Ulta (consideration), Amazon (not ideal) | Direct and specialty retail only — avoid mass market |
| LTV Potential | 3+ repurchases per year (serum + moisturizer subscription) | Any repeat category |

---

## 3. Disqualifiers

* Consumers primarily seeking drugstore-equivalent price points — they are not the core market and heavy discounting would erode brand equity.
* One-time gift buyers with no intent to build a routine — low LTV.
* Consumers with highly reactive or medically-managed skin seeking prescription-grade treatment — refer to dermatologist, do not overpromise.
* Markets without reliable cold-chain or expedited shipping capabilities for temperature-sensitive formulations.
