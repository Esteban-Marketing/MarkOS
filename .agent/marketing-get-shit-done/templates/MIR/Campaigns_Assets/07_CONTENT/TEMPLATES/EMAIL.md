## Lead Nurture Email — Structural Template

```
FROM: [from_name] <[from_email]>
SUBJECT LINE: [5–8 words — specificity > cleverness]
PREVIEW TEXT: [40–90 chars — complements, does not repeat, subject line]

---

BODY:

[OPENING — 1 sentence]
[Reference their context or a problem they have. No preamble.]

[PROBLEM / INSIGHT — 2–3 sentences]
[Expand the problem. Give it weight. Use specifics.]

[BRIDGE — 1–2 sentences]
[Connect the problem to the solution mechanism. Not a hard sell.]

[PROOF / CREDIBILITY — 1–3 sentences, optional]
[One specific proof point. Client result or insight.]

[CTA — 1 sentence + button]
[Single action. Specific. No "let me know if you're interested."]
[Button text: 5 words max]

[SIGN-OFF]
[Name]
[Title]
[Company]

P.S. [Optional — reinforce CTA or add secondary proof]
```

## Welcome Email (Post Lead Capture) — Structure

```
SUBJECT: [Confirm the action + set expectation]
PREVIEW: [What happens next in concrete terms]

BODY:
- Paragraph 1: Confirm receipt + restate what they're getting
- Paragraph 2: Set expectation — when/how they'll hear from you
- Paragraph 3: One quick win they can use now (optional)
- CTA: Secondary — something to do while they wait

SIGN-OFF: Personal. From {{LEAD_AGENT}} by name.
```

---

## Re-engagement Email — Structure

```
SUBJECT: [Short. Direct. Slightly personal.]

BODY:
- 1 sentence: Acknowledge they've been quiet
- 2–3 sentences: Something new, relevant, or useful to share
- 1 sentence: Low-friction re-engagement CTA
- No pressure. No guilt-trip.
```

---

## Email Rules (From VOICE-TONE.md)

- Subject lines: sentence case, no clickbait
- One CTA per email
- Max 300 words for nurture emails
- Never use: "just checking in", "touching base", "circling back"
- P.S. line must be used purposefully or not at all
- Unsubscribe link: always present, never hidden
