# EU AI Act for Marketers

> First comprehensive AI regulation globally. Adopted 2024, phased enforcement Feb 2025 → Aug 2027. Extraterritorial: applies to any AI system affecting EU persons. Marketers touch it via content generation, personalization, profiling, chatbots, emotion detection, biometric targeting.

## Risk tiers

| Tier | Definition | Marketing relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Unacceptable** (banned) | social scoring, manipulative subliminal techniques, untargeted facial scraping, emotion recognition in work/education, predictive policing | bans some dark-pattern + biometric targeting tactics |
| **High-risk** | credit decisions, hiring, law enforcement, critical infra | rare in marketing except credit + insurance ads |
| **Limited risk** | chatbots, emotion/biometric categorization, deepfakes, AI-generated content | **most marketing AI** — transparency obligations apply |
| **Minimal risk** | everything else | voluntary codes of conduct |

## Marketer-specific obligations

1. **Deepfake / synthetic-media labelling** — AI-generated or substantially-altered images, video, audio must be clearly labelled (Article 50).
2. **Chatbot disclosure** — users must know they're interacting with AI.
3. **Biometric categorization disclosure** — any emotion/sentiment targeting discloses up front.
4. **General-purpose AI (GPAI) transparency** — providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, Mistral) must publish training-data summaries + respect TDM opt-outs.
5. **Manipulation bans** — techniques that materially distort behaviour to harm are prohibited.
6. **Copyright / provenance** — respect `TDMRep`, `ai.txt`, robots.txt for training-data scraping.

## Enforcement timeline

- **2 Feb 2025** — unacceptable-risk practices ban + AI literacy obligations.
- **2 Aug 2025** — GPAI provider obligations; national regulators designated.
- **2 Aug 2026** — high-risk + limited-risk transparency (deepfakes, chatbots) fully in force.
- **2 Aug 2027** — high-risk systems in regulated products (cars, medical devices).

## Penalties

Up to €35M or 7% global revenue (higher than GDPR).

## Checklist for marketing orgs

- [ ] Label AI-generated creative in the asset and in downstream platforms (Meta, TikTok, Google already require similar).
- [ ] Chatbot disclosure on any AI-powered support / sales agent.
- [ ] Content provenance metadata (C2PA, IPTC) in produced assets.
- [ ] Review emotion-detection or sentiment-based targeting use cases; disclose or remove.
- [ ] Vendor due-diligence on GPAI providers — request transparency summaries + TDM compliance.
- [ ] AI literacy training for staff who deploy AI (Article 4).
- [ ] DPIA-adjacent AI risk assessment in internal governance.

## Interaction with other laws

- **GDPR** — AI Act complements; GDPR still governs personal data processing.
- **DSA / DMA** — dark-pattern rules overlap with AI Act manipulation bans.
- **UK** — pro-innovation approach; no equivalent act yet in 2026.
- **US** — Colorado AI Act (2026), NYC Bias Audit (employment), state patchwork.
