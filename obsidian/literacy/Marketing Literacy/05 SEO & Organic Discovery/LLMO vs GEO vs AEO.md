# LLMO vs GEO vs AEO

> Three + acronyms pointing at roughly the same 2026 practice: getting content into AI-generated answers. They differ in emphasis, not in underlying tactics.

## Matrix

| Acronym | Full name | Emphasis | Origin / usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SEO** | Search Engine Optimization | rank in classical SERP | 1990s → |
| **AEO** | Answer Engine Optimization | show up in answer boxes / featured snippets | mid-2010s (Bing / Google answers) |
| **GEO** | Generative Engine Optimization | cited by generative answer engines | 2023 academic paper; mainstream in 2024–2026 |
| **LLMO** | LLM Optimization | cited / embedded by any LLM, incl. in-app | 2024–2026 |
| **AIO** / **SGE** | AI Overviews / Search Generative Experience | Google-specific AI-answer placement | Google launch vocabulary |
| **CEO** | Conversational Experience Optimization | voice assistant + chat surfaces | minor, platform-specific |

## How they actually overlap

Tactics are largely shared:

- Declarative, quote-ready prose.
- Entity-dense, schema-rich markup.
- E-E-A-T and source authority.
- Off-site presence (Wikipedia, analyst, community).
- Crawlability for AI bots (`GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `PerplexityBot`, `Google-Extended`, `OAI-SearchBot`, `CCBot`).
- Fresh content, versioned and dated.
- Multimodal assets (alt-rich images, tables, diagrams).

## When to pick which term

- **GEO** — cleanest umbrella term when writing strategy docs. Most widely used in 2026.
- **LLMO** — when the surface is in-product (LLM-powered app search, internal enterprise assistants) rather than public answer engines.
- **AEO** — when the surface is a "classic" answer box (PAA, featured snippet). Legacy but still relevant.
- **SGE / AIO** — only when referring to Google's AI Overviews specifically.
- **SEO** — still the right term for the *overall* organic discipline. GEO sits inside it.

## Don't bikeshed

Internal arguments about naming have zero SEO/GEO value. Pick the term your team will use consistently, document it, move on.
