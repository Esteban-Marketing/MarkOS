# Revenue CRM and Customer Intelligence Integration Report

**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Scope:** Integrate or revive the Revenue CRM and Customer Intelligence milestone inside the existing MarkOS architecture.

## 1. Integration points

### A. Onboarding and approved knowledge
- Reuse the existing onboarding intake and approval flow as the CRM bootstrap entry.
- Map approved onboarding outputs into tenant CRM defaults: lead source taxonomy, default pipeline, lifecycle stages, ICP tags, account health rules, and owner routing.
- Keep approved internal artifacts as the safe grounding layer for customer intelligence and copilot recommendations.

### B. Tenant-aware runtime and auth
- Keep CRM fully behind the existing tenant-scoped runtime context.
- Reuse the current auth and entitlement checks so every CRM read, mutation, report, and automation remains fail-closed when tenant context is missing or ambiguous.

### C. Tracking and identity stitching
- Feed first-party tracking events into the CRM activity ledger rather than creating a parallel analytics silo.
- Use the identity-link model to stitch anonymous sessions, form events, tracked redirects, and authenticated actions into a customer timeline.

### D. CRM workspace and API surface
- Reuse the canonical CRM contracts, record kinds, workspace view definitions, and reporting endpoints already present in the repo.
- Position CRM records as the operational system of record while telemetry remains the behavioral evidence layer.

### E. Governance and verification
- Keep approval-gated actions for outbound, copilot recommendations, merge decisions, and reporting drill-down.
- Write every material action back into timeline and evidence surfaces so closeout stays auditable.

## 2. New components or modules likely needed

Most foundation pieces already exist historically. The next milestone should focus on thin integration layers and extensions:

1. **Onboarding-to-CRM bootstrap mapper**
   - Converts onboarding seed plus approved drafts into CRM setup objects.

2. **Customer intelligence profile service**
   - Builds a unified tenant-scoped profile from CRM state, tracking signals, approved knowledge, and outbound history.

3. **Revenue health and lifecycle rules module**
   - Adds MRR or ARR, renewal risk, expansion signals, health scores, and success play triggers.

4. **Identity review queue**
   - Gives operators a governed place to accept, review, or reject ambiguous identity links.

5. **External sync adapters, optional**
   - Stripe, billing, email, calendar, or third-party CRM ingestion if needed later.
   - Recommend native-first before adding broad sync complexity.

6. **Milestone reporting pack**
   - Rolls readiness, attribution, SLA risk, and executive summaries into one closeout bundle.

## 3. Recommended build order

Follow the same proven dependency order already reflected in the historical v3.3 milestone:

1. **Canonical schema and identity graph**
   - CRM entities, custom fields, activity ledger, tenant-safe contracts.

2. **Behavioral tracking and identity stitching**
   - First-party ingest, redirect attribution, anonymous-to-known linking.

3. **Pipeline engine and multi-view workspace**
   - Kanban, table, detail, timeline, calendar, funnel, protected API routes.

4. **Sales and success execution workspace**
   - Queues, next-best-action ranking, notes, tasks, operator workflow.

5. **Native outbound execution**
   - Email or SMS or WhatsApp sends, consent handling, conversation writeback.

6. **AI copilot and governed playbooks**
   - Grounded summaries, recommendations, approval-required mutations, replay safety.

7. **Attribution, reporting, and verification closeout**
   - Readiness reports, executive dashboard, cross-tenant rollups, evidence capture.

## 4. Data flow considerations

### Source-of-truth model
- CRM records should remain the durable operational source of truth.
- Tracking, research, and outbound systems should enrich CRM state, not replace it.

### Tenant isolation
- Every entity, activity, identity link, outbound artifact, and report must carry tenant scope.
- Reject writes when tenant identity is absent or conflicts with verified auth claims.

### Signal quality
- Only high-signal authenticated events should appear in CRM-visible timelines.
- Keep low-value UI noise out of customer intelligence summaries.

### Identity confidence
- Treat identity stitching as confidence-scored and approval-aware.
- Auto-accept only high-confidence matches; route ambiguous matches to review.

### Governance and lineage
- Every mutation should emit source references, actor metadata, timestamps, and evidence payloads.
- Central reporting and deep drill-down should require explicit operator authorization.

### AI safety
- Copilot actions should package recommendations and proposed changes, not silently mutate records.
- Durable side effects should stay approval-gated and replay-safe.

## Bottom line

MarkOS already has the right seams for this milestone: onboarding intake, approved knowledge, tenant-scoped auth, first-party tracking, CRM contracts, copilot packaging, and reporting verification. The safest path is to reactivate the historical CRM layering pattern and add a thin customer-intelligence profile service on top, rather than inventing a separate parallel subsystem.
