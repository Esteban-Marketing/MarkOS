## Channel Priority Stack

**Summary:**
Ranks and explains the most effective channels for B2B2C property management, with rationale, content format, frequency, and KPIs for each.

**1. Industry Events + Trade Associations (B Side)**
- Why: Property management decision-makers are reached most efficiently in person. NAA (National Apartment Association) and NMHC conferences are where $100K+ software decisions are made.
- Content format: Speaking sessions, demo booths, sponsored cocktail reception, printed case study leave-behinds.
- Posting frequency: 4–6 major events/year.
- KPI: Meetings booked at event, SQLs from event-sourced leads, deal velocity vs. non-event leads.

**2. LinkedIn Thought Leadership (B Side)**
- Why: VPs of Operations and Property Management Owners use LinkedIn for vendor research and peer validation. Organic content from the CEO builds brand credibility before the first sales conversation.
- Content format: Data-driven posts ("What we learned from 500 maintenance request workflows"), customer video case studies, market trend commentary.
- Posting frequency: 3–4 posts/week (executive personal + company page).
- KPI: Connection request acceptance from ICP personas, demo requests attributed to LinkedIn content.

**3. App Store + Google Play (C Side — Tenant Acquisition)**
- Why: Tenants discover and rate the app independently of the property manager relationship. A 4.5+ star rating on both stores is a competitive differentiator in renewal conversations.
- Content format: App store optimization (keyword-rich description), review response program, feature highlight screenshots.
- Posting frequency: Ongoing — monthly review response cadence, quarterly listing update.
- KPI: App store rating (target 4.5+), organic download rate, review response rate >90%.

**4. Email (B Side — Nurture + Retention)**
- Why: Long B2B sales cycle (3–6 months) requires sustained nurture. Email is the most cost-effective channel for keeping prospects warm between sales touchpoints.
- Content format: Monthly property management industry newsletter, case study spotlight, product update announcements.
- Posting frequency: 2x/month to prospects, 1x/month to customers.
- KPI: Email-sourced demos, open rate >30%, customer newsletter retention.

**5. Push Notifications + In-App Messaging (C Side — Engagement)**
- Why: Tenant daily active usage is a leading indicator of property manager renewal. In-app notifications drive maintenance request completions, rent payment on-time rates, and NPS.
- Content format: Maintenance status updates, rent reminders (friendly tone), community announcements, renewal prompts.
- Posting frequency: Triggered (event-based), not broadcast.
- KPI: Day 30 / Day 90 retention rates, monthly active tenants / total tenants ratio, NPS score.


## Quick Wins (First 30 Days)

**Summary:**
Tactical actions to drive early traction and credibility for a B2B2C property management launch.

1. **NAA/NMHC Outreach**: Identify 3 upcoming regional property management events. Submit a speaking proposal for the CEO on "Technology adoption in property management: what actually works." No brand pitch — pure practitioner content.
2. **App Store Review Request Campaign**: Send in-app prompt to tenants who have logged in 3+ times asking for a store review. Target: 50 new reviews in 30 days to move rating above 4.4.
3. **LinkedIn Case Study Series**: With one existing customer's permission, document their journey from pre-platform chaos to post-platform metrics in a 3-part LinkedIn article sequence. Share on executive account.
