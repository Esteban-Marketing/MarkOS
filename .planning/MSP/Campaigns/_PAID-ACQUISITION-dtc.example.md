# Reference Example: **DTC** (Premium Skincare Brand)

## Phase Budget & Parameters
- **Target CAC Limits:** $42 blended across all paid
- **Target CPA Bounds:** $32 purchase (Meta); $6 email opt-in (for email→purchase nurture path)
- **LTV Goal:** $310 (4 purchases/18 months, avg $78/order)
- **Daily Spend Threshold:** $145; scale 15% every 72h if ROAS >3.5x

## 1. Audience Matrix

**Cold Audiences (Meta):**
- Interest layers: Skincare, Sephora, Glow Recipe, The Ordinary, Paula's Choice (engaged with pages)
- Behavioral: Online shoppers + Health & beauty + High-end goods buyers
- Demographic: 24–44, female-skewing (adjust post creative test), income index >1.3

**Lookalike Audiences:**
- 1% LAL from: repeat purchasers (2+ orders) — highest LTV cohort
- 1% LAL from: 90-day email openers with purchase event
- 2% LAL from: 30-day site visitors with >3 page views

**Retargeting:**
- Product page view, no cart (Day 0–2): "The ingredient behind [product]" — educational angle
- ATC, no purchase (Day 0–3): "Still thinking? Here's what 1,200 customers are saying" + review carousel
- ATC, Day 4–7: 15% first-order offer with clear expiry
- Post-purchase (Day 30–45): "How's your skin feeling? Your [product] is running low" + replenishment nudge

## 2. Pipeline Execution: Meta Ads (Primary)

**Sprint 1 — TOF Cold (Ingredient Education Angle):**
- Format: 20s video — founder explaining WHY the brand uses ceramides instead of retinol
- Copy: "Most serums use X but cause Y. We use Z because…"
- CTA: "Learn more" → ingredient education landing page → lead capture (email for free 7-day skin guide)
- Budget: 50% Meta allocation
- KPI: View rate >40% at 50%, CPL <$5 for email capture, or CPA <$42 direct

**Sprint 2 — MOF (Social Proof / Review):**
- Format: UGC video (real customer — unscripted, phone quality)
- Copy: "I've tried everything for [specific skin concern]. This is the only thing that actually worked."
- CTA: "Shop [product name]"
- Budget: 35% Meta allocation
- KPI: ROAS >3.5x, hook rate >30% (watched 3+ seconds)

**Sprint 3 — BOF (Retarget ATC):**
- Format: Static image with review overlay + discount badge
- Copy: "[Customer name]: 'My skin texture changed in 2 weeks.'"
- CTA: "Get 15% off — offer ends [48h]"
- Budget: 15% Meta allocation
- KPI: ROAS >7x, purchase rate >18% of ATC audience

## 3. Pipeline Execution: Google (Defensive + Shopping)

- **Brand Search Defense:** $20/day. Exact match on brand + product names.
- **Google Shopping:** Focus on hero SKU only (best margin, best reviews). Feed-optimize title to include skin concern keyword.
- **Competitor Search:** Only after organic SEO ranks top 3 for primary terms — not cost-effective before then.

## 4. Creative Principles for DTC Skincare

- **Real skin > perfect skin:** Unretouched before/after (FTC-compliant) outperforms studio imagery
- **Authentic ingredient story:** Founder-to-camera explaining formulation rationale builds trust and creates differentiator
- **Social proof over claims:** "1,200 customers reviewed this" beats "clinically proven" at every funnel stage
- **UGC refresh weekly:** Algorithm rewards new creative; stale creative is wasted spend

## Quick Wins (First 14 Days)

1. **Launch post-purchase email replenishment sequence** — highest-ROAS action, zero incremental ad spend
2. **Create 3 UGC-style videos yourself** in "authentic review" format before recruiting real customers — validate angle, then scale
3. **Build the "ingredient education" landing page** for email capture — email addresses from TOF cost $4–6; each email is worth $30+ in LTV

## Channels to Avoid (For Now)

- Pinterest Ads: Large creative library required; not cost-effective before Month 4
- TikTok Paid: Organic TikTok first — validate format before paying for reach
- LinkedIn: Wrong context entirely for beauty/skincare consumer
