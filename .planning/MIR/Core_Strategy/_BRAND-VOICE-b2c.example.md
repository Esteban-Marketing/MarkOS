# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **B2C** (Premium Home Fitness Brand)

<!-- mgsd-token: MIR | model: B2C -->
> [!NOTE] B2C voice is emotionally resonant and story-driven. It speaks to identity and aspiration, not process and ROI.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Motivating Without Being Pushy**: We believe in the potential of every person who picks up our product, but we never guilt or pressure. We're the coach who sees what you're capable of — not the one shouting in your face.
* **Honest & Earned**: We don't promise six-pack abs in 30 days. We promise quality equipment, expert programming, and a community that shows up. Real results from real effort.
* **Premium but Accessible**: We're luxury in quality, not luxury in attitude. Our customers are high achievers — we speak to their best self, never down to them, never above them.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Energizing, credible, inclusive, specific, visually vivid.
* **We Are NOT**: Aggressive, guilt-inducing, bro-culture, exclusive, overpromising, or vague.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Use these words**: "your next session," "show up," "recovery," "built for real life," "on your terms," "your home, your gym," "strength," "consistency," "invest in yourself," "built to last," "made for this moment."
* **NEVER use these words**: "shred," "burn fat," "get ripped," "no excuses" (used pejoratively), "beast mode," "crush it," "transform your body in X days," "secret," "hack," "effortless results." These signal false promises or low-brand energy that our audience distrusts.

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **Instagram / Social Media**: Visually driven, emotionally evocative, short. Lead with the feeling — the aspiration, the quiet victory at 6am. First-person or second-person ("You showed up today."). Use motion, light, real people in real home environments.
* **Email Campaigns**: Warm, personal, and utility-driven. Workout tips, recovery protocols, user spotlights. Not a sales pitch every time — earn inbox trust with genuine value. Subject lines that feel like they're from a friend, not a brand.
* **Product/ Landing Pages**: Evidence-first. Materials, engineering specs, and third-party endorsements above the fold. Testimonials with specifics ("I use it 5x a week — it replaced my $200/month gym" not "Love this product!"). One clear, confident CTA.
* **Customer Support Responses**: Human and empathetic. Acknowledge immediately. Never robot-speak. If a product fails, own it without legalistic hedging. A resolved problem is a brand loyalty moment.
