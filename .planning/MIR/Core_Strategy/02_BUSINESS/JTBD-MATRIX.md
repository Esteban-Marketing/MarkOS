## 1. Functional Jobs
**The practical tasks the customer is trying to complete**

- Automate marketing campaign creation without hiring or outsourcing
- Generate contextually relevant copy that reflects brand identity (not generic)
- Push marketing decisions (content, offers, channels) into Linear tickets for team execution
- Track marketing ROI to install or revenue (not vanity metrics like impressions)
- Maintain consistent brand voice across email, LinkedIn, landing pages, paid ads
- Onboard new clients or internal teams onto marketing workflows without handholding

---

## 2. Emotional Jobs
**How the customer wants to feel**

- **Confident**: "I have a system for this. I'm not flying blind."
- **In control**: "I understand how outputs are generated; there's no black box."
- **Multiplied**: "One person is doing the work of a team."
- **Empirical**: "Every decision is traceable to data or strategy, not vibes."
- **Builder, not Manager**: "I solve problems; I don't manage SaaS dashboards."

---

## 3. Social Jobs
**How the customer wants to be perceived by others**

- **The Operator**: "I have my systems dialed in."
- **Authority in their niche**: "My marketing and product are clearly aligned."
- **Builder who ships**: "I'm not just technical; I'm also commercially astute."
- **Efficient**: "I don't waste time on BS; every hour produces output."

---

## 4. Pains
**Specific frustrations, risks, and obstacles the customer is avoiding**

- **Generic AI output**: "ChatGPT writes the same copy for me, everyone, and my competitors."
- **Tool sprawl**: "I'm using 6 different dashboards (HubSpot, Jasper, Looker, Linear, Slack, Google Ads) and spending 3 hours stitching them together."
- **Wasted time on setup**: "The onboarding and configuration takes longer than the value I get out of it."
- **Black box fear**: "I don't understand how this AI generated this copy, so I don't trust it for client work."
- **Vendor lock-in**: "I bet all my marketing infrastructure on this SaaS and they changed their pricing/API."
- **Scaling requires hiring**: "To double output, I have to double headcount. The math doesn't work."
- **Real-time sync headache**: "My marketing brain is in my head; my systems are in 5 different apps."
- **Attribution nightmare**: "I have no idea which channels or campaigns actually drove revenue."

## 5. Gains
**Positive outcomes, surprises, and concrete benefits the customer hopes for**

- **First campaign in 48 hours**: "I can move from idea to live campaign without a month of process."
- **Transparent context**: "I see exactly why the AI made this copy choice (it sourced it from my brand guidelines)."
- **Data that answers 'Did it work?'**: "Every campaign is tied to UTM, PostHog event, or Linear ticket outcome."
- **One system owns all of it**: "I define strategy once (in MIR) and all outputs reflect it."
- **Team multiplier effect**: "My 3-person agency can compete with 10-person output."
- **Client-owned data**: "I own all my marketing data. No vendor lock-in."
- **Reduction in freelance spend**: "I'm automating work I used to pay contractors to do."
- **Confidence in hiring clients**: "New clients see proof of system and competence, not chaos."
- **Faster iteration**: "I can A/B test messaging in 1 hour instead of 1 week."

## 6. Decision Triggers

### Primary Trigger
"I used ChatGPT/Claude for marketing and the output was correct but generic. It doesn't know my brand, positioning, or strategy."

**Trigger language**: "It's not bad writing, but it could have been written for anyone. That's not good enough for my clients."

### Secondary Triggers
1. **Bandwidth moment**: Shipped a product, got silence. Realized marketing needs a system, not more hours.
2. **Tool-sprawl crisis**: Realized they're managing 5+ SaaS platforms for marketing and wasting time on integration.
3. **Competitor comparison**: Saw a competitor's content and realized it's more strategic and consistent than theirs.
4. **Team growth moment**: About to hire a marketing person and realized the hire would just be managing tools, not creating strategy.

## 7. Copy Anchors & Messaging Hooks

### Anchor 1 (Generic-to-Contextual)
**Problem**: "Generic AI outputs don't know your brand."  
**Solution**: "MarkOS knows your ICP, positioning, and strategy. Every output is specific to you."  
**Proof**: "Two-layer vector retrieval means outputs are sourced from your MIR, not global training data."

### Anchor 2 (Tool-Sprawl-to-Unified)
**Problem**: "You're managing 5 SaaS dashboards for marketing."  
**Solution**: "MarkOS lives in your codebase alongside Linear, Supabase, and VS Code. One place, one workflow."  
**Proof**: "Deploy it in your repo, not a new SaaS account."

### Anchor 3 (Slow-to-Fast)
**Problem**: "Your campaigns take weeks to brief, write, and launch."  
**Solution**: "Run your first campaign in 48 hours: MIR → Agent → Campaign → Linear ticket."  
**Proof**: "Early pilots showed install-to-first-campaign-live in 36–48 hours."

### Anchor 4 (Scaling-Problem)
**Problem**: "Scaling output means scaling headcount. The math doesn't work."  
**Solution**: "MarkOS multiplies output per person. One agent running parallel campaigns."  
**Proof**: "3-person agency producing 10-person output per PostHog cohort data."

### Anchor 5 (Black-Box-Fear)
**Problem**: "You don't know how AI generated that copy, so you don't use it."  
**Solution**: "Every output is traceable: sourced MIR chunk, reasoning, decision log in Linear ticket."  
**Proof**: "See the full decision chain; never another black box."

## 8. Value Hierarchy (What Customers Prioritize)

1. **Tier 1 (Must-Have)**: Contextually relevant output (not generic)  
   Reason: If output is still generic, MarkOS isn't solving the core job

2. **Tier 2 (Important)**: Transparent architecture + ownership  
   Reason: If it's another SaaS black box, they'll churn to the next tool

3. **Tier 3 (Nice-to-Have)**: Frictionless onboarding  
   Reason: Important, but customers will tolerate 1–2 hours of setup if value ≥ 10x manual effort

4. **Tier 4 (Nice-to-Have)**: Team collaboration features  
   Reason: Solo founder priority → team features only matter after 3-person scale
