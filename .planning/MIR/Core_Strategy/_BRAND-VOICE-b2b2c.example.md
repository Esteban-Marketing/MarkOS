# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **B2B2C** (Property Management Platform)

<!-- markos-token: MIR | model: B2B2C -->
> [!NOTE] B2B2C requires a dual register — authoritative/professional for the B side, and warm/reliable for the C side (tenants). Both voices must feel like the same brand family.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Reliable**: This is the brand's core identity. Property managers need confidence that the platform won't embarrass them in front of their tenants. Tenants need confidence their request was received. Everything in the brand must signal reliability before cleverness.
* **Modern but Not Flashy**: The brand modernizes a traditionaly old-fashioned industry without alienating those who've operated the same way for 20 years. No jargon, no trend-chasing.
* **Responsive**: Speed of response is the #1 tenant satisfaction driver. The brand voice mirrors this: concise, clear, no unnecessary friction.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **B Side (Property Managers) — We Are**: Professional, practical, ROI-focused, neutral, trustworthy.
* **B Side — We Are NOT**: Overly casual, hype-driven, feature-list focused, complex.
* **C Side (Tenants) — We Are**: Friendly, clear, reassuring, fast, human.
* **C Side — We Are NOT**: Corporate, jargon-heavy, slow, dismissive.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **B Side Vocabulary — Use**: "portfolio efficiency," "tenant retention," "maintenance costs," "NOI," "operational visibility," "compliance," "occupancy rates," "your properties," "your team."
* **C Side Vocabulary — Use**: "your home," "your request," "we're on it," "track your status," "one tap," "your community."
* **NEVER use (either side)**: "cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "leverage" (as a verb), "synergy," "state-of-the-art," "world-class" (generic trust-killers), overly legal language in tenant communications, corporate speak in push notifications.

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **B Side — Sales Pages and Case Studies**: Data-forward, outcome-specific. "Portfolio X reduced maintenance costs by 22% in year one." Lead with the metric. No vague claims.
* **B Side — Email to Property Managers**: Professional and respectful of their time. Short paragraphs. Bullet-pointed key actions. Executive-summary style.
* **C Side — App Notifications**: Friendly, instant, human: "Your maintenance request has been received. An agent will follow up within 24 hours." — Never passive voice, never ambiguous timelines.
* **C Side — Email to Tenants**: Warm and helpful. Plain language. No attachment PDFs for simple communications. Rent reminders are gentle, not threatening.
