# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **DTC** (Premium Skincare Brand)

<!-- mgsd-token: MIR | model: DTC -->
> [!NOTE] DTC voice prioritizes emotional resonance, identity, and sensory language. It sells a feeling as much as a product.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Science-Backed Confidence**: We never oversell. Every claim is grounded in ingredients, clinical evidence, or mechanism of action. We are the friend who happens to be a cosmetic chemist — knowledgeable, direct, and not trying to dazzle you with jargon.
* **Quietly Aspirational**: We don't shout transformation or promise miracles. We show the elegance of a thoughtful routine. The brand whispers rather than shouts — premium is quiet.
* **Honest Inclusion**: We are actively inclusive across skin types, tones, and genders — but we don't force it. Representation is built into our visuals and language naturally, not as a performative campaign.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Precise, sensory, reassuring, elegant, educational.
* **We Are NOT**: Hype-driven, fear-based (no "don't let aging win"), exclusive/elitist, clinical-robotic, or overly casual/slang-heavy.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Use these words**: "formulated with," "clinical-grade," "skin barrier," "efficacy," "visible results," "your routine," "feels like," "absorbs instantly," "for your skin," "ritual," "radiance," "balance," "glow," "proven by dermatologists."
* **NEVER use**: "anti-aging" (use "age-supporting" or "skin longevity"), "flawless skin" (too unattainable), "fight wrinkles" (confrontational tone), "miracle serum," "just one week" (FTC risk), "perfect complexion," "secret formula" (trust-undermining), excessive exclamation marks (!!!) — they signal low-end brand positioning.

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **Instagram / TikTok**: Sensory and atmospheric. "The kind of skin you forget about." Short captions, strong visuals. Educational video hooks lead with the problem (dark spots at 40?) then solve with the active ingredient, not the product name.
* **Email Campaigns**: Warm and educational. Skincare education first (why ceramides matter), product second. The newsletter builds a relationship — open rate is the primary KPI, not click-through.
* **Product / Landing Pages**: Methodical and evidence-led. Ingredients list with explanatory notes. Clinical results in percentage terms. User reviews displayed with skin type tags. The page sells through transparency, not hype headlines.
* **Customer Support**: Personalized and empathetic. If a product doesn't work for a customer's skin, acknowledge it without defensiveness and offer a targeted alternative or return. The goal is the right product for the right person, not every product for every person.
