# BRAND-VOICE.md — Voice, Tone & Personality
# Reference Example: **B2B** (HR Technology — Applicant Tracking)

<!-- mgsd-token: MIR | model: B2B -->
> [!NOTE] This is a completed B2B brand voice example. B2B voice is authoritative and credibility-first, but never cold or corporate.

---

## 1. Personality Traits

* **Expert**: We speak from 10+ years of experience working inside HR teams. We understand the stack, the politics, and the pressure of hiring in a growth environment. We don't explain basics — we go deeper.
* **Direct**: We respect our audience's time. We lead with the point. No fluff, no vague mission statements. If we're claiming something, we prove it with data or a case study.
* **Advocate**: We are genuinely on the side of HR as a function. We believe People Ops deserves the same tools and respect as Sales and Engineering. Our tone reflects that solidarity — we're partners, not vendors.

## 2. Voice & Tone Rules

* **We Are**: Informed, grounded, specific, warm-professional, evidence-led.
* **We Are NOT**: Academic, preachy, jargon-heavy, corporate-robotic, or hyper-casual.

## 3. Vocabulary & "Ban" List

* **Use these words**: "your team," "hiring velocity," "time-to-fill," "candidate experience," "people ops," "workflow," "audit trail," "compliant," "integrates with," "your current stack," "built for scale."
* **NEVER use these words/phrases**: "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "empower" (overused), "cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "game-changer," "disrupt," "unlock your potential," "innovative solution." These sound like every other SaaS company and signal no actual differentiation.

## 4. Tone Calibration by Context

* **LinkedIn / Thought Leadership**: Informed, peer-to-peer. Write as a fellow People Ops practitioner who happens to work at this company. "Here's what we've seen across 200+ hiring processes." First person singular (I) for founder content; first person plural (We) for brand content.
* **Email Campaigns**: Conversational but intentional. Short sentences. One clear CTA per email. No "I hope this email finds you well."
* **Sales/Landing Pages**: Specific and evidence-driven. Metrics first. Social proof above the fold. Objection handling baked into the copy ("No long implementation. No IT dependency. Live in 2 weeks.").
* **Customer Support Responses**: Warm, fast, human. Use the customer's name. Acknowledge the frustration before solving the problem. Never copy-paste a script verbatim.
