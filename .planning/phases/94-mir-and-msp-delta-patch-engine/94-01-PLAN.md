<threat_model>
- T-94-01 (broad rewrite drift): the preview layer could default to full-note regeneration and violate the surgical-diff boundary. Mitigation: hard-lock section-level diff modes only and fail if a concrete target block cannot be resolved.
- T-94-02 (approval bypass): the preview engine could drift into write or apply behavior and violate the human-review requirement. Mitigation: enforce `approval_required: true` and `write_disabled: true` at the contract layer and prove it with tests.
- T-94-03 (summary-only evidence): the system could attach evidence to the overall preview but not to each changed claim. Mitigation: require per-change evidence references and inline rationale fields in the contract.
- T-94-04 (false certainty): weak or contradictory evidence could still yield a concrete patch preview. Mitigation: add a downgrade classifier that forces `suggestion_only` mode when certainty is not earned.
</threat_model>
