# Phase 209 Context - Evidence, Research, and Claim Safety

**Status:** Seeded from the 2026-04-22 incoming 00-17 discussion review.

## Why this phase exists

MarkOS cannot be a trusted marketing operating system if it produces claims, pricing recommendations, competitive statements, outreach, PR, sales copy, or strategic advice without evidence. The vault requires EvidenceMap, source quality, research freshness, claim TTL, known gaps, and inference labeling as shared substrate.

Phase 209 must serve content, social, Pricing Engine, Tenant 0 proof, SaaS Suite revenue/support facts, and future doc 17 growth motions.

## Existing implementation substrate to inspect

- MCP marketing tools: `audit_claim`, `audit_claim_strict`, and `expand_claim_evidence`.
- Existing research, reporting, CRM, governance, and citation/evidence pack code.
- Any source/citation schemas in contracts or migrations.
- Approval UI evidence display patterns.
- Pricing Engine research requirements for competitor pricing evidence.

## Required phase shape

1. Define EvidenceMap contract/schema for claim, source, confidence, timestamp, freshness, TTL, known gaps, and inference labels.
2. Define Source Quality Score and research-tier policy.
3. Define claim TTL, stale-context behavior, and unsupported-claim blocking.
4. Expose evidence in approval UI.
5. Require agents to reuse non-stale research context before starting new research.
6. Extend evidence rules to pricing, competitor intelligence, Tenant 0 proof, PR/review claims, and SaaS growth experiments.

## Non-negotiables

- No customer-facing factual claim without evidence or explicit inference label.
- No unsupported claim in external dispatch.
- No competitor/pricing claim without source quality and timestamp.
- No public Tenant 0 proof without evidence and approval.
- No future PR, analyst, event, review, ABM, or developer-marketing outreach without evidence rules.

## Done means

GSD can plan a shared evidence substrate that makes MarkOS credible, auditable, and safe to operate externally.
