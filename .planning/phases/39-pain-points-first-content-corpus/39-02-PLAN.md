Title: `# High Cost Per Result: Diagnosing and Reversing CPR Inflation`
Definition paragraph: 3-4 sentences explaining CPR inflation = gradual cost increase per desired action (click, lead, purchase) caused by audience saturation, creative fatigue, or bid competition. Why it matters: CPR inflation directly degrades CAC efficiency and makes budget scale unprofitable.

EVIDENCE BASE section: ≥4 bullet points with sourced statistics:
- Meta 2024: 72% of advertisers see CPR increase >30% after 21 days without creative rotation
- Metadata.io 2024: Median CPR for B2B SaaS lead-gen on Meta = $18–$45; top quartile < $12
- Google Ads 2024: Quality Score below 6 increases CPC by 16–50% vs. baseline
- WordStream 2024: Facebook average CPM increased 89% from 2021 to 2024 for US B2B audiences

CORE TACTICS section (≥3 H3 tactics):
- ### Creative Rotation Cadence: Rotate every 14–21 days or when frequency > 3.0/week. Include: how to identify fatigue signals (CTR decline >20% week-over-week, CPC increase >15%), what to rotate (hook, visual, format, CTA variant), how to maintain an ad library with ≥8 creative variants to enable systematic rotation.
- ### Audience Segmentation and Exclusion: Segment by ICP tier, exclude 30-day converters and 180-day non-clickers, build lookalikes from top-10%-LTV customers only. B2B: use job title + company size layering on LinkedIn/Meta. DTC: use post-purchase suppression + LTV percentile seed audiences.
- ### Bid Strategy Alignment: Match bid strategy to funnel stage. Top-of-funnel = Maximize Reach or Target CPM. Mid-funnel = Target CPA with 14-day click attribution. Bottom-of-funnel = ROAS with 7-day click/1-day view. Explain: manual CPC protects against CPR spikes in volatile auctions; switch to automated when ≥50 conversions/week.

PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS table: Paid_Media CPR benchmarks — poor/median/top-quartile for Meta B2B lead-gen, Google Search lead-gen, LinkedIn lead-gen.

COUNTER-INDICATORS section: ≥3 bullets covering:
- Brand awareness campaigns (CPR doesn't apply — use CPM/reach metrics instead)
- Early audience test (< 500 impressions — too early to diagnose, let it run)
- Nonprofit/grant-period campaigns (budget constraints override CPR optimization)

VOCABULARY section: ≥5 terms: CPR, Creative Fatigue, Frequency Cap, Lookalike Audience, Suppression List

Title: `# Ranking Stagnation: Why Pages Don't Rank and How to Break Through`
Definition: Ranking stagnation = target keywords stuck outside top 20 SERP positions despite content investment, resulting in near-zero organic traffic contribution. Primary causes: insufficient topical authority, thin content depth vs. competitors, technical crawlability issues, and backlink gap.

EVIDENCE BASE: ≥4 data points — Ahrefs 2024 on pages that rank vs. those that don't; Backlinko on correlation between content length and ranking position; Google's guidance on helpful content; SimilarWeb data on organic traffic distribution (top 3 results capture 75%+ of clicks).

CORE TACTICS (≥3):
- ### Topical Authority Clustering: Build content clusters before targeting competitive keywords. Pillar page (2,000+ words on broad topic) + 8–12 supporting cluster pages on specific subtopics + internal linking. Ahrefs data: sites with 10+ pages on a topic rank 40% higher on average for the primary keyword.
- ### Competitor Content Gap Analysis: Use Ahrefs or SEMrush "Content Gap" to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't. Prioritize: high volume (≥500/month) + medium difficulty (KD 20–50) + transactional or informational intent aligned to your funnel. Write one piece targeting each gap.
- ### Technical Crawl Health Audit: Verify: sitemap.xml submitted to GSC, canonical tags correct, 0 competing page signals (duplicate title tags), Core Web Vitals green (LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1), mobile-responsive.

PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS: Table — keyword positions and their click-through rate probability; ranking velocity by domain authority tier (DA < 20, 20–50, > 50); typical time-to-rank by content type.

COUNTER-INDICATORS: ≥3 — brand new domains (< 6 months old: focus on long-tail first); highly competitive terms (KD > 70) without strong domain authority; e-commerce product pages (different optimization strategy than editorial content).

VOCABULARY: ≥5 terms: Topical Authority, Content Cluster, Keyword Difficulty (KD), Domain Rating (DR), Featured Snippet.
