<threat_model>
- T-92-01 (Cross-tenant leakage): callers may try to search or fetch another tenant's approved knowledge. Mitigation: require tenant claims and fail closed with the existing pageindex-scope pattern before any artifact URI or snippet is returned.
- T-92-02 (Draft exposure): draft or unapproved MIR, MSP, or evidence could be returned by a loose query path. Mitigation: hard-code an approved-only policy layer per D-01 and D-02 and reject any draft or review-pending artifact.
- T-92-03 (Token-bloat overexposure): search may accidentally return full documents instead of compact snippets. Mitigation: cap search to snippet-first previews with citation and metadata only; full body is fetch-only per D-07.
- T-92-04 (Unsafe method expansion): write, patch, approve, or catalog-browse behavior could sneak into the service surface. Mitigation: expose only read-oriented search and fetch helpers in this plan and explicitly deny write-like intents.
- T-92-05 (Path leakage): raw local file paths or storage layout could leak through fetch identifiers or errors. Mitigation: use markos URI wrappers and deterministic safe error codes instead of filesystem paths.
</threat_model>
