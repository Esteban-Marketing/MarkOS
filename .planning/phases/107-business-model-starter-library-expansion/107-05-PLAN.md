**# Services Tone and Naturality Guidance**
- Opening (2 sentences): Note overlay doc provides core posture. This doc adds funnel-stage specifics for long-cycle service models: building enough trust across Awareness and Consideration for a buyer to take a low-commitment first step.
- Key distinction: Services ≠ SaaS. Services is practitioner-led (the people are the product). Decision timeline is weeks to months. The buyer is "sold" by conviction, not trial.

**## EVIDENCE BASE** (2 short bullets):
- LinkedIn organic research: B2B professional services decision-makers consume 3-7 pieces of content from a provider before engaging. Thought leadership with named author converts 2x vs. brand-generic.
- LinkedIn demand gen report: "Trust in the provider" ranked #1 purchase driver for professional services above price, scope, and speed.

**## CORE TACTICS** per stage:
- **Awareness**: Thought leadership as peer share. LinkedIn articles, podcast mentions, published POVs. Voice: senior practitioner, not brand account. Topic: the problem you solve, not your solution. CTA: soft — "follow for more", "download the guide".
- **Consideration**: Process credibility. Walk through how you work, your methodology, who you've worked with, and what you protect against. Use case studies and client logos with permission. CTA: free consult/audit, "book a 30-min diagnostic".
- **Decision**: Proof confident, low pressure. No discount offers. Reinforce conviction with specific, named case studies. Address objections like "we tried this before" with "here's what was different". CTA: proposal, pilot, or scoped first engagement.
- **Onboarding**: Expectation-setting premium. Confirm scope, introduce team, explain what measurable progress looks like at week 4 and week 12. Reduce buyer's remorse before it forms.
- **Retention**: Relationship reinforcing. Regular status, proactive suggestions (not just reactive deliverables), account growth moments (new scope opportunity framed as client benefit). Make clients your advocates.

**## COUNTER-INDICATORS**:
- Urgency or scarcity language ("limited spots", "this offer expires").
- Discount-led conversion tactics.
- Generic ROI claims without named case study backing.

```markdown
# Services Starter Pack

**Business model:** Professional or managed services — revenue generated through expertise, time, and deliverables rather than products. The provider IS the product.
**Primary marketing challenges:**
- Trust friction: buyers must believe the team can solve a problem they haven't yet seen proof of
- Long sales cycles: 4-16 week decision timelines requiring consistent presence across multiple touchpoints
- Unclear differentiation: most agencies and service firms describe what they do, not why they win

**Disciplines covered:** Paid_Media · Content_SEO · Lifecycle_Email · Social · Landing_Pages

## When to use this pack
Use for agencies, consulting firms, managed service providers, law/accounting practices, and any firm where "the people are the product". Signals: service retainer model, custom scoping, proposal-based sales.

## Pack contents
| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for thought leadership ads, LinkedIn awareness, and retargeting |
| Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for expertise articles, case study SEO, and service category pages |
| Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for nurture, proposal follow-up, and account retention sequences |
| Social/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for LinkedIn organic, POV posts, and expertise proof |
| Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for service pages, consultation booking, and case study landing pages |
```

**Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md** (Services — Content & SEO):
> Context: Services SEO wins on long-tail practice area keywords ("how to choose X", "what does X cost", "X consultant vs in-house"), case study pages, and thought leadership that earns links. Ranks on topical authority.

**Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md** (Services — Lifecycle Email):
> Context: Services email is primarily relationship-based, long-cycle nurture. Key sequences: post-inquiry nurture, proposal follow-up, and account check-in for existing clients. No promotional urgency. Practitioner voice.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Post-inquiry nurture sequence (3 emails, spaced 3-5 days): Email 1 = relevant case study; Email 2 = "how we approach [their problem type]" methodology preview; Email 3 = soft ask for discovery call, (2) Proposal follow-up sequence (3 emails over 2 weeks): Email 1 = follow up with a single clarifying resource (article, case study); Email 2 = address the most common objection; Email 3 = "still evaluating?" — peer story of someone who delayed and outcome of acting, (3) Existing client quarterly check-in email (acknowledge progress, surface a new growth opportunity framed as client benefit, not a pitch), (4) Win-back email for a lapsed client or prospect who went dark (practitioner check-in, no hard ask, link to recent work).

**Social/PROMPTS.md** (Services — Social Media):
> Context: Services social is 90% LinkedIn. Content types that work: POV posts, case study fragments, process posts, team/credibility proof. Tone: author-led, named practitioner, calm confidence. No hype. No follower games.

Write 4 prompts: (1) LinkedIn POV post brief — share a contrarian view on a common practice in your space (name the author, state the thesis, evidence 2-3 points, end with a question to drive comments), (2) Case study fragment post — pull one number + one quote from a recent client win, frame as a story with a lead, (3) "How we approach [process]" transparency post — show methodology or thinking, 3-5 bullet format, builds process credibility, (4) Practitioner credential post — publication mention, speaking slot, certification, award — framed as proof for the client, not self-congratulation.
