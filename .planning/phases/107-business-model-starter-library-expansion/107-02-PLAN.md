**# B2C (Business-to-Consumer) Tone and Naturality Guidance**
- Opening paragraph (2–3 sentences): Frame B2C as emotionally-driven and brand-relationship-focused. Buying decisions range from impulse to considered depending on AOV. Brand identity and social proof drive trust more than ROI data.
- Note the key B2C vs ecommerce distinction: B2C is a broader model covering brand products, subscription services, apps, and digital goods. Ecommerce is a specific subset focused on transaction mechanics (covered in its own pack).

**## EVIDENCE BASE** (2–4 bullets):
- Nielsen: 92% of consumers trust peer recommendations over brand advertising.
- Consumer decisions for sub-$100 products average 1–3 days from first exposure.
- UGC-led ads outperform polished brand content by 20-50% on Meta at identical CPM.

**## CORE TACTICS** with Awareness/Consideration/Decision/Onboarding/Retention:
- **Awareness**: Emotionally resonant. Lifestyle-forward. Show identity fit ("people like you use this"). Avoid feature lists. Lead with the feeling, aspiration, or social identity the product enables.
- **Consideration**: Shift from "what it represents" to "what it does for you." Benefit-specific: surface the 1-2 most differentiated features in consumer language. Peer review snippets, before/after media, and micro-influencer coverage land here.
- **Decision**: Social proof-led. Ratings count, review excerpts, "X people bought this today", trust signals (returns policy, secure checkout). Remove friction with clear CTAs and reassurance language.
- **Onboarding**: Rewarding and confirmatory. "Great choice" + what to expect next. For subscriptions: welcome the member, surface the clearest first-value moment.
- **Retention**: Community and identity. Reward loyalty without cheapening the brand. Use milestone language ("you've been with us 6 months") and referral prompts.

**## COUNTER-INDICATORS**:
- Clinical spec-heavy copy detached from identity or lifestyle.
- B2B-style ROI framing (cost-per-outcome) when the buyer is emotionally driven.
- Generic "you might also like" retargeting without personalization signals.

```markdown
# B2C Starter Pack

**Business model:** Selling products or services directly to individual consumers, where trust is built through brand identity, lifestyle resonance, and peer social proof rather than ROI data.
**Primary marketing challenges:**
- Building brand affinity before the first purchase
- Converting social discovery into on-site action
- Retaining subscribers and repeat buyers against churn

**Disciplines covered:** Paid_Media · Content_SEO · Lifecycle_Email · Social · Landing_Pages

## When to use this pack
Use for direct-to-consumer brands, subscription products, consumer apps, lifestyle products, and digital goods targeting individual buyers. Signals: AOV < $500, purchase decision made by one person, brand identity is a differentiator, social media drives discovery.

## Pack contents
| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for Meta/TikTok/Instagram paid social |
| Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for lifestyle content and buyer guide SEO |
| Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for behavioral triggers and post-purchase |
| Social/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for UGC, influencer-compatible, and organic social |
| Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for product pages and direct-response landing pages |
```

**Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md** (B2C — Paid Media Starter Prompts):
> Context: B2C paid media runs primarily on Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and TikTok for discovery, and Google Shopping for intent. Prompts reflect emotional hooks, UGC-style creative direction, and short decision windows.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Meta/Instagram cold audience ad with emotional hook targeting a lifestyle identity ("people who X"), (2) TikTok-style UGC video brief for a product reveal with authentic testimonial structure, (3) Google Shopping product listing title/description optimization for a competitive category, (4) Meta retargeting ad for add-to-cart abandoners with social proof (review snippet + urgency).

**Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md** (B2C — Content & SEO Starter Prompts):
> Context: B2C content earns trust through lifestyle alignment, buyer guides, and how-to content that surfaces during research. SEO targets informational and commercial-intent queries, not just brand names.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Lifestyle/inspiration article brief targeting a "best [product category] for [persona]" keyword, (2) Comparison guide brief positioning your product against 3 alternatives (buyer-intent keyword), (3) How-to/tutorial content brief using your product as the solution tool, (4) Gift guide round-up targeting seasonal commerce traffic.

**Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md** (B2C — Lifecycle Email Starter Prompts):
> Context: B2C email is triggered by behavior and lifecycle stage. Key sequences: welcome, post-purchase, win-back, and loyalty. Tone is warm and reward-forward, not transactional.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Welcome series (3-part): brand story → first-purchase recommendation → social community invite, (2) Post-purchase follow-up at day 7 (review request + care tips + next complement), (3) 60-day win-back email for subscribers who stopped engaging (identity reminder, not discount lead), (4) Loyalty milestone email ("You've been with us 1 year") with exclusive early access offer.

**Social/PROMPTS.md** (B2C — Social Media Starter Prompts):
> Context: B2C social is identity and community-first. UGC and influencer-compatible content performs better than polished brand content. Content should be shareable and "people like me" resonant.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Instagram carousel showing 3-5 customer transformation/use-case moments (UGC-sourced, reposted), (2) TikTok script brief for "day in the life" brand integration (non-sponsored feel), (3) Community engagement post: question or poll that invites followers to share their experience with the product, (4) Behind-the-scenes story content showing product origins, sourcing, or team — builds brand authenticity.

**Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md** (B2C — Landing Pages Starter Prompts):
> Context: B2C landing pages convert on emotional resonance, social proof, and minimal friction. Urgency must be used honestly. Return policy and trust signals remove hesitation.

Write 4 prompts: (1) DTC product page structure (hero with lifestyle image, benefit bullets, review excerpts, FAQ, sticky add-to-cart), (2) Quiz/personalization funnel landing page (find your product via 3 questions), (3) Subscription/membership upgrade page (what you get, social proof from subscribers, low-commitment CTA), (4) Limited drop or new-product launch page (exclusivity framing without manufactured scarcity).
