**# B2B (Business-to-Business) Tone and Naturality Guidance**
- Opening paragraph (2–3 sentences): Frame B2B as committee-driven, multi-stakeholder, weeks-to-months cycles. Emphasize that trust mechanics center on ROI proof, implementation confidence, and peer social validation rather than emotional resonance.

**## EVIDENCE BASE** (2–4 evidence bullets, sourced from research patterns):
- Gartner B2B buying group data: the average B2B purchase involves 6-10 stakeholders.
- LinkedIn B2B Institute: thought leadership content drives 2-3x engagement vs. product-push content at awareness stage.
- Mention that 5-7 touchpoints are typical before a first demo/meeting in mid-market and enterprise.

**## CORE TACTICS** with subsections Awareness, Consideration, Decision, Onboarding, Retention:
- **Awareness**: Lead with the category problem and the operational cost of not solving it. Use thought leadership framing. Avoid product mentions. Target the practitioner who experiences the pain.
- **Consideration**: Increase specificity. Surface quantified differentiators, integration capabilities, and ROI range data. Multi-threaded messaging: address economic buyer (ROI/TCO), champion (ease of use/adoption), and IT/security gatekeeper (compliance/API) in parallel.
- **Decision**: Proof-confident, low-uncertainty. Named customer case studies with specific metrics. Implementation path clarity. Address procurement concerns (SLAs, support, security). Clear, low-commitment next step (demo, pilot, POC).
- **Onboarding**: Clear, process-oriented. Remove ambiguity about first steps. Re-confirm the value promise against the implementation reality.
- **Retention**: Reinforce value with business outcomes achieved. Encourage power-user expansion, upsell framing as increased ROI, not "more features". Reference QBR cadence language.

**## COUNTER-INDICATORS**:
- Startup-hype language ("game-changing", "revolutionary") at any stage.
- Urgency tactics borrowed from B2C (countdown timers, flash sales) — B2B buyers distrust artificial pressure.
- Single-persona messaging when the buying group is multi-stakeholder.

```markdown
# B2B Starter Pack

**Business model:** Selling products or services to other businesses, typically involving multi-stakeholder buying decisions, longer sales cycles, and ROI-driven purchase criteria.
**Primary marketing challenges:**
- Multi-stakeholder alignment: 6-10 decision-makers with conflicting priorities
- Long nurture cycles: 3–12 months from awareness to closed deal
- Proving ROI before commitment: procurement, legal, and security gatekeepers

**Disciplines covered:** Paid_Media · Content_SEO · Lifecycle_Email · Social · Landing_Pages

## When to use this pack
Use for SaaS companies selling to businesses, professional services firms, B2B marketplaces, enterprise software, and any offer where the buyer is a company employee making a business decision. Signals: deal size > $5K, involves demo or proposal stage, buyer has an ICP job title.

## Pack contents
| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for B2B paid acquisition (LinkedIn ABM, Google Search, lead-gen) |
| Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for thought leadership and solution-page SEO |
| Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for multi-persona nurture sequences |
| Social/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for LinkedIn thought leadership and educational content |
| Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for demo/trial request and lead capture pages |
```

**Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md** (`B2B — Content & SEO Starter Prompts`):
> Context: B2B content SEO earns trust through original research, thought leadership, and solution-specific pages that address the buying committee's questions. Prompts reflect longer-form content that builds authority with the practitioner and commercial pages for the economic buyer.

Write 4 prompts covering: (1) Outline for a pillar article targeting a high-intent buying-stage keyword (e.g., "best [category] for [industry]"), (2) Brief for an original research/benchmark report to use as a lead magnet and PR asset, (3) Solution page outline targeting a specific ICP pain point with proof blocks, (4) Comparison/alternative page targeting competitors by name.

**Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md** (`B2B — Lifecycle Email Starter Prompts`):
> Context: B2B email nurtures multi-stakeholder decisions over weeks to months. Sequences are persona-tiered (champion vs. economic buyer) and timed to move prospects from awareness to demo to close.

Write 4 prompts covering: (1) Lead magnet follow-up sequence (3 emails, 7-day cadence, moves from content delivery → related problem framing → soft demo CTA), (2) Re-engagement email for MQLs who went cold after 30 days, (3) Post-demo "close the loop" email for the economic buyer who wasn't on the call, (4) Customer onboarding welcome email (day 1) focused on first activation milestone.

**Social/PROMPTS.md** (`B2B — Social Media Starter Prompts`):
> Context: B2B social focuses on LinkedIn. Content is educational and thought-leadership-first rather than promotional. The goal is trust-building with the practitioner audience before they enter the buying cycle.
