```markdown
# SaaS Starter Pack

**Business model:** Software delivered as a subscription, typically with self-serve trials, product-led growth at lower tiers, and sales-assisted enterprise expansion.
**Primary marketing challenges:**
- Activating trial users to their first value moment within 7 days
- Converting free/trial users to paid with clear pricing and upgrade triggers
- Reducing churn at renewal through usage-based retention signals

**Disciplines covered:** Paid_Media · Content_SEO · Lifecycle_Email · Social · Landing_Pages

## When to use this pack
Use for SaaS products, developer tools, B2B software, and software platforms with free trial, freemium, or PLG entry points. Signals: product trial available, monthly or annual subscription pricing, activation milestone defines "converted".

## Pack contents
| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for Google + LinkedIn trial-focused acquisition |
| Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for comparison pages, use-case SEO, integration pages |
| Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for trial onboarding, trial-to-paid, and renewal sequences |
| Social/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for product screenshots, feature demos, customer wins |
| Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md | Starter prompts for free trial and pricing pages |
```

**Paid_Media/PROMPTS.md** (SaaS — Paid Media):
> Context: SaaS paid media runs on Google Search (high-intent, bottom-funnel), LinkedIn (enterprise tier ABM), and retargeting. Prompts reflect trial CTAs, integration-awareness, and clear pricing confidence.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Google Search ad copy targeting "[category] software for [ICP]" with a free trial CTA and time-to-value callout, (2) LinkedIn Sponsored Content for awareness targeting Operations/Product personas with a category problem hook (not product-first), (3) Retargeting ad for pricing page visitors who didn't start trial (address the most common objection), (4) LinkedIn Lead Gen Form for a ROI calculator or benchmark report gated offer.

**Content_SEO/PROMPTS.md** (SaaS — Content & SEO):
> Context: SaaS content wins on comparison/alternative pages, integration pages, and use-case content targeting specific ICP roles. High-intent bottom-funnel keywords convert at 2-5x informational blog traffic.

Write 4 prompts: (1) "[Your product] vs [Competitor]" comparison page outline (fair but differentiator-led), (2) "[Your product] for [ICP role/industry]" use-case page outline, (3) Integration page outline for a popular tool in your category (drives qualified traffic from partner audience), (4) Bottom-funnel solution page for the core problem your product solves, with quantified outcome proof.

**Lifecycle_Email/PROMPTS.md** (SaaS — Lifecycle Email):
> Context: SaaS email is lifecycle-milestone-driven, not time-driven. Trial onboarding sequences should trigger on activation behavior, not calendar days. The trial-to-paid conversion window is typically days 3-14.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Trial onboarding Day 1 email (activation goal clear, 1 specific action, value promise re-stated), (2) Trial Day 7 check-in for users who haven't activated (re-engage with the single biggest first-value action), (3) Trial expiry email sequence: 3 days before expiry → on day → 3 days after (include trial extension offer and pricing clarity), (4) Post-upgrade welcome email (confirm value, introduce power features, NPS timing note for day 30).

**Social/PROMPTS.md** (SaaS — Social Media):
> Context: SaaS social content performs best on LinkedIn for B2B-oriented products. Format winners: product screenshots showing outcomes, feature explainer carousels, and customer win posts. Avoid corporate polish — product credibility outperforms brand aesthetics.

Write 4 prompts: (1) LinkedIn product screenshot post showing a specific outcome or dashboard view with a "here's what this means for your [role]" callout, (2) Feature explainer carousel: 5-7 slides walking through one key feature with use-case context, (3) Customer win post: one metric, one testimonial line, one takeaway (short and specific), (4) LinkedIn thought leadership post from founder/team: opinion on a category trend or common misconception, inviting comments.

**Landing_Pages/PROMPTS.md** (SaaS — Landing Pages):
> Context: SaaS landing pages convert on trial clarity and pricing confidence. Free trial pages should address security, time-to-value, and upgrade path. Pricing pages must answer "which plan is right for me" without a sales call.

Write 4 prompts: (1) Free trial landing page outline (headline with outcome, 3 benefit bullets, social proof bar, minimal form, "no credit card required" reassurance), (2) Pricing page copy brief (feature comparison table, plan naming, "most popular" anchor, FAQ block addressing upgrade and cancellation), (3) ROI/payback calculator landing page brief (input fields, output framing, CTA to trial or demo), (4) Integration partner landing page (headline for partner audience, 3 integration use cases, mutual customer logos, CTA).
