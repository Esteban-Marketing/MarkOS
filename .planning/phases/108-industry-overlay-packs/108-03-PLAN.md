    # IT & Technology — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** IT paid media targets expert buyers who have high skepticism and long evaluation cycles. The most effective IT paid media matches the buyer's current research mode: awareness-stage buyers respond to thought leadership and category framing; consideration-stage buyers respond to ROI/TCO proof and competitive differentiation; decision-stage buyers respond to case studies, proof-of-concept offers, and compliance validation. Flashy creative underperforms; technical credibility and specific claim density outperform.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. Technical Buyer Intent Search Campaign

    Write a Google Search ad campaign for a [software/infrastructure/security/managed services vendor] targeting evaluation-stage keywords used by IT decision-makers.

    Campaign parameters:
    - **High-intent keyword examples:** "[product category] for enterprise," "[product category] security compliance," "best [category] solution [industry]," "[category] vs [competitor]," "[category] ROI calculator"
    - **Ad structure (3 RSA headlines, 30 chars max each):**
      - Headline 1: Category + core differentiator ("Enterprise [Category] | [Key Cert or Feature]")
      - Headline 2: Strongest proof hook (customer count, uptime SLA, analyst recognition)
      - Headline 3: Conversion action ("Free POC" / "Get the ROI Calculator" / "Talk to a Solutions Architect")
    - **Descriptions (2, 90 chars max each):**
      - Description 1: Architecture or integration claim with a specific metric ("Deploys in [timeframe]. Integrates with [N] enterprise systems out of the box.")
      - Description 2: Security/compliance proof + trial friction reduction ("SOC 2 Type II · ISO 27001 · 30-day POC with dedicated implementation support")
    - **Ad extensions:** Sitelinks to technical documentation, security certifications page, ROI calculator, and case studies; callout extensions for named certifications and SLA guarantees

    Tone: Credible and specific. Every claim must be verifiable.

    ---

    ### 2. LinkedIn ABM Campaign for IT Decision-Makers

    Write a LinkedIn paid campaign brief and ad creative for reaching IT decision-makers (CTO, CISO, VP Engineering, IT Director, VP Infrastructure) at target accounts.

    Campaign structure:
    - **Audience targeting:** Job title list (CTO, CISO, IT Director, VP Engineering, VP Infrastructure, Head of IT) + company size (500+ employees) + industry filters relevant to your ICP; optionally: company list upload for named-account ABM
    - **Campaign objective:** Lead gen form (for demo/POC request) or website conversions (for content syndication)
    - **Ad format:** Single image + lead gen form; or document ad for white paper / benchmark report distribution
    - **Ad creative (Single Image + Lead Gen Form):**
      - Image: Non-stock, technical diagram or dashboard screenshot — IT buyers distrust polished stock imagery
      - Headline (150 chars): Problem-aware framing + credibility signal ("How [Company Type] IT teams are reducing [specific pain] without [feared tradeoff]")
      - Intro text (600 chars): Lead with the technical problem in buyer-native language; state one specific outcome from a real customer; introduce the offer (demo, POC, report); include named certification if relevant
      - Form headline: "Request a [Technical Demo / POC Scoping Call / Benchmark Report]"
      - Form fields: Work email, company name, company size, primary IT challenge (dropdown: security, compliance, performance, cost reduction, integration)
    - **Follow-up sequence trigger:** Form submission → immediate email with asset + SDR outreach within 4 business hours

    ---

    ### 3. Retargeting Campaign with ROI and TCO Proof Content

    Write a retargeting ad strategy and creative brief for a [software/infrastructure vendor] targeting IT buyers who visited the product, pricing, or technical documentation pages but did not convert.

    Audience segments and message by segment:
    - **Segment A — Visited product overview page (awareness):** Message: Customer case study or analyst recognition; "How [company like theirs] solved [their problem]." Keep the ask low: "Read the case study" or "Download the benchmark report."
    - **Segment B — Visited pricing or ROI calculator page (consideration):** Message: TCO comparison framing; "What [Your Product] actually costs vs. the alternative." Offer: ROI calculator or TCO worksheet download. CTA: "Calculate your ROI."
    - **Segment C — Visited trial/demo request page, did not complete (high-intent):** Message: Remove the friction point — address the most common hesitation ("Concerned about deployment complexity? Our POC takes [X] hours with a dedicated solutions architect."). CTA: "Schedule your POC" with direct SDR calendar link.

    For each segment: write a Meta retargeting ad (primary text 125 chars + headline 40 chars) and a Google Display ad (headline 30 chars + description 90 chars).

    ---

    ### 4. Competitive Displacement Campaign

    Write a paid search campaign targeting branded keywords for [2–3 named competitors] and category keywords where the incumbent solution is being re-evaluated.

    Campaign structure:
    - **Competitor brand keyword strategy:** Why bidding on competitor brand names is valuable for IT buyers in re-evaluation mode; recommended bid modifiers (higher bids for competitor + "alternative" or competitor + "vs" queries); ad copy compliance (cannot claim to be the competitor — must clearly brand as your product)
    - **Ad copy for competitor brand keywords (3 RSA headlines + 2 descriptions):**
      - Headline 1: "[Your Product] — [Competitor] Alternative" or "Compare [Your Product] vs [Competitor]"
      - Headline 2: Strongest switching proof point (migration support, cost reduction vs. competitor, feature parity + advantage)
      - Headline 3: Low-friction conversion action ("Free migration assessment" / "Side-by-side comparison")
      - Description 1: State one concrete advantage over the competitor — specific, not generic ("Unlike [Competitor], [Your Product] includes [feature] at all tiers — no extra licensing.")
      - Description 2: Migration support framing + social proof ("[N] IT teams switched from [Competitor] in the last year. Migration support included.")
    - **Landing page alignment:** What the competitor comparison landing page must include — the comparison table, migration support details, and 2–3 case studies from teams who switched

    Include a note on legal/policy compliance: Google and LinkedIn prohibit deceptive competitor claims — all claims must be verifiable and the ad must display your brand name.

    # IT & Technology — Content & SEO Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** IT content SEO converts through technical credibility and evaluation support. The highest-performing IT content helps a skeptical buyer build their internal business case — ROI/TCO models, compliance documentation, and technical comparison guides all serve this need. Keyword strategy in IT targets evaluation queries ("best [category] for enterprise"), proof queries ("[category] security certifications"), and competitive queries ("[product A] vs [product B]"). Thin or promotional content performs poorly with expert IT buyers.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. Technical Comparison and Evaluation Guide

    Write a technical comparison guide for a [software/infrastructure/security vendor] targeting the keyword "[Your Product] vs [Competitor]" or "best [product category] for [IT environment/use case]."

    Page structure:
    - **H1:** "[Your Product] vs [Competitor]: A Technical Comparison for [IT Environment, e.g., Enterprise Cloud, SMB IT, Regulated Industries]"
    - **Intro (100 words):** Acknowledge this is a legitimate evaluation question; state who this guide is for (IT architect, IT director, cost-center owner); establish your credibility to make this comparison
    - **Evaluation framework (define 6–8 criteria):** Architecture and deployment model; integration ecosystem; security certifications and compliance framework coverage; performance benchmarks (latency, throughput, uptime SLA); total cost of ownership (licensing model, implementation, operational overhead); support and SLA tiers; vendor roadmap and stability
    - **Comparison table:** For each criterion, score honestly — include 2 criteria where the competitor is genuinely competitive; this builds credibility with skeptical IT buyers
    - **"Who [Your Product] is optimized for" (100 words):** Specific IT environment, company size, or compliance requirement
    - **"Where [Competitor] may be the better fit" (80 words):** Honest guidance — if the buyer's requirements align better with the competitor, say so; the long-term trust is worth the short-term loss
    - **Migration path section:** What it takes to move from [Competitor] — timeline, data migration, integration reconfiguration, and available support resources
    - **CTA:** "Request a POC" or "Talk to a solutions architect"

    ---

    ### 2. ROI and TCO Content

    Write a ROI-focused content piece for a [software/infrastructure vendor] targeting the keyword "ROI of [product category]" or "[product category] total cost of ownership."

    Content structure (choose: long-form article 1,200 words or interactive calculator landing page):
    - **H1:** "The True Total Cost of [Product Category]: A TCO Framework for IT and Finance Teams"
    - **Intro (100 words):** Why TCO analysis is necessary — procurement and finance require it; IT teams need to quantify the business case; surface the common error (licensing cost only, vs. full TCO including implementation, training, operational overhead, and downtime cost)
    - **TCO framework (4 components):**
      - Direct costs: licensing/subscription, implementation, training, ongoing support
      - Operational costs: IT staff time for administration, patch management, incident response
      - Risk costs: downtime frequency and business impact; cost of a security incident; compliance audit failures
      - Opportunity costs: time-to-value, migration complexity from incumbent
    - **Calculation example (use realistic but anonymized figures):** A [company size, e.g., 500-person] company with [current approach] has an estimated 3-year TCO of $[X]. [Your Product] scenario: $[Y]. Delta: $[Z] saved / [N] months to ROI.
    - **[Your Product] TCO positioning:** Where [Your Product] specifically reduces cost vs. the alternative — be specific
    - **CTA:** "Download the TCO worksheet" (gated) or "Talk to a solutions architect about your environment"

    ---

    ### 3. Security and Compliance Capability Overview Page

    Write a security and compliance overview page for a [software/infrastructure vendor] targeting IT buyers in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government, critical infrastructure).

    Page structure:
    - **H1:** "[Your Product] Security and Compliance: Certifications, Architecture, and Audit Support"
    - **Certification grid (above fold):** Visual display of held certifications — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, FedRAMP, HIPAA BAA, PCI DSS, GDPR compliance, CCPA compliance — each with issue date and renewal status
    - **Security architecture section (300 words):** Data encryption (at rest and in transit — specify algorithm and key length); access control model (RBAC, MFA, SSO integrations); network security posture (zero-trust, VPN, private cloud options); audit log and monitoring capabilities
    - **Compliance-by-industry section:** For each target regulated industry (healthcare, finance, government), describe the specific compliance framework coverage and how [Your Product] supports audit readiness
    - **Data residency and sovereignty:** Available regions, data processing agreements, GDPR controller/processor roles
    - **Security incident response:** SLA for notification (must meet GDPR 72-hour requirement if applicable); incident response process; customer notification procedure
    - **CTA:** "Request our security documentation package" (for IT/procurement due diligence) or "Schedule a security review call"

    ---

    ### 4. IT Use-Case Solution Page

    Write an SEO-optimized solution page for a [software/infrastructure vendor] targeting a specific IT environment or use case: [cloud migration / hybrid IT / remote workforce security / data center modernization / DevSecOps pipeline].

    Page structure:
    - **H1:** "[Your Product Category] for [Specific IT Use Case]: [Outcome-Led Positioning]"
    - **Above fold (150 words):** Technical problem framing in the language of the IT buyer who owns this use case — specific pain, specific constraint, specific risk; state the solution approach in 2 sentences
    - **How it works (architecture overview):** Diagram description — show how [Your Product] fits into the customer's existing environment (note: describe what a technical diagram should show; don't just say "add a diagram"); 3 integration points with named enterprise systems (e.g., Azure AD, Splunk, ServiceNow)
    - **Capabilities by use-case need (3–4 sections, 80 words each):** Map each capability to the specific use-case requirement — not generic feature descriptions
    - **Customer case example:** Real or representative — company size and industry (not name if anonymous), specific environmental challenge, implementation timeline, measurable outcome
    - **Technical specifications section:** Deployment models supported (SaaS, on-prem, hybrid); OS/platform support; API documentation link; integration list
    - **CTA:** "Request a technical demo for your environment" — not a generic "Book a demo"

    # IT & Technology — Lifecycle & Email Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** IT lifecycle email must match the buyer's role and decision stage. Technical evaluators need architecture depth; IT directors need ROI and business case support; procurement needs compliance documentation. The highest-performing IT email sequences are tightly role-segmented and value-dense — delivering genuinely useful technical content at each stage rather than promotional messaging. Overly frequent or promotional email erodes credibility with expert IT buyers.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. Technical Onboarding Sequence

    Write a 4-email technical onboarding sequence for a [software/infrastructure/security vendor], starting after an IT buyer completes a POC sign-up or trial activation.

    Email 1 — POC/trial activation (send: immediately):
    - Subject: "Your [Product] POC is ready. Here's how to get the most out of it."
    - Body: Access credentials or activation link; 3 recommended first steps for the IT evaluator's environment; link to technical documentation and setup guide; dedicated POC support contact (name, email, Slack/Teams channel if available); expected deployment time for standard environment

    Email 2 — Technical configuration check-in (send: 48 hours post-activation):
    - Subject: "48 hours in: how's your [Product] setup going?"
    - Body: Quick configuration health check (3 items to verify — link to documentation); link to integration guide for their stated tech stack (if captured at sign-up); offer of solutions architect session for environment-specific configuration questions

    Email 3 — Use-case depth guide (send: Day 5–7 of POC):
    - Subject: "Getting to [specific outcome] with [Product]: the setup most IT teams use."
    - Body: Step-by-step guidance for the use case they indicated at sign-up; a real customer configuration example for a similar environment (anonymized); 2 advanced configuration options worth exploring once the baseline is working

    Email 4 — POC wrap-up and next steps (send: Day 12–14 of POC or 2 days before trial expiry):
    - Subject: "Your POC wraps up in [N] days. Here's how to document your findings."
    - Body: POC evaluation checklist (criteria to assess: performance, integration success, security validation, admin effort); link to ROI calculator pre-populated with POC data inputs; CTA: "Schedule your POC review call" with direct calendar link for solutions architect

    ---

    ### 2. Proof-Point Nurture Series

    Write a 3-email nurture sequence for a [software/infrastructure vendor] targeting IT prospects in the consideration stage who are evaluating options but have not yet requested a demo or POC.

    Email 1 — Security and compliance credentialing:
    - Subject: "How [Company] stays [SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / FedRAMP] certified — and what that means for your team."
    - Body: 3-paragraph structure: (1) Why [Your Product] invested in [certification] — the customer demand and compliance requirement driving it; (2) What [certification] requires in practice — 2–3 specific controls that protect the buyer's data; (3) What it means for their IT and compliance teams — audit documentation, incident logs, and BAA/DPA availability if applicable; CTA: "Download our security documentation"

    Email 2 — ROI/TCO proof point:
    - Subject: "What IT teams actually spend on [product category] — and where they reduce it."
    - Body: Lead with the TCO framework (4 components: licensing, operational, risk, opportunity cost); share one anonymized customer example with specific numbers; link to TCO worksheet or ROI calculator; CTA: "Calculate your ROI in 5 minutes"

    Email 3 — Customer case study:
    - Subject: "How [anonymous: 'a 700-person financial services firm'] moved from [incumbent] to [Your Product] in [timeframe]."
    - Body: 300-word case study — environment type, challenge, solution approach (technical specifics), migration timeline, measurable outcomes (uptime improvement %, cost reduction, compliance certification maintained); CTA: "Read the full case study" or "Request a call with a customer reference"

    ---

    ### 3. Renewal and Contract Expansion Sequence

    Write a 3-email renewal and expansion sequence for a [software/infrastructure vendor], targeting IT customers 90–60–30 days before contract renewal.

    Email 1 — Renewal notice + value retrospective (send: 90 days before renewal):
    - Subject: "Your [Product] contract renews in 90 days. Here's your year in review."
    - Body: Usage summary (if telemetry available: API calls, users active, incidents prevented, deployments completed); specific outcomes attributable to [Your Product] during the contract period; preview of upcoming roadmap features relevant to their use case; renewal CTA: "Review your renewal options"

    Email 2 — Expansion and new capability showcase (send: 60 days before renewal):
    - Subject: "New in [Product]: [3 features/capabilities] your team can use now."
    - Body: 3 new capabilities added since their last renewal — framed as answers to IT problems they were already solving; capacity or seat expansion offer (if relevant); CTA: "Talk to your account team about [specific capability they haven't activated]"

    Email 3 — Renewal deadline + expansion offer (send: 30 days before renewal):
    - Subject: "[Contract renewal] — [N] days to lock in your current rate."
    - Body: Renewal summary (current tier, pricing, included SLA); expansion offer with deadline (upgrade to next tier at current-tier pricing before renewal date); friction reduction: "Your account manager can handle the paperwork in one call — [calendar link]"

    ### 4. Executive ROI Summary Email

    Write a quarterly executive-level ROI summary email for a [software/infrastructure vendor], targeting the IT director, CTO, or CFO who sponsors the IT budget for [Your Product].

    Email structure:
