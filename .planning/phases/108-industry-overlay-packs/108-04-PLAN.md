    # Marketing Services — Paid Media Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** Paid media for marketing agencies sells performance to a buyer who understands performance. The biggest trap is using generic "we drive results" language to an audience that can immediately identify weak claims. CMOs and VP Marketing buyers evaluate agency paid media the way a chef evaluates a restaurant: they know exactly what they're looking at. Lead with proof density, channel-specific fluency, and a differentiated offer that reduces trial risk.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. LinkedIn Campaign Targeting CMO, VP Marketing, and Growth Leaders

    Write a LinkedIn paid campaign brief and ad creative for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Director, and Head of Growth roles at [target company size / industry].

    Campaign structure:
    - **Audience targeting:** Job title list (CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Director, Head of Growth, VP Demand Generation) + company size (50–5,000 employees depending on ICP) + industry filter (if vertical-specialist agency); optionally company list upload for named-account ABM
    - **Campaign objective:** Lead gen form (for audit/consultation offer) or website conversions (for case study or benchmark report downloads)
    - **Ad format:** Single image ad or document ad (for distributing a benchmark report)
    - **Ad creative (Single Image + CTA):**
      - Image direction: Performance dashboard screenshot or chart showing impressive result — avoid stock photos; this audience spots them immediately
      - Headline (150 chars): Result-first framing ("How [anonymized client type] achieved [specific metric] in [timeframe] — no creative rebrand required")
      - Intro text (600 chars): Lead with the client's performance problem in CMO-native language (CPL, CAC, ROAS, pipeline coverage); state specific outcome from a real engagement; describe the offer (free paid media audit, benchmark analysis, or strategy session); include why response is warranted now (benchmark data, seasonal opportunity, or regulatory shift relevant to their industry)
    - **Lead gen form fields:** Work email, company name, company size (dropdown), primary performance challenge (dropdown: paid media efficiency, lead quality, attribution, scaling, agency transition), monthly paid media budget range
    - **Follow-up:** Form submission → immediate delivery of audit framework or benchmark report + SDR outreach within 4 hours with personalized message referencing their stated challenge

    ---

    ### 2. Case Study-Led Google Search Campaign

    Write a Google Search ad campaign for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting CMO-level intent keywords around agency selection, performance marketing audits, and channel-specific growth.

    Campaign parameters:
    - **High-intent keyword examples:** "performance marketing agency," "paid media agency [city/region]," "marketing agency for [industry]," "hire marketing agency," "marketing audit," "Google Ads agency," "LinkedIn ads agency," "agency results case study"
    - **Ad structure (3 RSA headlines):**
      - Headline 1: Agency specialty + result lead ("Performance Marketing Agency | [N]% avg ROAS lift")
      - Headline 2: ICP or proof signal ("B2B SaaS · [N]+ Clients · Case Studies Available")
      - Headline 3: Low-friction offer ("Free Paid Media Audit" / "See Our Results" / "Talk to a Strategist")
    - **Descriptions (2, 90 chars max each):**
      - Description 1: Specific outcome + methodology hint ("We rebuilt [client type]'s paid media from $[X] CPA to $[Y] CPA in [timeframe]. See how.")
      - Description 2: ICP signal + offer friction reduction ("No retainer required for audit. No lock-in contract in year one. [CTA]")
    - **Extensions:** Sitelinks to case studies (by industry vertical), services overview, pricing/engagement model, and free audit offer; callout extensions for named certifications (Google Premier Partner, Meta Business Partner, HubSpot Diamond)

    Tone: Confident and specific. Every claim backed by a case study link or methodology reference.

    Ad strategy:
    - **Why audits work for agency acquisition:** They demonstrate competence before commitment; they are high-perceived-value for the prospect; they naturally surface the problems the agency solves; they create a transition from marketing to sales with a warm, specific conversation
    - **Audience:** CMO, VP Marketing, in-house marketing team leads with budget responsibility; retarget website visitors who browsed the agency's case studies or services pages
    - **Ad creative (Meta Primary Image Ad):**

    # Marketing Services — Content & SEO Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** Content SEO for marketing agencies targets buyers who are actively evaluating agency partners or trying to solve a marketing performance problem they can't solve in-house. The highest-performing content demonstrates competence — benchmark reports, methodology guides, and case studies that show how the agency thinks, not just what it does. The buyer is a marketing expert; surface-level content immediately signals low-value agency positioning.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. Agency Capability / Services Overview Page

    Write an SEO-optimized services overview page for a [marketing agency / performance firm] targeting the keyword "[agency type] services" or "best [marketing channel] agency."

    Page structure:
    - **H1:** "[Agency Name] — [Specialty] Marketing Agency: [Outcome-Led Positioning]" — e.g., "Performance Marketing Agency for B2B SaaS: Paid Media, SEO, and CRO That Drives Pipeline"
    - **Intro (100 words):** Lead with the performance problem the agency solves (in CMO language: "Most B2B SaaS companies are generating leads — but less than [X]% convert to pipeline. The problem is rarely the channel; it's the targeting, creative, and conversion path."); state the agency's approach in 2 sentences; establish credibility (client count, years, vertical focus)
    - **Service lines (3–5 sections, 80 words each):** For each service: problem it solves, the agency's methodology angle, measurable outcome to expect, and a client metric example
    - **Proof section:** Aggregate performance stats (average ROAS improvement %, average CPL reduction %, average pipeline contribution lift for clients in [niche]) + 3 mini client logos or anonymized case metrics
    - **ICP clarity section:** "We work best with [company profile]" — specific: revenue stage, team size, channel mix, growth stage; include "We're probably not the right fit if..." — builds credibility through honesty
    - **Primary CTA:** "Get a free audit" or "Talk to a strategist" — not "Contact us"

    ---

    ### 2. Industry Benchmark Report or State-of-the-Market Content

    Write a benchmark report outline and executive summary for a [marketing agency] producing original data or curated analysis on [specific channel or marketing discipline] performance benchmarks.

    Report structure:
    - **Title:** "The [Year] [Channel/Discipline] Benchmark Report: [What the Data Reveals About Top-Performing [Company/Campaign Type]]"
    - **Executive summary (300 words for the SEO-landing version):** Key finding lead (the single most surprising or actionable data point); 4–5 punchy finding bullets; who collected this data and why you can trust it; what the reader should do with it
    - **Report sections (outline for full report):** (1) Benchmark methodology — data sources, sample size, date range, definitions; (2) Industry-wide performance benchmarks — key metrics by company size, vertical, and spend range; (3) Top-quartile vs. median performance — what separates top performers; (4) Channel-specific deep dive; (5) Forecast or trend for next 12 months; (6) Recommendations
    - **SEO landing page for report download (CTA gate):**
      - H1: Report title
      - Lead paragraph: The most compelling finding — specific number that makes the reader want the full report
      - 3 bullet must-read findings
      - Form: First name + work email + company size + primary channel focus (dropdown)
      - Trust signal: "Used by [N] marketing leaders. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."

    ---

    ### 3. Client Results Case Study in SEO Format

    Write a case study in SEO content format for a [marketing agency], targeting keywords like "marketing agency case study [industry]" or "[agency type] results [company type]."

    Case study SEO format:
    - **H1:** "How [Anonymized: 'a 150-person B2B SaaS company'] Reduced CAC by [X]% with [Agency Service] in [Timeframe]"
    - **Client context (100 words):** Company profile (industry, size, revenue stage, team structure) — enough for readers to self-identify as similar; the specific marketing performance challenge they faced before engaging the agency
    - **Why they chose [Agency] (80 words):** What differentiated the agency in the selection process — be specific (methodology, vertical expertise, contract terms); don't just say "they liked our approach"
    - **What we did (200 words):** Specific actions taken, in order of implementation — include the reasoning behind key decisions (why this channel, why this audience, why this creative direction); show that this is a strategy, not a template
    - **Results (100 words):** Primary KPI improvement with exact figures; secondary metrics; timeline to first result; comparison to pre-engagement benchmark
    - **What [Client] says:** Direct quote from CMO or marketing lead — specific to the outcome, not generic praise
    - **Related resources:** Link to relevant service page; link to related case study in same vertical; CTA to request a free audit

    ---

    ### 4. "Why Outsource [Marketing Channel]?" Decision-Support Guide

    Write an SEO decision-support article for a [marketing agency] targeting the keyword "when to outsource [paid media / SEO / email marketing / social media]" or "in-house vs. agency [channel]."

    Article structure:
    - **H1:** "When to Outsource Your [Channel]: A Practical Framework for Marketing Leaders"
    - **Intro (100 words):** Frame this as a genuine decision, not a sales pitch — acknowledge that in-house can be the right answer; your goal is to help the reader make the right decision for their situation
    - **The decision framework (3 factors, 100 words each):**
      - Factor 1: Current performance gap — what does your [channel] currently cost per outcome vs. industry benchmark? If you're 2x the benchmark, you need a diagnosis before deciding build vs. buy.
      - Factor 2: Internal capability and bandwidth — do you have the specialist expertise, tooling access, and time needed to close the performance gap in-house within [timeframe]?
      - Factor 3: Stage and speed — at what revenue stage does in-house become more cost-effective than agency? (Include a rough breakeven model.)
    - **When agency beats in-house (specific scenarios):** 3 concrete situations where agency is the dominant choice; include the financial case for each
    - **When in-house beats agency (specific scenarios):** 2 concrete situations — do not omit this; it builds trust and self-selects toward better-fit agency clients
    - **How to evaluate an agency once you've decided (100 words):** 5 questions to ask; what good answers look like
    - **CTA:** Link to free audit offer; link to agency services comparison page

    # Marketing Services — Lifecycle & Email Industry Overlay Prompts

    > **Context:** Marketing services email lifecycle is sales-cycle management for a high-consideration, high-trust purchase. CMO-level buyers receive many agency outreach emails; the ones that convert are segment-specific, proof-dense, and low-pressure. The sequence moves the prospect through: initial credibility (audit/asset delivery), proof accumulation (case study and methodology drip), and commitment removal (proposal and objection handling). Promotional or generic email performs poorly with marketing-expert buyers.

    ## Prompts

    ### 1. Inbound Lead Follow-Up and RFP Invitation Sequence

    Write a 3-email sequence for a [marketing agency] following up on an inbound inquiry (audit request, contact form, or content download).

    Email 1 — Delivery + first impression (send: immediately):
    - Subject: "Your [audit / benchmark report / content] from [Agency Name]"
    - Body: Deliver the promised asset or audit findings immediately; 2-sentence context on what's in it and how to use the findings; single next step: "If any of these findings match what you're experiencing, I'd be glad to walk through them — here's a 20-minute slot: [calendar link]"; no pitch

    Email 2 — Relevant case study (send: 48 hours after Email 1, if no meeting booked):
    - Subject: "How [anonymized: 'a company like yours'] solved [specific problem from their audit findings or content interest]"
    - Body: 200-word case study matched to their stated challenge or the context of what they downloaded; specific metrics; single CTA: "Would this approach be a fit for [their company]? [Calendar link]"

    Email 3 — RFP invitation or agency presentation offer (send: Day 5–7 if no meeting):
    - Subject: "Would a brief on [their channel challenge] make sense?"
    - Body: 3-line setup — acknowledge they're likely evaluating options; offer to send a capabilities brief matched to their specific situation (not a generic deck); make the ask explicit and low-commitment: "If you'd share the current challenge you're solving for, I'll send a relevant case study or brief. No pitch call required."

    ---

    ### 2. Proposal Follow-Up and Differentiation Sequence

    Write a 3-email post-proposal sequence for a [marketing agency], supporting a CMO or marketing director who received a proposal and is now comparing options.

    Email 1 — Proposal delivery confirmation + differentiation anchor (send: immediately after proposal):
    - Subject: "Your [Agency Name] proposal — plus one thing most proposals don't show"
    - Body: Confirm delivery; point to the one section of the proposal that differentiates the agency from typical pitches (methodology appendix, attribution model, or performance guarantee terms); offer a 30-minute "build vs. buy" conversation that is explicitly not a sales call — it's to help them make the right decision for their situation

    Email 2 — Reference or additional proof (send: 3–5 days after proposal):
    - Subject: "A case study you might not have seen — relevant to [their company type or challenge]"
    - Body: Share a case study that directly parallels their situation (not generic proof — specific to what they're solving for); offer a customer reference call if they want to hear from a peer; no pressure, no deadline

    Email 3 — Objection removal (send: Day 8–10 if no response):
    - Subject: "The two questions we hear most at this stage"
    - Body: Address the 2 most common CMO objections in writing — e.g., (1) "What happens if results take longer than projected?" → describe the performance review process and contract protection; (2) "What's the onboarding time before we see results?" → specific timeline from kickoff to first campaign live; close: "Happy to address anything else — no call needed if you prefer email."

    ---

    ### 3. Client Onboarding Sequence

    Write a 3-email client onboarding sequence for a [marketing agency], starting when a contract is signed.

    Email 1 — Contract signed + kickoff invitation (send: same day as contract execution):
    - Subject: "Welcome to [Agency Name] — your kickoff is being scheduled"
    - Body: Congratulate warmly but briefly; kickoff meeting invitation (date, attendees, agenda); what to prepare before kickoff (access grants, shared assets, account logins, team introductions); what will be accomplished in the first 30 days

    Email 2 — Pre-kickoff preparation guide (send: 48 hours before kickoff):
    - Subject: "Kickoff prep: what to have ready for [date]"
