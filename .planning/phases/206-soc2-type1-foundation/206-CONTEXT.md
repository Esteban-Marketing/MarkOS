# Phase 206 Context - SOC 2 Type I Foundation for MarkOS v2

**Status:** Updated after 2026-04-22 v2 vault/codebase compliance audit, SaaS Suite intake, and SaaS Marketing OS Strategy discussion review.

## Why this phase changed

The original Phase 206 discussion described generic SOC2 readiness. The v2 blueprint and Pricing Engine intake introduce specific control surfaces that SOC2 planning must cover:

- AgentRun lifecycle, approvals, side effects, retries, and failure evidence.
- Pricing Engine recommendations, price-test approvals, competitor-pricing source governance, and public pricing page controls.
- SaaS Suite subscription lifecycle, legal billing, support response approval, product usage data, revenue intelligence, and processor/accounting/provider evidence.
- SaaS Marketing OS growth motions: in-app campaigns, PLG upgrade triggers, ABM/customer marketing outreach, referral rewards, affiliate commissions, community programs, event sequences, PR/analyst/review outreach, partnerships, developer marketing, experiments, and revenue-team handoffs.
- Connector credentials, scopes, retention, failure recovery, and dependent-agent pause behavior.
- EvidenceMap, source quality score, claim TTL, and research freshness.
- Artifact performance logs, tenant overlays, anonymized learning, and central literacy promotion.
- Tenant 0 proof and public case-study evidence.

## Existing substrate

- Tenant model, RLS, audit log, webhooks, MCP sessions, passkeys, billing evidence, and CRM approval patterns already exist.
- SOC2 work should extend these patterns and avoid inventing parallel evidence systems.

## Required phase shape

1. Data/control inventory for v2 operating-loop objects.
2. AI governance and approval-control policy.
3. Pricing Engine control mapping.
4. SaaS Suite billing/support/product/revenue control mapping.
5. SaaS Marketing OS growth-motion control mapping for future post-217 modules.
6. Connector privacy and vendor/subprocessor review.
7. Evidence automation from audit logs, AgentRun events, approval gates, billing ledgers, webhook evidence, DIAN/accounting sync evidence, support approvals, product usage processing records, pricing decisions, and future growth mutations.
8. Incident response, BCP/DR, and pen-test readiness.
9. Auditor workspace and Type I readiness package.

## Done means

SOC2 Phase 206 can prove that the new agentic, pricing, SaaS Suite, and future SaaS growth surfaces are covered by controls before the public SaaS posture depends on them.
