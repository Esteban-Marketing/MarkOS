## Executive Read

The commercial-engine lane is no longer just reserved. Phases `221-228` now have:

- `DISCUSS.md`
- `CONTEXT.md`
- `RESEARCH.md`

The main conclusion from this pass is encouraging: MarkOS already has enough substrate to build the commercial stack additively, but not enough yet to claim any of the new engines as implemented. The repo already contains:

- identity stitching and tracking ingest
- CRM entity, timeline, merge, workspace, and reporting foundations
- outbound provider adapters, consent rules, sequences, conversations, and webhooks
- public signup/conversion surfaces and campaign/segment schemas
- plugin, pack, and marketplace-style runtime foundations

What it does **not** yet have is the unified system-of-record layer across those pieces. That is exactly what the new phase family is for.

## Cross-Phase Findings

1. **CDP should be additive, not replacement-heavy**
   - Current tracking and identity stitching are useful seeds.
   - The right move is to elevate them into first-party identity, consent, event, and audience objects rather than redoing them from zero.

2. **CRM is real, but still pre-360**
   - The codebase already has CRM entity, timeline, merge, workspace, execution, and reporting substrate.
   - It does not yet have the full customer-360, lifecycle, buying-committee, and commercial-memory model from doc 18.

3. **Email/messaging already has an adapter seam**
   - Resend and Twilio adapters, consent rules, sequence scheduling, conversation threads, and webhook normalization already exist.
   - That work should become the foundation of the native owned-channel engine rather than a parallel stack.

4. **Analytics exists as CRM reporting, not yet as a semantic layer**
   - Attribution, readiness, executive summary, and central rollup logic already exist.
   - What is missing is the cross-domain metric catalog, attribution semantics, freshness/provenance model, and narrative intelligence layer.

5. **Ecosystem has runtime primitives, not business workflows**
   - Plugin registry, loader, pack selection, MCP readiness, and integration pages are already useful building blocks.
   - Partner, affiliate, referral, certification, community, and developer-growth workflows still need explicit business objects.

6. **Phase 228 is justified**
   - The codebase already shows several good replaceable-adapter patterns.
   - A final integration phase is still necessary to stop the commercial stack from drifting into new silos or hidden lock-in.
