### Finding 3 - Phase 205 must absorb doc 17 pricing signals

Doc 17 introduces new pricing-sensitive surfaces: PLG upgrade prompts, feature gates, usage-limit prompts, annual-plan nudges, save offers, referral rewards, affiliate commissions, pricing-page experiments, G2/Capterra pricing profile sync, and sales/CS pricing copy.

Phase 205 should not implement all those growth modules, but it must model enough Pricing Engine primitives so those future surfaces cannot bypass approved PricingRecommendation records.

### Finding 4 - Phase 206 controls must include growth motions

SOC2 control design should cover not only current SaaS Suite billing/support/product data, but also future doc 17 external mutations: in-app campaigns, referral/affiliate payouts, partner actions, PR and analyst outreach, event sequences, ABM/customer marketing outreach, review requests, experiments, and developer community communications.

### Finding 5 - Phases 214-217 should stay foundation-scoped

The SaaS Suite phases should not balloon into the whole SaaS Marketing OS. Their job is to supply subscription, billing, health, support, usage, and revenue facts. Doc 17 then turns those facts into growth motions in later work.

The key compatibility requirement is to leave extension points:

- SaaS growth mode routing (`b2b`, `b2c`, `plg_b2b`, `plg_b2c`, `b2b2c`).
- Product usage events useful for activation, PQL, upgrade triggers, community health, and expansion.
- Revenue metrics usable by ABM, expansion, revenue alignment, experiments, and executive reporting.
- Approval and pricing hooks for every customer-facing growth action.
