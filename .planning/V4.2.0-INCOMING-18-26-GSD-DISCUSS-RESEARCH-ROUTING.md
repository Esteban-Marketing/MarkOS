Incoming documents `18` through `26` do not describe one more feature cluster. They describe the next commercial layer MarkOS needs if it wants to become a category-leading, AI-agentic Marketing Operating System:

- first-party identity and audience truth
- native CRM and commercial memory
- native owned-channel execution
- native conversion and launch orchestration
- explainable analytics and attribution
- sales enablement tied to evidence and pricing
- ecosystem distribution as an operating system, not a side program

These documents are too large and too cross-cutting to fold into the existing v4.1.0 SaaS Suite + Growth Strategy bundle. The correct routing is:

1. Keep the current execution order intact through Phases 204-220.
2. Reserve a new milestone candidate, `v4.2.0 Commercial Engines 1.0`.
3. Seed Phases `221-228` now with discussion and context scaffolding.
4. Use those seeded files as mandatory inputs for the next GSD discuss and research passes.

This pass intentionally does **not** pretend the research is already done. It prepares the terrain so the next pass can think clearly and go deep.

## Quality Standards For The New Lane

These are non-negotiable and should be carried into every later discuss, research, and plan artifact:

1. One identity truth. No duplicate profile, consent, or audience systems.
2. One commercial memory. No separate CRM notes, deal notes, campaign notes, and support notes that cannot be reconciled.
3. One governed mutation path. Email, messaging, launch, partner, referral, pricing, and sales outputs all pass through task, approval, evidence, audit, and AgentRun controls.
4. No passive systems. CDP, CRM, analytics, ecosystem, and sales enablement must create tasks, alerts, or decisions.
5. No pricing bypasses. Every pricing-sensitive surface uses Pricing Engine or the pending placeholder.
6. No unsupported external claim. Proof, case study, ROI, competitor, and launch language must be evidence-linked and freshness-aware.
7. No provider lock as architecture. Every major channel or platform dependency needs adapter contracts, export posture, and migration thinking.
8. No designed obsolescence. The stack needs versioned schemas, import/export posture, archival, and recoverability.
9. UI, API, and MCP parity. New engines are incomplete if they exist in only one surface.
10. Testability is part of design. Business logic must be unit/integration testable in `Vitest`, workflow truth must be provable in `Playwright`, and UI surfaces with component states must be eligible for `Chromatic`.
